# Magnetically confined plasma (tokamak MHD stability)
State space: Plasma pressure p, magnetic field B, fluid velocity v
Dynamics operator: Ideal and resistive Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) equations
Perturbation operator: Kink modes, tearing modes, ballooning instabilities, neoclassical tearing
Stability condition: Safety factor q > 1; energy principle δW > 0 for all displacements
Failure mode: Major disruption, thermal quench, magnetic reconnection, loss of confinement
Observables: Plasma current, Magnetic safety factor q, Beta (ratio of plasma pressure to magnetic pressure)
Timescale regime: Microseconds (Alfvén time) to seconds (resistive diffusion time)
Persistence type: STATE
Non-geometric elements: Magnetic helicity, Topological reconnection of magnetic field lines
Edge conditions: Density limit (Greenwald limit), Resonant magnetic perturbations
Notes: Confinement requires continuous external magnetic flux maintenance against intrinsic fluid instabilities.
