# Constitutional legal systems under judicial review
State space: Corpus of binding legal doctrine and constitutional precedent
Dynamics operator: Legislative enactment, executive action, judicial interpretation
Perturbation operator: Unconstitutional legislation, novel jurisprudential challenges, societal value shifts
Stability condition: Judicial invalidation of unconstitutional acts maintains doctrinal coherence
Failure mode: Constitutional crisis, judicial capture, or systemic abrogation of binding precedent
Observables: Overturned laws ratio, Supreme Court decision alignments, Amending frequency
Timescale regime: Years to decades (judicial terms and legal generations)
Persistence type: STATE
Non-geometric elements: Normative interpretation, Institutional legitimacy, Semantic ambiguity
Edge conditions: Court packing, Executive non-compliance
Notes: Stability requires continuous interpretative maintenance against legislative drift.
