# Helix Axioms

## What Helix Claims
1. The macroscopic failure geometry of any bounded system is deterministic and low-rank, dictated almost entirely by its **Substrate** (continuous vs discrete vs stochastic) and its **Persistence Ontology** (the topological goal it must maintain).
2. Human-engineered **Hybrid Systems** mapping continuous physics to discrete algorithmic spaces are mathematically cursed with structural decoupling, fracturing 600% more often than pure physics into discontinuous states. 
3. **Timescale Parity** governs locality: if stress perturbations outpace relaxation rates, repair operators mathematically cannot localize the geometric collapse. It becomes strictly global.
4. **Maintenance operators** on stochastic equilibriums inherently inject correlated noise, mathematically forcing the system toward an eventual distributional correlation collapse (`DISTRIBUTIONAL_COLLAPSE`).

## What Helix Does Not Claim
1. Helix does not claim the ability to predict the exact numeric time or location of failure (e.g. at exactly 25.4 Joules). Helix predicts *how* systems break, not exactly *when*. 
2. Helix is not an intuitive metaphor index. The similarities observed across biology and hardware are structural and topological boundaries, not poetic analogies. 
3. Helix does not propose a new mathematical substrate or fundamental force. It merely maps the structural limits of known operators crossing mismatched topologies.

## What Falsifies Helix
1. Finding a pure continuous domain that geometrically shatters into a discontinuous topological phase boundary (without possessing a discrete underlying topological invariant like a Chern number).
2. Finding an algorithmic constraint (discrete logic architecture) subjected to instantaneous perturbation (FAST_PERTURB) that fails via smooth, geometric stretching instead of instantaneous global combinatorial collapse.
3. Rotational disruption of the latent eigenspace under bootstrapping or isotopic rotation (meaning the predictive clusters are labeling artifacts, not objective geometrical manifolds).

## What Requires Schema Change
1. Encountering a failure topology that cannot be expressed as one of the 5 canonical structural collapses (Smooth, Singular, Combinatorial, Discontinuous, Distributional).
2. Demonstrating that boundary distance *can* be mathematically projected without domain-specific scalarization, which would necessitate upgrading the core Schema `measurement_layer`.
