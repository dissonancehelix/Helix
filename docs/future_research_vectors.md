# Helix: Future Research Vectors & Minimal Constraint Discovery

*A roadmap for empirical structural testing targeting the absolute minimal constraints required for existence and persistence.*

---

## The Core Objective
Helix is now a purely deterministic, invariant-first structural instrument capable of running adversarial tests on ontological hypotheses. The ultimate goal is to strip away all narrative assumptions to discover the absolute minimum topological requirements for a structure to maintain coherence and resist collapse.

The following represent formal hypothesis tracks that can be synthetically modeled, evaluated against strict statistical thresholds (like KERNEL-2E), and either elevated to structural laws or discarded.

## 1. Thermodynamic & Entropy Dissipation (KERNEL-3T)

**Hypothesis:** The ability to route and dump operational entropy outside a system’s boundary is an independent, non-derivative predictor of its failure geometry, independent of physical substrate.
**To Test:** 
- Create a `domains_entropy_pack` tracking how systems handle accumulation (e.g., heat sinks, memory garbage collection, economic inflation, biological excretion).
- Measure Information Gain (IG) between dissipation strategies and boundary collapse types.
- **Minimal Constraint Target:** If KERNEL-3T proves orthogonal, it suggests that *existence* strictly requires a directional algorithmic exhaust mechanism. A completely closed internal loop is mathematically barred from persisting.

## 2. Boundary Permeability & Filtration (KERNEL-BP)

**Hypothesis:** The degree to which a system's boundary is selectively permeable strictly dictates the localization of its failure. 
**To Test:**
- Define closed structural vocabulary around filtering (e.g., `ABSOLUTE_ISOLATION`, `SELECTIVE_OSMOSIS`, `FORCED_INGESTION`, `NO_FILTER`).
- Test if permeability perfectly isolates or exacerbates cascading network failures across domains (using null permutations over Locality flags).
- **Minimal Constraint Target:** Determine if a completely impermeable boundary is fundamentally less persistent than a selectively permeable one. What is the minimum rate of external information exchange required to avoid internal deadlocking?

## 3. Depth of Self-Reference / Internal Modeling (KERNEL-SR)

**Hypothesis:** A system's capacity to maintain a self-model (or predictive loop about its own geometry) shifts its failure from `COMBINATORIAL_THRESHOLD` into `DISTRIBUTIONAL_EQUILIBRIUM`.
**To Test:**
- Construct an `internal_reference_pack` across varying domains (predictive brains, self-monitoring servers, institutional bureaucracies).
- Ablate the "self-reference" loop from synthetic domains and measure the exact shift in collapse survival time.
- **Minimal Constraint Target:** Can a system maintain long-term persistence under high noise *without* simulating itself? Or is recursive self-reference a fundamental requirement for advanced error-correction?

## 4. Asymmetry & Directionality (KERNEL-AD)

**Hypothesis:** Structure only stabilizes due to forced asymmetry in how rules are applied (e.g., time's arrow, gravity, unidirectional network protocols). Symmetrical rule sets are inherently unstable under external perturbation.
**To Test:**
- Generate paired domains: one explicitly symmetrical in its dynamics, one functionally asymmetrical.
- Subject them to identical adversarial perturbation operators and measure the boundary disruption.
- **Minimal Constraint Target:** Prove that symmetry-breaking is the foundational generative driver of all structure constraint, meaning perfectly symmetrical objects mathematically cannot persist as distinct entities.

## 5. The "Minimum Viable Holobiont" (Ablation Sandbox)

**Hypothesis:** Complex structures are inherently composites. There is a mathematical minimum number of interlocking subsystems required to achieve stable persistence.
**To Test:**
- Use the Identity/TSM architectures to create highly detailed modular entities.
- Systematically ablate components (remove the memory trace, remove the sensory input, remove the homeostatic feedback, remove the structural buffer).
- Run combinatorial survival tests to find the "dead zone" where the geometry instantly collapses.
- **Minimal Constraint Target:** Determine the exact minimal configuration of structural rules (the atoms of persistence) that must overlap to create a self-sustaining entity in a noisy universe.
