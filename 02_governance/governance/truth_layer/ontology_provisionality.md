# Ontology Provisionality Contract

## 1. Scope
This contract governs all bases within the Helix instrument. No ontological component is immune to revision, splitting, merging, or total replacement.

## 2. Fundamental Rules
- **Provisional Nature**: No base (Ring 0 or Ring 1) is permanent.
- **Inertia Exclusion**: Existing ontology is never protected out of historical inertia or aesthetic preference.
- **Evolutionary Mandate**: Any base MUST be split, merged, or replaced if it is proven to increase discovery resolution.

## 3. Promotion Requirements
The promotion of any candidate base requires documented survival of the following:
- **Measurable Resolution Gain**: Improvement in the `Resolution_Gain` metric.
- **Invariance Survival**: Stability across scaling, normalization, and projection families.
- **Leakage Rejection**: Verification of non-derivative status (R^2 < threshold relative to existing bases).
- **Ablation Elasticity Preservation**: Verification that the base provides irreducible information (ablation causes measurable signal collapse).

## 4. Confidence vs. Immunity
Temporal stability (success across multiple cycles) increases the probability of a base's accuracy but never grants immunity from future falsification.
