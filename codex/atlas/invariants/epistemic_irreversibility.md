# Invariant: Epistemic Irreversibility

**Type:** Invariant
**Status:** Exploratory
**Origin:** Helix Atlas — Phase 8 upgrade
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-15

---

## Domain Coverage

- Language (sequential symbolic systems)
- Cognition (decision-making under uncertainty)

---

## Mechanism

Some epistemic transitions — shifts in the information state of a system — are structurally irreversible. Once a belief or interpretive commitment is made, the prior information structure cannot be recovered from the posterior alone. The transition destroys information rather than merely rearranging it.

---

## Predictions

1. Information-theoretic measure of irreversibility > threshold at genuine epistemic commits
2. Systems under time pressure show sharper irreversibility transitions
3. Reversible-appearing decisions will show low irreversibility signal

---

## Falsifiers

1. Any epistemic commit that shows full posterior-to-prior reconstruction within tolerance
2. Irreversibility signal does not differ between reversible and irreversible decisions
3. Probe shows signal < 0.20 in systems with confirmed irreversible commits
