# HELIX LAB (RESEARCH WORKSPACE LAYER)

## Definition
Helix Lab is the structured research workspace operating strictly inside the Helix substrate.
- It is NOT the kernel.
- It is NOT ontology.
- It is NOT Core.
- It is a **Forge-level execution chamber** governed entirely by kernel constraints.

## Purpose
Helix Lab executes:
- Runtime Propagation Studies (SRD)
- Fragility Forecasting Research
- Cross-domain propagation experiments
- Structural atlas refinement
- Mutation-based falsification suites

## Architectural Rule
Helix Lab may generate empirical models, metrics, and experimental geometry.

It **may not** mutate:
- `00_core/` (or `01_core/` if reinstated)
- Ontology enums
- Structural basis registry
- Protocol enforcement layers

## Economic Alignment
Helix Lab research feeds projection tools such as:
- Runtime Resilience Scanner (RRS)
- ML Fragility Toolkit
- Structural risk diagnostics

Tooling outputs are projections of Lab findings. Lab findings are not defined by tooling success; they are structural truths measured empirically.

## Long-Term Positioning
Helix Lab represents the investigative branch of Helix.
Future GUI / OS shell layers will visualize both:
1. Operational tools
2. Lab research outputs

## Dependency Law
If substrate metrics are falsified, Helix Lab abstractions collapse automatically.
**No branch survives root falsification.**
