## The framework, current state

**The threshold framework.** Consciousness is not a single property of physical systems. It emerges through layered structural achievements, each enclosing the previous, each a stronger version of the same operation: *the system contains a representation of itself.*

The current staging (revised after adversarial review):

- **T1 — Self-replication (~3.8 Bya).** Chemistry contains an encoding of itself. RNA-world molecules. Not consciousness, but the structural precondition.

- **T2 — Environment and reciprocal modeling (~500 Mya through ~50 Mya).** Nervous systems represent surroundings, including other agents. The earlier framing had this split as T2 (environment) and T3 (other-mind), but predator-prey co-evolution means they did not separate sequentially; reciprocal modeling is a structural specialization within environment modeling. Theory of mind in mammals/corvids/cephalopods is the high-resolution end. Where IIT-style integration begins to apply. Proto-experience.

- **T2.5 — Temporal decoupling and offline simulation.** A distinct structural achievement: episodic memory, mental time travel (Tulving's chronesthesia), counterfactual reasoning. Demonstrated in great apes, corvids, possibly cephalopods. The capacity to hold modeling operations as objects across time. Without temporal decoupling, T4 cannot stabilize.

- **T4 — Recursive self-modeling.** The system models itself modeling. Self-reference at the cognitive level. This is where strong-form consciousness plausibly emerges. Mirror-test pass (Gallup) is one operationalization; richer diagnostics include counterfactual self-prediction, self-revision under contradictory evidence, persistent self-coherence under adversarial probing. Higher-order thought (Rosenthal, Lau). Damasio's core self. Hofstadter's strange loop. Metzinger's phenomenal self-model.

- **Modes of T4 over distributed substrate.** Earlier formulations included T5 (externalized self-modeling) and T6 (recursive externalized self-modeling). Adversarial review identified this as a category error: extending the substrate over which T4 operates is not a new consciousness threshold. Externalized media (writing, archives, machine-readable specs) enlarge the surface of T4 without deepening phenomenal experience.

**Necessary preconditions beyond recursive structure.** Pure recursive self-modeling alone is too permissive — it would grant consciousness to cryptographic hash functions on their own outputs, chaotic attractors, self-referential proof systems. To prevent collapse into pancomputationalism, three additional preconditions are required:

1. *Markov blankets and free-energy boundary maintenance* (Friston). The system must actively maintain a thermodynamic boundary differentiating self from world. Living systems persist by minimizing free energy at the Markov blanket. Cryptographic hashes don't.

2. *Hysteresis and temporal thickness.* Phenomenal experience is path-dependent; T4 in real systems is a trajectory through self-models, not a static fixed point.

3. *Adjoint structure (speculative).* Consciousness may be a dynamic adjunction — right adjoint preserving limits (compressing input into models) paired with left adjoint preserving colimits (expanding models into action) — rather than a static fixed point.

**Toward proof — Recursive Self-Modeling Theorem (sketch, with revisions).**

Setup: Let S be a system with state space Σ. Let M: Σ → Σ^Σ be a self-modeling operation. Threshold 4 requires M to be weakly point-surjective in Lawvere's sense.

By Lawvere's fixed-point theorem (1969), there exists σ* such that M(σ*)(σ*) = σ* — a stable self-state representing itself representing. This is the same diagonal structure underneath Gödel incompleteness, Russell's paradox, the halting problem, Tarski, and Cantor (cf. Yanofsky 2003 for the unified treatment).

Three problems with the original sketch, surfaced by adversarial review and now acknowledged in the framework:

- *Cardinality.* Cantor's theorem says |Σ^Σ| > |Σ| for non-trivial Σ, so weak point-surjectivity is impossible in pure set theory for any finite physical system. To make the math work, Σ must be defined as compressed macrostates (Scott domains, computable functions). The fixed point becomes an artifact of compression rather than an ontological discovery.

- *Prior literature.* Yanofsky 2003 already mapped Lawvere to Cantor/Gödel/Turing. The contribution is the application to phenomenal consciousness via the threshold framework, not the unification.

- *Claim 3 downgrade.* The original Claim 3 identified σ*'s unanalyzability-from-outside with phenomenal experience. This conflates an epistemic property with an ontological property. Under that identification, cryptographic hashes and chaotic attractors with diagonal structure would be granted phenomenal experience. The honest revision: σ* (under the additional preconditions) is *necessary* for phenomenal experience but not *sufficient*. The hard problem is localized to a structural condition without being dissolved.

**Question-sharpening as method.** The framework was produced by a general operation: take a diffuse question, identify the structural condition doing the explanatory work, restate the question in those terms. Carnap's *explication*; Cappelen and Plunkett's *conceptual engineering*. Historical examples: heat → kinetic energy → thermodynamics; life → self-replicating informational structure → molecular biology; gravity → spacetime curvature → general relativity; computation → Church-Turing → computability theory. In each case the breakthrough was in the reframe.

Sharpenings from this framework:
- "Why does any physical process feel like something?" → "Why does recursive self-modeling produce phenomenal experience under appropriate preconditions?"
- "Are animals conscious?" → "Which thresholds do they cross, and do their preconditions hold?"
- "Is AI conscious?" → "Does it exhibit Lawvere-fixed-point structure with persistence across contexts AND maintain Markov-blanket boundaries?"
- "What is the self?" → "What is the trajectory through fixed-point self-models under thermodynamic constraint?"
- "Is there a soul?" → "Does the trajectory have continuity-preserving properties across substrate changes?"
- "What is free will?" → "Under what conditions does a system's self-model become structurally causal rather than epiphenomenal?"

The framework's hypothesis: the *method itself* — sharpening diffuse questions until structural conditions are named — may be the actual unifying contribution.
