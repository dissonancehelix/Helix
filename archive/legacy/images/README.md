## Full-Pass Re-analysis

Coverage: 295 still images inspected in numbered batches after merging the archive. Two `.webm` files are preserved in the archive but are not modeled as still images here.

The recurring signal is not capture context or a single demographic type. It is channel convergence with low internal friction: face, body type, posture, styling, self-presentation, and softness/sharpness balance need to feel mutually authored.

The older dataset should be compared against the newer one rather than averaged blindly. Convergence across both sets is high-confidence attraction structure; mismatch is taste drift or old-context residue.

### Face Signal

The face pattern is strongest when softness and sharpness are both present:
- large, alert, almond/doe-like eyes
- dark eyes or high eye contrast; eyeliner/eyelash emphasis often acts as a sharp event
- soft cheeks, rounded or heart/oval face structure, and a smaller/softer chin
- full lips or a soft mouth line
- long dark hair, bangs, side parts, face-framing layers, or slightly messy hair as common amplifiers
- glasses, dark clothing, chokers, jewelry, colored hair streaks, or alternative styling as secondary sharp events

The preferred face does not read as blank model polish. It tends to feel expressive, slightly private, cute-sharp, self-possessed, or interior.

### Body Signal

The body pattern is not narrow thinness. It is soft volume arranged around clear proportional contrast:
- waist-to-hip contrast
- waist-to-bust contrast
- hips, thighs, and glute volume as a repeated strong signal
- bust volume as a repeated strong signal, but not the only signal
- soft stomach/softness is not disqualifying and often supports the pattern
- petite-curvy and fuller-curvy bodies both fit when the proportions stay coherent
- athletic tone can work when it supports curve and posture; hard muscular/angular presentation is not the center

Best body compression:

> Soft volume with a readable waist relationship.

### Drift And Weighting

The older set is more explicit, more gravure/porn-coded, and more exaggerated around bust/hip display. That is partly historical residue and should not overpower the newer set.

The newer set gives more current weight to whole-subject coherence: face, hair, body, posture, and styling feeling like one owned signal. The older set still matters where it repeats the same underlying morphology: soft face plus sharp eyes/styling, dark hair/framing, curves, hips/thighs, bust/waist contrast, and private/self-possessed presentation.

Do not encode "youth" as a target. If an image is age-ambiguous or only works by seeming too young, it should not be promoted. Preserve the adult morphology signal instead: soft/rounded features, large-eye contrast, and gentle face structure.

Observed pattern handles:
- `attraction_channel_convergence`: attraction strengthens when several channels agree without feeling staged into a single flat type.
- `signal_ownership`: the image works better when the person appears to own the signal rather than being overwhelmed by it.
- `private_edge_requirement`: a slight interior/private edge makes presentation feel more live and less billboard-like.
- `soft_base_sharp_event`: soft visual base with sharper events in eyes, hair, silhouette, jewelry, makeup, pose, or clothing.
- `bounded_abundance`: variation is welcome when the presentation remains coherent.

Non-signals:
- location
- selfie versus professional shot
- mirror versus posed shoot
- nudity by itself
- original folder labels or demographic buckets
- exact outfit category
- explicitness as a substitute for subject coherence

Best compressions:

> Attraction is channel convergence with low internal friction, not demographic type-detection.

> The signal needs an owner.

> Beauty here often has a private edge, not just public polish.

> Older attraction evidence gets weight when it still resonates or repeats in newer examples.

> Face/body type matters more than the capture container.

## Preservation Rule

Do not commit broad raw image dumps unless a future visual-corpus application needs reproducible fixtures. Promote only selected, rights-safe, deliberately named examples when they become necessary for a tool, report, or model check.
