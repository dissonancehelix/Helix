## 1. Purpose

The minimal-self operationalization report found that minimal self survives as a
high-value bridge candidate only if treated as a thin for-me-ness channel under
dissociation pressure. This report compares that channel directly against
self-model theory.

Minimal self may be closer to lived for-me-ness. Self-model theory may be more
operationally tractable. The pressure question is whether represented
subjecthood is enough, or whether lived for-me-ness requires something more.

## 2. Claims Under Pressure

Claim A:

> Pre-reflective for-me-ness is not reducible to self-modeling or represented
> subject structure.

Claim B:

> Self-modeling may explain the appearance of subjecthood without requiring a
> separate minimal-self primitive.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- minimal self is not assumed to solve A0
- self-model theory is not assumed false
- self-modeling is not assumed sufficient
- for-me-ness is not assumed independent by definition
- LLM negative controls do not prove future AI lacks consciousness

## 5. Core Discrimination Question

What would show that for-me-ness is more than modeled subject structure?

### Dissociation evidence

- What favors minimal self:
  - lived mineness or thin presence varies without matching explicit
    self-representation changes
- What favors self-model theory:
  - the relevant dissociations map cleanly to subject-model changes
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - all evidence remains report-level and interpretation-heavy

### Negative controls

- What favors minimal self:
  - strong self-description and persona continuity occur without any reason to
    attribute lived for-me-ness
- What favors self-model theory:
  - negative controls only block weak self-model evidence, not richer
    representational accounts
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - negative controls show insufficiency of report but not what extra factor is
    required

### Altered ownership cases

- What favors minimal self:
  - body ownership or agency can vary while thinner for-me-ness appears to
    persist
- What favors self-model theory:
  - altered mineness tracks model-level body/agency disruptions with no extra
    remainder
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - cases are too mixed to separate thin selfhood from representational change

### Flow/reduced reflection cases

- What favors minimal self:
  - reflection drops while lived task-presence remains intact
- What favors self-model theory:
  - reduced reflection only hides an unchanged self-model rather than exposing
    a distinct thin self
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - flow is described vividly but no operational split appears

### Dream/re-entry cases

- What favors minimal self:
  - first-person structure may persist despite degraded report or unstable
    waking narrative
- What favors self-model theory:
  - dream and re-entry structure are best explained as altered self-modeling
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - later report residue is too thin to separate the candidates

### LLM persona/self-report cases

- What favors minimal self:
  - they show that strong first-person description and persona continuity do
    not establish for-me-ness
- What favors self-model theory:
  - they only weaken shallow report-based versions, not full self-model theory
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - current negative controls do not decide future artificial selfhood

### Body ownership / agency dissociations

- What favors minimal self:
  - thinner for-me-ness persists through altered body/agency channels
- What favors self-model theory:
  - those alterations are fully captured by body and agency model changes
- What leaves both unresolved:
  - no independent criterion distinguishes lived remainder from model residue

## 7. LLM Negative Control Pressure

Current LLMs can produce:

- first-person language
- persona continuity
- explicit self-description
- memory-like summaries and records
- forked session identity

Which parts look like self-modeling?

- perspective language
- self-referential role structure
- continuity summaries
- modeled preferences and narrative position

Which parts fail to establish for-me-ness?

- none of them independently establish inhabited subjectivity
- none of them secure ownership, non-branching continuity, or lived presence
- all remain compatible with patterned output and represented subject-structure

Does this weaken self-model theory, or only weak self-model evidence?

- mostly it weakens weak self-model evidence
- it does not automatically refute richer self-model theories
- but it does force self-model theory to specify what extra factor would turn
  represented subjecthood into lived subjecthood

How does this protect minimal self from being reduced to report/persona?

- it cleanly subtracts first-person language, persona continuity, and stable
  self-description from any claim to for-me-ness
- it stops minimal self from being lazily identified with verbally competent
  subject-talk

What remains unresolved about future artificial systems?

- whether a richer artificial system could host non-copy-trivial owner evidence
- whether future self-model architectures could support something closer to thin
  for-me-ness
- whether current negative controls scale to future systems

## 8. Flow / Reduced Reflection Pressure

Flow states are central because they put pressure on explicit self-model access
without obviously erasing lived presence.

Pressure questions:

- Does flow reduce self-modeling or only reflective access to self-modeling?
  - current best answer: unclear; it may reduce explicit access more than the
    deeper representational structure
- Does pre-reflective self-awareness remain?
  - plausibly yes, and this is one of the strongest reasons minimal self
    remains competitive
- Can self-model theory explain flow without eliminating for-me-ness?
  - yes in principle, by treating minimal self as hidden or compressed
    representational structure
- What would distinguish minimal self from hidden self-model structure?
  - a case where reflection and explicit self-representation drop, yet a thin
    first-person channel remains that is not predicted well by self-model
    vocabulary alone
- What false positives remain?
  - smooth performance mistaken for pre-reflective selfhood
  - reduced reflection mistaken for absent self
  - compelling phenomenology mistaken for mechanism

Current result: flow gives minimal self some bridge credit, but not enough to
win the comparison.

## 9. Ownership Dissociation Pressure

Use A2 pressure cases:

- body ownership can vary while fielded appearance and report persist
- agency/authorship can vary while perspective and some sense of presence remain
- depersonalization can distort mineness while reflection/report remain strong

Pressure questions:

- Can body ownership vary while for-me-ness persists?
  - plausibly yes
- Can agency/authorship vary while for-me-ness persists?
  - plausibly yes
- Can self-model theory predict these dissociations better than minimal self?
  - sometimes, because it is more mechanistically explicit about representational
    shifts
- Does minimal self become circular if defined only as "what remains"?
  - yes, that is its main operational risk

Current result: ownership dissociation cases keep minimal self alive, but they
also protect self-model theory from easy dismissal because representational
accounts do have plausible handles on these distortions.

## 12. What Would Strengthen Minimal Self?

- non-circular dissociation from self-modeling
- persistence through reduced reflective self-awareness
- altered ownership cases not fully explained by self-model changes
- negative controls showing self-model-like output without for-me-ness evidence
- predictions against self-model theory specific enough to fail

## 13. What Would Strengthen Self-Model Theory?

- modeled subject structure predicts ownership or for-me-ness dissociations
  better than minimal-self categories
- flow, depersonalization, body illusion, and agency cases map cleanly to
  self-model alterations
- LLM or self-report negative controls can be separated from richer self-model
  requirements
- self-model theory explains for-me-ness without circular residue

## 14. Main Finding

Minimal self currently survives self-model pressure as a plausible thin
for-me-ness target, especially under flow and ownership-dissociation cases. But
it remains underoperationalized unless it can produce non-circular predictions
against self-model theory.

Self-model theory remains more mechanistically tractable but more vulnerable to
negative controls where represented selfhood appears without owner evidence.
