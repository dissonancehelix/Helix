## 1. Purpose

The ownership nonunitary Level 2 design selected depersonalization/derealization
as the highest-priority next pressure case because it may preserve perception
and report while altering ordinary mineness, self-world intimacy, affective
ownership, and embodied presence.

Depersonalization is not proof of minimal self. Depersonalization is not proof
of ownership nonunitarity. It is a pressure case for separating report,
self-model, valence, body/world intimacy, and thin mineness.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> Depersonalization/derealization may show report-preserved but
> ownership-altered experience: perception and report can persist while
> ordinary mineness / for-me-ness thins, estranges, or loses intimacy.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- depersonalization does not prove minimal self
- depersonalization does not prove ownership nonunitarity
- depersonalization does not prove A0
- clinical reports are not transparent access to original structure
- self-model theory is not assumed false
- valence/stakes are not assumed sufficient

## 5. Core Discrimination Question

Is depersonalization best explained as:

A. altered thin mineness / minimal self  
B. altered self-model / self-world representation  
C. altered valence / affective intimacy  
D. altered embodiment / body-world coupling  
E. a mixed nonunitary ownership disturbance

### A. Altered thin mineness / minimal self

- What it predicts:
  - report and perception can remain while the ordinary sense of being the
    subject of experience thins or estranges
- What would strengthen it:
  - repeated depersonalization cases show a structured ownership texture that is
    not reducible to ordinary self-description, arousal, or body-model change
- What would weaken it:
  - every reported alteration is fully captured by self-model, valence, or body
    disturbance
- What would leave it unresolved:
  - reports remain rich but operationally underdetermined

### B. Altered self-model / self-world representation

- What it predicts:
  - depersonalization is best understood as a disturbance in self/world
    representation
- What would strengthen it:
  - the observed pattern maps cleanly onto model-level shifts in self/world
    relation
- What would weaken it:
  - report-preserved cases show a stable altered-mineness pattern that the
    self-model account cannot separate
- What would leave it unresolved:
  - the model explanation is too broad and absorbs every contrast

### C. Altered valence / affective intimacy

- What it predicts:
  - emotional numbing, aversive estrangement, or flattened mattering are central
    to the ownership change
- What would strengthen it:
  - affective flattening tracks depersonalization more closely than report or
    self-model disturbance
- What would weaken it:
  - depersonalization can persist without strong affective flattening or
    arousal change
- What would leave it unresolved:
  - valence and self-model move together, making the explanation too broad

### D. Altered embodiment / body-world coupling

- What it predicts:
  - body alienation and world-unreality arise from altered embodiment or
    body-world coupling
- What would strengthen it:
  - body alienation patterns explain the ownership texture better than report or
    self-description
- What would weaken it:
  - world-alienation persists without body alienation or strong control changes
- What would leave it unresolved:
  - embodiment explains some but not all of the altered intimacy

### E. Mixed nonunitary ownership disturbance

- What it predicts:
  - depersonalization is not one channel but a patterned mix across report,
    self-model, valence, embodiment, and thin mineness
- What would strengthen it:
  - semi-independent variation appears across multiple depersonalization cases
- What would weaken it:
  - the pattern collapses into one dominant explanation
- What would leave it unresolved:
  - the mix is real but too report-heavy to discriminate

## 6. Report-Preserved / Mineness-Altered Pressure

Pressure questions:

- Can report remain strong while mineness thins?
  - yes, that is the central pressure question.
- Can perception remain ordinary while ownership texture changes?
  - plausibly yes.
- Does reflective distress increase while pre-reflective mineness weakens?
  - plausibly yes, and that split is part of what makes depersonalization useful.
- Does this pressure report-first theories?
  - yes, because report can persist while ownership changes.
- Does it support ownership nonunitarity or only clinical description?
  - it supports ownership nonunitarity as a pressure result, but not yet as a
    promoted claim.

Current result: depersonalization is one of the strongest pressure cases for
distinguishing report from mineness, but its evidential value still depends on
how cleanly the altered-mineness pattern can be separated from self-model,
valence, and embodiment explanations.

## 7. Minimal Self vs Self-Model

Pressure questions:

- Does depersonalization show thin for-me-ness altered beyond explicit
  self-model/report?
  - plausibly yes, but that remains interpretive unless the pattern is made
    fail-able.
- Can self-model theory explain the syndrome more cleanly as altered self-world
  representation?
  - yes, and that is its strongest nearby explanation.
- What would favor Minimal Self?
  - structured altered-mineness with report and perception preserved, but not
    exhausted by self-description or self-world model change.
- What would favor Self-Model Theory?
  - the syndrome maps cleanly to model-level disturbance with no leftover
    ownership texture.
- What result would weaken Minimal Self?
  - every case reduces to self-model disturbance or report-level distress.
- What result would weaken Self-Model Theory?
  - depersonalization preserves a distinct altered-mineness pattern that the
    self-model account cannot isolate.

Current result: depersonalization supports Minimal Self as a target better than
it supports Self-Model Theory as a complete explanation, but only provisionally.

## 8. Minimal Self vs Valence / Stakes

Pressure questions:

- Does emotional numbing or aversive estrangement explain the ownership change?
  - it may explain part of it, and sometimes a lot of it.
- Is mineness partly affective intimacy?
  - that is now a live pressure possibility.
- Can valence shift while perception/report remain?
  - yes, and this is one reason valence is a serious rival.
- Does valence explain why the state feels alien without explaining appearance?
  - it may explain alienation well without explaining why anything is present
    from within.
- What would favor Valence/stakes over Minimal Self?
  - the ownership texture follows affective flattening or estrangement more
    closely than self-model or body/world changes.
- What would favor Minimal Self over Valence/stakes?
  - altered mineness remains detectable when valence is held as a secondary or
    partial factor.

Current result: valence is a real competing explanation, not an accessory.

## 9. Depersonalization vs Body Ownership / Embodiment

Pressure questions:

- Is body alienation enough to explain depersonalization?
  - sometimes, but not obviously always.
- Can world-unreality occur without strong body ownership change?
  - plausibly yes.
- Can body ownership, world-intimacy, and thin mineness split?
  - yes, and that split is exactly what matters here.
- What would favor embodiment accounts?
  - body alienation or altered self-location explains most of the syndrome.
- What would favor nonunitary ownership?
  - body alienation is only one lane, with other lanes (report, valence,
    mineness) showing semi-independent variation.

Current result: embodiment remains a strong rival, especially when depersonalization
feels bodily, but it does not automatically absorb world-alienation plus
affective flattening plus report preservation.

## 11. Level 2 Evaluation

Level 2 criteria:

- discriminates against at least two neighbor theories
- blocks known proxy collapses
- makes predictions specific enough to fail
- could change claim maturity
- pre-specifies overclaim routes
- does not rely only on report or subtraction

Evaluation:

- Minimal Self
  - strengthened if:
    - structured altered-mineness is present with report and perception intact
      but not reducible to self-model, valence, or embodiment alone
  - weakened if:
    - the syndrome collapses into self-model disturbance or affective flattening
      in every case
- Self-Model Theory
  - strengthened if:
    - self/world representation changes explain the pattern without residual
      ownership texture
  - weakened if:
    - report-preserved cases consistently retain an altered-mineness pattern
      beyond self-model prediction
- Valence/stakes
  - strengthened if:
    - emotional numbing or estrangement predicts the ownership change better
      than other accounts
  - weakened if:
    - affect is present but not explanatory of the ownership texture
- Embodiment
  - strengthened if:
    - body alienation or self-location changes explain the syndrome more cleanly
      than the alternatives
  - weakened if:
    - world-alienation and affective flattening remain after body factors are
      controlled

Current result: depersonalization can support Level 2 discrimination only if it
is paired with structured contrasts against ordinary anxiety, body alienation,
and report fluency. On its own, it is powerful but still report-heavy.

## 13. What Would Strengthen Depersonalization As A Level 2 Route?

- structured altered-mineness pattern not exhausted by report fluency
- preserved perception/report with predictable ownership texture change
- distinction from ordinary anxiety, arousal, or distress
- separation from pure self-model disturbance
- separation from pure valence or affective flattening
- prediction specific enough to fail

## 14. What Would Weaken Depersonalization As A Level 2 Route?

- all evidence remains clinical report only
- mineness alteration reduces to self-model disturbance
- mineness alteration reduces to affective flattening or anxiety
- mineness alteration reduces to embodiment disturbance
- no clear contrast against ordinary anxiety or dissociation
- thin mineness remains residual label

## 15. Main Finding

Depersonalization/derealization is one of the strongest A2 pressure cases
because it may preserve report and perception while altering ordinary mineness
and self-world intimacy. It can push ownership nonunitarity closer to Level 2
only if it yields structured contrasts against self-model, valence, and
embodiment explanations.

Otherwise it remains a powerful but report-heavy pressure case.
