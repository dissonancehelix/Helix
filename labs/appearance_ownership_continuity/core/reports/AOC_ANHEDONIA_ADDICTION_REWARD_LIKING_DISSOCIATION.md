## 1. Purpose

The previous reward/relief report blocked the broad shortcut from reward, reinforcement, optimization, preference, or relief behavior to lived pleasure. This report applies harder pressure using cases where those components split: anhedonia, addiction, compulsion, craving, liking/wanting dissociation, and relief without positive pleasure.

Core question:

> Do reward pursuit, wanting, liking, pleasure, relief, compulsion, and felt value vary semi-independently enough that AOC needs a more granular valence/ownership decomposition?

Conservative expectation:

- wanting is not liking
- compulsion is not pleasure
- reward pursuit is not felt value
- relief is not always positive pleasure
- anhedonia pressures reward-as-pleasure shortcuts
- addiction pressures preference-as-lived-value shortcuts
- dissociation strengthens valence as ownership-texture pressure without solving A0

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Shortcut under test:

> If a system pursues, chooses, repeats, craves, or optimizes for a target, that target is liked, pleasurable, or felt as valuable.

AOC conservative target:

> Reward pursuit, wanting, liking, compulsion, pleasure, and relief are separable enough that no single marker should be allowed to stand in for lived valence.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- anhedonia does not solve A0
- addiction does not prove a theory of valence
- dopamine is not pleasure
- wanting is not liking
- craving is not satisfaction
- compulsion is not enjoyment
- relief is not necessarily positive pleasure
- report of pleasure is not pleasure itself
- reward dissociation does not prove minimal self

## 5. Anhedonia Pressure

Anhedonia is useful because it can preserve action, habit, cognitive evaluation, or even reward-relevant behavior while reducing felt pleasure or interest. This pressures any account that equates reward markers, choice, or functioning with lived positive valence.

AOC value:

- separates preference/choice from felt liking
- separates function from lived value
- separates reportable evaluation from positive ownership texture
- pressures self-model-only accounts if self-description remains while felt value drops
- pressures reward-only accounts if reward/pursuit can persist without pleasure

Anhedonia does not prove what pleasure is. It only blocks weak substitutions.

## 6. Addiction / Compulsion Pressure

Addiction and compulsion are useful because pursuit can become strong when pleasure is weak, absent, ambivalent, or negative. Wanting can become pathologically intense while liking decreases.

AOC value:

- separates wanting from liking
- separates pursuit from endorsement
- separates compulsion from pleasure
- separates reward-linked behavior from felt value
- exposes cases where the system behaves `as if` something matters while the lived texture is conflicted or aversive

This is directly relevant to ownership texture: a compulsive urge may be experienced as mine, alien, intrusive, resisted, or partially owned. That makes it useful for A2 pressure without turning it into A0 closure.

## 7. Relief Without Pleasure

Relief is not always positive pleasure. Ending an aversive state can feel good, neutral, exhausted, flat, or merely less bad. A system can terminate error or reduce threat without any lived relief being established.

AOC separation:

- aversion reduction is not identical to pleasure
- relief behavior is not identical to lived relief
- satiety signal is not identical to satisfaction
- restoration is not identical to felt completion

This matters because pain/bodily-need pressure made relief look like a strong stakes marker. It is strong, but not self-interpreting.

## 8. Residue Lens: Evidence Discipline Only

Behavior traces, relapse histories, self-reports, clinical scales, reward metrics, and preference logs are residue/evidence channels. They help reconstruct the state after transformation, but none is identical to lived pleasure or ownership.

Allowed use:

- compare multiple residue families before inferring valence
- treat action, report, physiology, and history as partial evidence
- flag dissociations as pressure points
- avoid treating one trace as the target

Blocked use:

- residue is not appearance
- relapse history is not pleasure
- reward metric is not felt value
- clinical report is not the full lived state
- behavioral trace is not ownership

## 10. Rival-Theory Pressure

### Predictive Processing / Active Inference

These accounts gain pressure if wanting, craving, compulsion, and relief are explained by prediction, precision, policy selection, and control under uncertainty.

They weaken if control descriptions explain pursuit without explaining lived liking, felt relief, or ownership texture.

### Self-Model Theory

Self-model theory gains pressure if valence depends on represented organism-relative value and self-evaluation.

It weakens if self-evaluation persists while lived pleasure collapses, or if self-model language is generated without owner evidence.

### Embodied / Enactive Accounts

Embodied/enactive views gain pressure because wanting, craving, relief, and anhedonia are organism-world coupling phenomena, not neutral symbolic labels.

They weaken if coupling/regulation is treated as sufficient for felt value.

### Minimal Self

Minimal self gains pressure if pleasure, craving, relief, and compulsion show thin `for-me` or `against-me` textures that vary independently from report and action.

It weakens if these textures reduce cleanly to reward, self-model content, or behavior.

## 11. Level 2 Evaluation

This report creates stronger Level 2 pressure than the broad reward report because it uses dissociation rather than only negative controls.

It discriminates against:

- reward-as-pleasure shortcuts
- wanting-as-liking shortcuts
- compulsion-as-enjoyment shortcuts
- pursuit-as-felt-value shortcuts
- relief-behavior-as-lived-relief shortcuts
- satiety-signal-as-satisfaction shortcuts
- report-only pleasure accounts
- synthetic reward/persona language as valence evidence

It does not establish:

- A0
- a full pleasure mechanism
- minimal self as canon
- artificial pleasure
- animal pleasure status by itself
- owner continuity

Result: Level 2 dissociation pressure. It strengthens `REWARD_NOT_PLEASURE` and suggests a narrower future guardrail: `WANTING_NOT_LIKING`.

## 13. Main Finding

Anhedonia and addiction strongly pressure reward-as-pleasure and pursuit-as-liking shortcuts. Wanting, liking, reward, compulsion, relief, satisfaction, and report can vary semi-independently enough that AOC should not treat any one channel as lived valence.

This supports the broader pattern already seen in ownership: the target is nonunitary under pressure. Valence may also be partially nonunitary, with distinguishable channels for motivational pull, hedonic liking, aversive pressure, relief, satisfaction, report, and ownership texture.

This is not a mechanism and not A0 closure. It is a sharper false-positive battery.
