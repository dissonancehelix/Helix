## 1. Purpose
The depersonalization pressure report moved `OWNERSHIP_NONUNITARY` closer but not ready. The next needed case family is rubber-hand / body-illusion measure dissociation, because it can test whether body ownership, proprioceptive/body-position measures, ownership judgment, embodiment, agency, and thin mineness split.

Rubber-hand illusion is not proof of ownership nonunitarity. Proprioceptive drift is not ownership. Subjective ownership judgment is report residue. Local limb ownership is not all mineness. The goal is a second independent case family for Level 2 pressure.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure
Test this claim:

Rubber-hand and related body-illusion cases show that body ownership is not a single unified variable: ownership judgment, proprioceptive/body-position measures, embodiment, agency/control, and thin mineness can partially dissociate.

State stronger claims NOT assumed:
- rubber-hand illusion does not prove thin mineness
- rubber-hand illusion does not prove ownership nonunitarity
- body ownership is not identical to mineness
- proprioceptive drift is not ownership
- ownership report is not transparent access
- embodiment is not assumed sufficient
- self-model theory is not assumed false

## 5. Core Discrimination Question
Is rubber-hand/body-illusion evidence best explained as:

A. body-model / embodiment remapping  
B. self-model / represented ownership change  
C. local body ownership shift  
D. measure-level dissociation without ownership  
E. partial ownership nonunitarity

Working pressure:
- A is tempting when multisensory conflict cleanly changes body representation.
- B is tempting when ownership judgment and body-position updating look like outputs of a representational model.
- C is tempting when the illusion seems to move a limb-specific ownership channel without touching the rest.
- D is tempting when drift and ownership judgment split too sharply for any simple ownership story.
- E is tempting only if the dissociation pattern repeats across cases and survives the strongest negative controls.

Current interpretation:
Rubber-hand is strongest when treated as a dissociation test, not a proof machine. It pressures body ownership reduction more directly than it pressures minimal self toward a positive mechanism.

## 6. Measure Dissociation Pressure
1. Subjective ownership without proportional proprioceptive drift  
   - What the measure captures: explicit ownership judgment.
   - What it does not capture: the full structure of owned presence.
   - Which theory is pressured: self-model and embodiment accounts that expect one clean output.
   - Support type: measure caution plus possible nonunitarity.

2. Proprioceptive drift without subjective ownership  
   - What the measure captures: body-position updating.
   - What it does not capture: ownership itself.
   - Which theory is pressured: drift-as-ownership shortcuts.
   - Support type: strong false-positive warning.

3. Body-position change without mineness change  
   - What the measure captures: sensorimotor recalibration.
   - What it does not capture: thin for-me-ness.
   - Which theory is pressured: embodiment accounts that overgeneralize.
   - Support type: measure dissociation only.

4. Local limb ownership without global ownership shift  
   - What the measure captures: region-specific ownership texture.
   - What it does not capture: whole-person mineness.
   - Which theory is pressured: unified ownership views.
   - Support type: partial nonunitarity pressure.

5. Ownership judgment without thin-mineness evidence  
   - What the measure captures: reportable stance.
   - What it does not capture: original ownership structure.
   - Which theory is pressured: report-first interpretations.
   - Support type: report residue caution.

6. Threat / physiological response without ownership judgment  
   - What the measure captures: bodily salience or defensive reaction.
   - What it does not capture: explicit ownership.
   - Which theory is pressured: physiology-as-ownership shortcuts.
   - Support type: mixed; useful only if dissociated from judgment and drift.

7. Agency / control absent or weak while ownership changes  
   - What the measure captures: reduced authorship or control coupling.
   - What it does not capture: mineness itself.
   - Which theory is pressured: agency/control accounts that overidentify control with ownership.
   - Support type: promising if dissociated from drift and judgment.

Current result:
Rubber-hand adds real measure dissociation pressure, but the main lesson is still negative: the measures can come apart. That makes the case family useful, but it does not yet give a clean positive handle on thin mineness.

## 7. Rubber-Hand vs Minimal Self
- Does local body ownership shift imply thin mineness shift? Not by itself.
- Can thin for-me-ness remain stable while local body ownership changes? Yes, and that is the pressure point.
- Does rubber-hand strengthen minimal self? Slightly, as a target, because it gives a concrete dissociation surface.
- What would favor Minimal Self? Repeated cases where body ownership and body-position updating split from a stable thin-mineness pattern.
- What would weaken Minimal Self? If every apparent mineness pattern reduces cleanly to body-position updating, report, or body-model remapping.
- What remains unresolved? Whether the remaining thin-mineness channel is genuine or just a residual label.

Current result:
Rubber-hand is supportive of minimal self only in a constrained, target-sharpening sense. It does not yet earn mechanism credit.

## 8. Rubber-Hand vs Embodiment
- Does embodiment/body-model remapping explain the observed measure pattern? Often, yes, at least for drift and self-location.
- Are proprioceptive drift and ownership judgment separable embodiment outputs? They can be.
- Does embodiment explain more than minimal self here? Yes for drift and body-position updating; less clearly for thin mineness.
- What would favor embodiment accounts? Tight prediction of drift, self-location, and ownership judgment from multisensory conflict patterns.
- What would weaken embodiment accounts? Cases where embodiment changes but the mineness texture remains patterned in a way embodiment does not predict.

Current result:
Embodiment remains a strong neighbor theory. Rubber-hand pressures it, but does not yet outrun it.

## 9. Rubber-Hand vs Self-Model Theory
- Does self-model theory explain ownership judgment and body-position updating better than minimal self? Often, yes.
- Can self-model theory absorb measure dissociation as different representational outputs? It can try.
- Does LLM/persona negative-control pressure matter here? Only indirectly. Rubber-hand is embodied enough that LLM controls stay backgrounded, but they still matter as a warning against treating reportability as evidence of lived ownership.
- What would favor Self-Model Theory? A clean mapping from ownership judgment, drift, and self-location onto representational updates with no leftover mineness-specific remainder.
- What would weaken Self-Model Theory? Persistent dissociation where report and representation move, but thin mineness still shows a stable or independent pattern not predicted by the model.

Current result:
Self-Model Theory remains competitive and mechanically useful, but rubber-hand does not settle the mineness question in its favor.

## 10. Relation To Depersonalization Case Family
Compared with depersonalization, rubber-hand provides a second independent case family with a different pressure geometry:
- depersonalization pressures report-preserved but ownership-altered experience from the inside-out side.
- rubber-hand pressures body-ownership and body-position dissociation from the outside-in side.

This helps because the two families are not the same proxy in different clothing. Depersonalization gives the clearest report-preserved altered-mineness pattern. Rubber-hand gives the clearest body-position / ownership-measure split.

Current result:
Yes, rubber-hand adds second-family support. It is not just measurement caution. It does not finish the job, but it does meet the “independent family” requirement better than flow did.

## 12. Level 2 Evaluation
- Minimal Self: gains some level-2 usefulness because rubber-hand shows a second independent dissociation family, but the thin-mineness handle is still not fully operational. Result that strengthens it: repeated semi-independent dissociation across ownership judgment, drift, and mineness texture. Result that weakens it: every split reduces to measurement artifact or embodiment remapping.

- Self-Model Theory: remains strong because it explains the representational side of ownership judgment and body-position updating. Result that strengthens it: a clean representational map for drift, judgment, and self-location with no stable mineness remainder. Result that weakens it: repeated dissociation patterns where report and representation move but thin mineness seems to persist or vanish independently.

- Embodiment: very strong on drift and body-position updating. Result that strengthens it: stable prediction of ownership judgment and body-position change under multisensory conflict. Result that weakens it: cases where embodiment explains the measures but not the mineness texture.

- Agency / control theory: useful for agency-linked cases, but weaker for pure ownership shifts. Result that strengthens it: agency change predicts ownership measures better than body-model or mineness channels. Result that weakens it: ownership shifts occur with agency relatively stable.

Current result:
Rubber-hand gives more than caution. It adds a second family with a different measure geometry, but it still does not by itself deliver a clean Level 2 bridge.

This is enough to move the claim closer to candidate-ledger readiness, but not enough to add it yet without human review.

## 14. What Would Strengthen Rubber-Hand As A Level 2 Route?
- clear drift/ownership dissociation across multiple cases
- local body ownership shifts without global ownership collapse
- measure patterns that embodiment or self-model theory predict differently
- distinction between ownership judgment and body-position updating
- second independent family support for `OWNERSHIP_NONUNITARY`
- predictions specific enough to fail

## 15. What Would Weaken Rubber-Hand As A Level 2 Route?
- all evidence reduces to measurement artifact
- ownership judgment and drift are too noisy to compare
- local limb ownership cannot generalize to thin mineness
- embodiment explains all measure patterns cleanly
- self-model theory explains all measure patterns cleanly
- no contrast with depersonalization or agency dissociation

## 16. Main Finding
Rubber-hand/body-illusion evidence is a strong second pressure family for ownership nonunitarity because it can separate local body ownership, proprioceptive/body-position updating, and ownership judgment. It adds independent support beyond depersonalization, so this is not merely measurement caution. But it still does not yet give a positive operational handle on thin mineness strong enough to crown a bridge. The report says “closer,” not “done.”
