## 1. Purpose

Synthetic negative controls blocked the weak move from affect language, reward, preference, persona, or distress-report to lived valence. This report tests the stronger positive pressure surface: pain, hunger, thirst, breathlessness, nausea, fatigue, temperature threat, itch, and bodily urgency.

These cases are useful because they are not merely abstract self-descriptions. They are organism-relative states with urgency, aversion, localization, regulation, and action pressure. But they are still not allowed to become A0 by rhetorical force. Pain is not a magic bridge. Hunger is not a theory of consciousness. Bodily stakes are pressure surfaces, not solutions.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

> Pain and bodily-need states may expose an affective-stakes lane in ownership texture: some experiences are not only present or reportable, but urgent-for, bad-for, needed-by, or intimately imposed on the subject.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- pain does not solve A0
- nociception is not pain
- bodily need is not ownership by itself

- suffering risk is not proof of consciousness

## 5. Pain vs Nociception

Pain is not nociception. Nociception can operate as signal and reflex pathway. Pain, as a lived state, includes aversive appearance, bodily localization, urgency, and subject-relative badness.

AOC pressure value:

- blocks responsiveness-as-consciousness
- blocks signal-as-experience
- blocks behavior-as-suffering
- forces valence to separate lived badness from detection/control

Pain strengthens stakes pressure only if the target is lived aversive intimacy, not mere tissue-threat processing.

## 6. Bodily Need vs Homeostatic Regulation

Hunger, thirst, breathlessness, fatigue, nausea, and temperature threat combine regulation with felt pressure. Homeostasis can explain why the system must act. It does not automatically explain why the need is felt as mine, urgent, or bad-from-within.

AOC interpretation:

- regulation is a plausible substrate/neighbor
- interoception may be a major bridge ingredient
- lived need remains underexplained if reduced to control variables

## 7. Pain / Need vs Minimal Self

Minimal self gains pressure if bodily stakes expose a thin `for-me` structure: pain is not merely detected; it is imposed on a subject. Hunger is not merely a variable; it is a need felt from a perspective.

What favors Minimal Self:

- pain/need states alter ownership texture beyond report and behavior
- aversive intimacy persists even when explicit self-reflection is minimal
- bodily urgency is experienced as happening-to-me before narrative self-evaluation
- depersonalization-like numbing changes bodily intimacy without eliminating perception

What weakens Minimal Self:

- all effects reduce to interoceptive regulation
- all effects reduce to self-model body evaluation
- all effects reduce to action tendency
- thin mineness remains a residual label

## 8. Pain / Need vs Self-Model Theory

Self-Model Theory can explain pain and bodily need as represented body-state evaluation. This remains a strong rival.

What favors Self-Model Theory:

- pain/need experience tracks body-model and self-evaluation structure
- affective ownership changes map cleanly to model updates
- no stable mineness pattern remains outside modeled content

What weakens Self-Model Theory:

- urgency and aversive intimacy arise before explicit self-evaluation
- pain/need ownership texture shifts independently of reportable self-model content
- synthetic self-model labels and pain-language fail to reproduce owner evidence

## 9. Pain / Need vs Agency-Control

Pain and bodily need create action pressure: withdraw, scratch, breathe, drink, rest, eat, vomit, seek warmth, seek relief.

But action pressure is not ownership. Withdrawal reflex is not suffering. Tool execution is not agency ownership. Avoidance behavior is not lived badness.

Agency/control accounts gain if action-pressure explains the relevant structure. They weaken if lived aversive intimacy remains separable from behavior, reflex, and report.

## 11. Level 2 Evaluation

Pain/bodily need gives stronger positive pressure than abstract valence language because it brings regulation, urgency, bodily localization, and action pressure into the same case family.

It discriminates against:

- report-only accounts
- responsiveness/reflex accounts
- reward/preference shortcuts
- weak self-model-label accounts
- pure agency/control reductions

It does not yet establish:

- A0
- a complete minimal-self mechanism
- lived valence in artificial systems
- owner continuity

Result: partial Level 2 pressure as a positive stakes surface, not A0 bridge progress.

## 13. Main Finding

Pain and bodily need are the strongest positive valence/stakes pressure surfaces so far. They show why valence cannot be dismissed as decorative report language: bodily states can impose urgency, aversion, need, relief, and intimacy on the subject.

But AOC guardrails still hold: nociception is not pain, regulation is not lived mattering, reward is not pleasure, and stakes are not appearance. Pain/bodily need strengthens the case for an affective-stakes lane inside ownership texture, but does not explain A0.
