## 1. Purpose

Pain, bodily need, interoception, and homeostasis all point toward relief, satisfaction, satiety, reward, and pleasure. The next false-positive route is treating reward/value optimization or relief behavior as lived pleasure.

This report tests a narrow guardrail:

> Reward, value optimization, reinforcement, and relief-directed behavior are not identical to lived pleasure, satisfaction, or felt relief.

This does not deny that reward systems and relief dynamics may scaffold pleasure. It blocks the stronger substitution: optimization signal = felt value.

Residue-science concepts are used only methodologically here: reward logs, behavior traces, preference records, and post-hoc reports are evidence residues after transformation. They can support inference, but they are not the lived state itself.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Shortcut under test:

> If a system maximizes reward, seeks relief, repeats a behavior, or reports satisfaction, that is sufficient evidence of pleasure or lived value.

AOC conservative target:

> Reward and relief can be important scaffolds for valence, but reward is not pleasure and relief behavior is not lived relief.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- reward does not prove pleasure
- preference does not prove felt value
- reinforcement does not prove enjoyment
- optimization does not prove mattering
- relief behavior does not prove lived relief
- satiety signals are not satisfaction by themselves
- pleasure report is not pleasure itself
- reward circuitry is not A0
- affective valence does not solve A0

## 5. Why Reward Is Tempting

Reward is tempting because it appears to connect behavior, learning, value, and future action. If a system repeatedly seeks something, protects access to it, updates around it, and reports liking it, the structure looks valence-like.

This creates real pressure for AOC:

- pleasure and relief may be ownership-texture ingredients
- felt value may bind need, action, and temporal expectation
- relief may mark transition from aversive pressure to restored bodily intimacy
- satisfaction may matter for continuity of embodied self-maintenance

But reward remains ambiguous. Reward can be third-person control, not first-person pleasure.

## 6. Why Reward Is Not Pleasure

Reward can be modeled as signal, error correction, optimization target, policy update, or selection bias. None of those descriptions requires lived pleasantness.

A reinforcement-learning agent can maximize reward without feeling pleasure. A thermostat can reduce error without relief. A plant can orient toward light without enjoyment. A spinal reflex can terminate stimulation without lived relief being established. A current LLM can describe satisfaction without owner evidence.

AOC separation:

- reward = learning/control signal
- preference = selection pattern
- relief behavior = action or transition away from aversive state
- satisfaction = possible lived valence texture
- pleasure = lived positive/attractive appearance
- owned pleasure = pleasure with mineness/for-me texture

The target is not reward. The target is lived positive valence owned from within.

## 7. Residue Lens: Evidence After Transformation

Reward histories, preference logs, action traces, verbal reports, and post-state summaries are residue. They can be useful evidence after a transformation, but they are not identical to the original lived state.

Allowed import from residue thinking:

- use behavior/log/report traces as inference substrates
- track provenance of pleasure claims
- detect missing or biased evidence
- distinguish live state from preserved trace
- compare multiple residue families before assigning confidence

Blocked overclaim:

- residue is not appearance
- residue is not owner
- residue is not continuity
- reward logs are not pleasure
- preference records are not felt value
- post-hoc report is not lived experience itself

This lens improves evidence hygiene without changing AOC's existing claims.

## 9. Rival-Theory Pressure

### Predictive Processing / Active Inference

These accounts gain pressure because reward, relief, and homeostatic correction can be modeled as prediction, error correction, policy selection, precision, and expected free-energy reduction.

They weaken if such control descriptions explain adaptive behavior while leaving lived pleasantness and felt value as residual targets.

### Self-Model Theory

Self-model theory gains pressure if pleasure and satisfaction require represented organism-relative value in a self/body model.

It weakens if self-evaluative pleasure language can be generated without owner evidence or if lived valence dissociates from explicit self-model content.

### Embodied / Enactive Accounts

Embodied/enactive views gain pressure because pleasure, relief, and satisfaction are often embedded in organism-world coupling, bodily restoration, and need resolution.

They weaken if regulation and coupling are treated as sufficient for felt value without a bridge.

### Minimal Self

Minimal self gains pressure if lived pleasure/relief has a thin `for-me` texture prior to narrative report: this relief, this satisfaction, this bodily easing.

It weakens if all positive valence reduces to reward signaling, report, action tendency, or self-model content.

## 13. Main Finding

Reward and relief are serious valence scaffolds, but not sufficient evidence of lived pleasure. AOC should treat reward histories, behavior traces, preference patterns, and pleasure reports as residue/evidence channels that require interpretation, not as the target itself.

This strengthens the affective-stakes lane while tightening false-positive controls. Pleasure, satisfaction, and relief remain important ownership-texture targets, but reward, optimization, and preference do not close A0 and do not establish ownership.
