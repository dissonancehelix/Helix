## 1. Purpose
The rubber-hand measure dissociation report moved `OWNERSHIP_NONUNITARY` closer but not ready. The next needed case family is agency/authorship dissociation, because it can test whether action authorship, motor control, body ownership, self-modeling, report, and thin mineness split.

Agency/authorship is not ownership. Agency loss is not proof of minimal self. Alien limb is not proof of ownership nonunitarity. Report remains residue. The goal is third-family Level 2 pressure and candidate-ledger readiness assessment.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure
Test this claim:

Agency/authorship can partially dissociate from ownership: action control and felt authorship may fracture while body ownership, report, self-modeling, and thin mineness partly persist.

State stronger claims NOT assumed:
- alien limb does not prove minimal self
- alien limb does not prove ownership nonunitarity
- agency loss is not ownership loss by default
- preserved report is not preserved ownership by default
- preserved body ownership is not thin mineness by default
- self-model theory is not assumed false

## 5. Core Discrimination Question
Is alien limb / agency-authorship dissociation best explained as:

A. agency/control disturbance  
B. self-model / agency-model disturbance  
C. body ownership preserved with authorship fractured  
D. thin mineness preserved despite agency fracture  
E. partial ownership nonunitarity

Working pressure:
- A is tempting when the deficit looks like a control problem.
- B is tempting when agency judgments track altered representational structure.
- C is tempting when movement is strange but body-belonging remains.
- D is tempting when lived subjectivity seems thinner or unchanged despite agency fracture.
- E is tempting only if the pattern repeats and adds something beyond motor-control caution.

Current interpretation:
Agency/authorship dissociation is strongest when treated as a split between action control and ownership texture, not as a direct mineness measure.

## 6. Agency vs Ownership Pressure
1. Action occurs without felt authorship  
   - What the case captures: action generation without clear source experience.
   - What it does not capture: thin mineness by itself.
   - Which theory is pressured: agency/control theories that expect authorship to track control.
   - Support type: strong dissociation pressure.

2. Body part remains experienced as bodily despite unwanted action  
   - What the case captures: ownership may persist while agency fractures.
   - What it does not capture: global mineness collapse.
   - Which theory is pressured: agency-as-ownership shortcuts.
   - Support type: strong non-equivalence pressure.

3. Authorship fractures while report remains preserved  
   - What the case captures: preserved verbal description of conflict.
   - What it does not capture: original authorship itself.
   - Which theory is pressured: report-first interpretations.
   - Support type: cautionary, not decisive.

4. Agency judgment changes while field/perception remains preserved  
   - What the case captures: explicit agency evaluation can vary against stable perception.
   - What it does not capture: ownership texture.
   - Which theory is pressured: models that collapse agency into perception.
   - Support type: useful dissociation.

5. Agency disruption without global mineness collapse  
   - What the case captures: local control failure.
   - What it does not capture: whether mineness remains thin.
   - Which theory is pressured: global ownership theories.
   - Support type: possible partial nonunitarity.

6. Motor control/output without owner evidence  
   - What the case captures: fluent movement can occur without a stable agency report.
   - What it does not capture: lived subjectivity.
   - Which theory is pressured: control-as-ownership shortcuts.
   - Support type: strong negative-control logic.

7. LLM/tool execution as negative control for fluent action-like output  
   - What the case captures: complex output without lived agency evidence.
   - What it does not capture: human authorship or mineness.
   - Which theory is pressured: represented-action without owner evidence.
   - Support type: guardrail only, not positive route.

Current result:
Agency/authorship dissociation adds a real third-family pressure point. It is stronger against agency/control reduction than it is directly for minimal self, but it still has enough structure to matter for candidate readiness.

## 7. Alien Limb vs Minimal Self
- Does agency fracture imply thin mineness fracture? Not by default.
- Can thin for-me-ness remain while authorship collapses? Yes, and that is the key pressure.
- Does alien limb strengthen minimal self? Only weakly, by keeping ownership texture separable from action control.
- What would favor Minimal Self? Repeated cases where agency/authorship fractures while a stable thin-mineness pattern remains visible across report and ownership channels.
- What would weaken Minimal Self? If every alien-limb pattern is exhausted by motor-control disturbance or action-monitoring error.
- What remains unresolved? Whether the remaining thin-mineness channel is a genuine ownership lane or just another residual label.

Current result:
Agency/authorship dissociation is supportive of minimal self in a constrained way, but the main gain is discriminating pressure, not mechanism closure.

## 8. Alien Limb vs Agency/Control Theory
- Does agency/control theory explain the observed disturbance cleanly? Often yes for the motor side.
- Is authorship disruption enough to explain ownership texture? Not always.
- Can agency/control theory predict when ownership-like pressure persists? Sometimes, but not without adding assumptions.
- What would favor agency/control accounts? A clean mapping from action disturbances to authorship loss with no stable remainder in ownership texture.
- What would weaken agency/control accounts? Cases where control disruption and ownership texture clearly diverge, especially across report-preserved cases.

Current result:
Agency/control theory remains strong, but alien-limb-style pressure prevents it from swallowing the whole map.

## 9. Alien Limb vs Self-Model Theory
- Does self-model theory explain agency disruption as altered representation of action ownership? Often, yes.
- Can self-model theory absorb body ownership, agency judgment, and report patterns without a thin-mineness remainder? It can try.
- What would favor Self-Model Theory? A clean representational account of agency judgment, motor monitoring, and conflict narrative with no leftover ownership texture.
- What would weaken Self-Model Theory? Stable dissociations where action/report patterns shift but thin mineness or ownership texture does not follow the model cleanly.

Current result:
Self-Model Theory remains live, but agency/authorship dissociation does not collapse into it automatically.

## 12. Level 2 Evaluation
- Minimal Self: gains because agency/authorship fracture can coexist with report and ownership texture. Result that strengthens it: repeated cases where agency fractures while thin mineness remains patterned and distinguishable. Result that weakens it: every case reduces to motor-control or action-monitoring disturbance.

- Self-Model Theory: remains strong because it can describe agency-model disturbance and reportable conflict. Result that strengthens it: a clean representational map of agency disruption across alien limb and related cases. Result that weakens it: persistent thin-mineness remainder not captured by the model.

- Embodiment: useful for body-related action patterns but not enough on its own. Result that strengthens it: if body-ownership and action-control disruptions map tightly onto sensorimotor organization. Result that weakens it: when body ownership stays relatively intact while agency/authorship fractures.

- Agency/control theory: strongest rival on this family. Result that strengthens it: if motor control and authorship differences explain the syndrome without remainder. Result that weakens it: if ownership texture persists independently of control changes.

- Valence/stakes: mostly secondary here, but relevant if affective distress tracks agency fracture. Result that strengthens it: if aversive estrangement or mattering reliably modulates agency complaints. Result that weakens it: if the syndrome appears without major affective change.

Current result:
This family gives real Level 2 pressure, but not a clean win for any one theory.

## 13. Candidate Claim Decision
Recommended status for `OWNERSHIP_NONUNITARY`:
ready for provisional candidate with human review

Why:
- depersonalization, rubber-hand, and agency/authorship dissociation now form three independent pressure families
- each family splits a different ownership channel from its neighboring proxy
- the remaining thin-mineness channel is still underoperationalized, but it is no longer unsupported
- the overall pattern now meets the multi-family threshold for review

This is still not canon, and it still needs human review because thin mineness remains partly residual. But the case is now strong enough to patch as provisional rather than continue hovering in “closer but not ready.”

## 14. What Would Strengthen Agency/Authorship As A Level 2 Route?
- agency/authorship fracture with body ownership partly preserved
- authorship disruption without global thin-mineness collapse
- dissociation between motor output, agency judgment, and owner evidence
- contrast between alien limb and ordinary involuntary/reflex movement
- measure or case pattern that agency/control theory predicts differently from minimal self or self-model theory
- third independent family support for `OWNERSHIP_NONUNITARY`
- predictions specific enough to fail

## 15. What Would Weaken Agency/Authorship As A Level 2 Route?
- all evidence reduces to motor-control disturbance
- agency and ownership always move together in relevant cases
- reports are too noisy to separate authorship from ownership
- self-model theory explains all patterns cleanly
- no contrast with rubber-hand or depersonalization
- thin mineness remains a residual label

## 16. Main Finding
Agency/authorship dissociation is a strong third pressure family for ownership nonunitarity because it can separate action authorship, motor control, body ownership, report, and thin mineness. It is stronger against agency/control reduction than it is directly for minimal self, but it adds enough independent semi-dissociation to move `OWNERSHIP_NONUNITARY` into provisional candidate territory with human approval.
