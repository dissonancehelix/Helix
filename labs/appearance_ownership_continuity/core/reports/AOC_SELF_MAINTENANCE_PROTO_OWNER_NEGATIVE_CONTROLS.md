## 1. Purpose

Interoception/homeostasis created scaffold pressure: bounded systems that regulate their own state can begin to look owner-like. The next hiding place is the proto-owner move:

> If a system maintains itself across time, resists perturbation, protects its boundary, and regulates internal variables, maybe that already supplies the owner.

This report stress-tests that move with negative controls. The goal is not to deny that self-maintenance matters. The goal is to block the shortcut from self-maintenance to lived ownership.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Proto-owner shortcut:

> A bounded self-maintaining system is already an owner, or close enough that ownership can be inferred from self-maintenance.

AOC conservative target:

> Self-maintenance can scaffold owner-like organization, but self-maintenance is not identical to ownership or owner preservation.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- life is not consciousness
- metabolism is not appearance
- boundary maintenance is not mineness
- self-preservation behavior is not selfhood
- continuity of organization is not same-owner continuity
- regulation is not lived mattering
- proto-owner is not owner
- emergence analogies guide test design but do not prove A0

## 5. Why the Proto-Owner Move Is Tempting

Self-maintenance is not a random feature. It bundles several ingredients AOC already cares about:

- boundary preservation
- internal/external distinction
- perturbation resistance
- temporal persistence
- regulation under threat
- need-like variable correction
- adaptive coupling to environment

This looks structurally close to ownership because ownership often appears as a state being `for this bounded continuant` rather than as neutral information.

That is real pressure. AOC should preserve it.

## 6. Why It Still Fails as Ownership

Self-maintenance can be described in third-person control language: variable, error, correction, boundary, persistence, repair, reproduction, optimization.

Nothing in that description yet establishes:

- appearance from within
- mineness
- lived concern
- affective badness/goodness
- a subject for whom the state matters
- same-owner continuity through interruption or transformation

A protocell can maintain a boundary. A thermostat can correct temperature. A robot can protect battery level. An immune system can distinguish self/non-self. These are scaffold-like, but not owner evidence by themselves.

AOC separation:

- self-maintenance = persistence/control scaffold
- proto-owner = owner-like structural precursor
- owner = lived mineness / ownership texture within appearance
- same-owner continuity = preserved owned continuity, not merely preserved organization

## 7. Origin-of-Life Lens: Useful but Dangerous

The origin-of-life analogy is useful because it shows how preconditions can stack into a new regime. Boundary plus gradient plus metabolism plus persistence can transform chemistry into life-like organization.

AOC import:

- look for stacked preconditions
- distinguish necessary from sufficient
- test transition thresholds
- preserve negative controls
- avoid single-factor bridges

Blocked overclaims:

- life is not consciousness
- metabolism is not appearance
- biological self-maintenance is not mineness
- heredity is not same-owner continuity
- emergence is not explanation
- proto-owner is not owner

The analogy helps the test harness. It does not license a metaphysical shortcut.

## 8. Rival-Theory Pressure

### Embodied / Enactive Accounts

Embodied/enactive accounts gain pressure because self-maintaining organism-world coupling is not optional background. It plausibly supplies much of the scaffold for lived stakes.

They weaken if they treat life, autopoiesis, or self-maintenance as sufficient for appearance or ownership without a bridge.

### Predictive Processing / Active Inference

Predictive processing gains pressure because self-maintenance can be formalized as regulation, prediction, action, and viability-preserving control.

It weakens if precision/error/control explain only adaptive regulation while lived mattering remains a residual target.

### Minimal Self

Minimal self gains pressure if self-maintenance plus interoceptive stakes produces a thin `for-me` structure that survives without reflective self-narrative.

It weakens if proto-owner cases remain fully explainable as control organization without mineness.

### Self-Model Theory

Self-model theory gains pressure if ownership appears only when self-maintenance is represented within a body/self model.

It weakens if represented selfhood remains possible without owner evidence, especially in LLM/persona negative controls.

## 12. Main Finding

Self-maintenance is a serious scaffold, not a sufficient owner. Bounded persistence, boundary defense, adaptive regulation, and self-protection make ownership-like structure more plausible than generic computation, but they still do not establish appearance, mineness, lived concern, or same-owner continuity.

The useful result is not `life = consciousness`. The useful result is precondition stacking with negative controls: the more a system has boundary, regulation, interoception, affective stakes, temporal persistence, and nonbranching continuity, the more serious the ownership-scaffold question becomes. But no single scaffold component gets to impersonate the owner.
