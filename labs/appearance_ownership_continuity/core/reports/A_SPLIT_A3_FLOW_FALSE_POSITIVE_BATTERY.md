## 1. Purpose

The Minimal Self vs Self-Model Matrix identified flow/reduced reflection as the
most promising next surface for separating thin for-me-ness from explicit
self-modeling. This report tests whether flow provides real bridge pressure or
only a cluster of false positives.

Flow may reduce reflective self-awareness. Reduced reflection is not absence of
self. Smooth performance is not lived flow. Retrospective flow report is not
the original field. The goal is false-positive separation, not promotion.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> Flow states may preserve pre-reflective for-me-ness while reducing reflective
> self-awareness, but this must be distinguished from self-model backgrounding,
> agency/control coupling, performance smoothness, temporal coding, and
> retrospective report.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- flow does not prove minimal self
- flow does not prove no-self
- flow does not solve A0
- flow reports are not transparent access to original experience
- task performance is not evidence of for-me-ness by itself
- agency/control is not ownership by itself

## 5. Main False Positives

### 1. Smooth performance mistaken for flow

- Why tempting:
  - flow often co-occurs with skilled, low-friction performance
- What it can show:
  - high coordination, low error, absorbed control
- What it cannot show:
  - lived for-me-ness or reduced reflective self-awareness
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `RESPONSIVENESS_NOT_CONSCIOUSNESS`
- Result:
  - blocked as a sufficient interpretation

### 2. Flow report mistaken for original field

- Why tempting:
  - people describe flow vividly and coherently afterward
- What it can show:
  - remembered structure and residue of the episode
- What it cannot show:
  - direct access to the original lived field
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `OWNERSHIP_NOT_REPORT`, `TEMPORAL_FLOW_NOT_MEMORY`
- Result:
  - blocked as transparent evidence

### 3. Reduced reflection mistaken for no-self

- Why tempting:
  - flow reports often describe loss of self-consciousness
- What it can show:
  - reduced self-as-object or explicit self-monitoring
- What it cannot show:
  - total absence of pre-reflective selfhood
- Guardrail triggered:
  - A2/A3 pressure logic, especially `OWNERSHIP_NOT_REPORT`
- Result:
  - blocked

### 4. Reduced reflection mistaken for minimal self proof

- Why tempting:
  - if reflective self drops and experience remains, a thin self seems likely
- What it can show:
  - compatibility with minimal self
- What it cannot show:
  - that minimal self is the only or best explanation
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`
- Result:
  - constrained, not promoted

### 5. Agency/control mistaken for ownership

- Why tempting:
  - flow often involves strong control and fluent doing
- What it can show:
  - strong action coupling and authorship-feel
- What it cannot show:
  - ownership or for-me-ness by itself
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `AGENCY_NOT_OWNERSHIP`
- Result:
  - blocked

### 6. Temporal distortion mistaken for lived duration bridge

- Why tempting:
  - flow often comes with altered sense of time
- What it can show:
  - reported temporal reshaping
- What it cannot show:
  - a mechanism of lived duration
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `TEMPORAL_CODING_NOT_LIVED_DURATION`
- Result:
  - constrained

### 7. Reward/autotelic valence mistaken for appearance

- Why tempting:
  - flow often feels meaningful, rewarding, and worth continuing
- What it can show:
  - mattering, positive valence, motivational grip
- What it cannot show:
  - why appearance exists
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`
- Result:
  - blocked as bridge closure

### 8. Hidden self-model mistaken for absent self-model

- Why tempting:
  - reduced reflective access may feel like reduced self-modeling
- What it can show:
  - explicit self-reference is backgrounded
- What it cannot show:
  - that a subject-model is no longer operating
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`
- Result:
  - unresolved but constrained

### 9. Absorption mistaken for A0

- Why tempting:
  - deep immersion feels close to lived presence
- What it can show:
  - narrowed attention and strong engagement
- What it cannot show:
  - why there is anything it is like from within
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `A0_UNRESOLVED`, `BRIDGE_REQUIRED_FOR_A0`
- Result:
  - blocked

### 10. Neural marker mistaken for lived flow

- Why tempting:
  - flow research often seeks neural signatures
- What it can show:
  - correlates, timing, network changes, control-related shifts
- What it cannot show:
  - original lived for-me-ness or A0
- Guardrail triggered:
  - `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`
- Result:
  - blocked

## 6. Flow vs Minimal Self

Pressure questions:

- Does flow preserve pre-reflective for-me-ness?
  - plausibly yes, but only as a candidate interpretation
- What would count as evidence?
  - reduced reflective self-awareness alongside stable task-world presence that
    is not exhausted by performance, agency, or report
- What would be merely report residue?
  - later claims about timelessness, effortlessness, or self-loss with no
    independent dissociation handle
- Can minimal self explain flow better than self-model theory?
  - not yet clearly; it explains the lived side more naturally, but with weak
    operational traction
- Does flow give minimal self Level 2 progress, or only Level 1 sharpening?
  - only Level 1 sharpening

Current result: flow strengthens minimal self as a plausible target surface,
but does not yet deliver a discriminating test that would raise it to Level 2.

## 7. Flow vs Self-Model

Pressure questions:

- Does flow reduce self-modeling, or only reflective access to self-modeling?
  - current best answer: probably only reflective access, though that remains
    part of the live dispute
- Can self-model theory explain flow as backgrounded or compressed
  self-modeling?
  - yes, and that is one reason flow is not decisive on its own
- What would distinguish hidden self-model from pre-reflective for-me-ness?
  - a case where lived first-person presence remains but the available
    representational/self-descriptive structure does not track it well
- What negative controls matter?
  - LLM self-report and persona continuity
  - copied self-description
  - performance without reported flow

Current result: flow does not refute self-model theory, but it does stop
reflection-heavy or report-heavy versions from taking themselves as the whole
story.

## 8. Flow vs Agency / Control

Pressure questions:

- Is flow mainly a control or skill state?
  - partly yes; that is one of the main impostor routes
- Does strong agency/control coupling imply ownership?
  - no
- Can agency be high while reflective self-awareness is low?
  - yes, and this is one reason flow is interesting
- What does this imply for A2?
  - A2 cannot be reduced to agency or fluent authorship

Current result: flow usefully pressures the agency/ownership split, but strong
control remains a major false positive rather than a solution.

## 9. Flow vs Temporal Coding

Pressure questions:

- Is altered time in flow lived temporal structure or post-hoc report?
  - usually it is available as post-hoc report plus indirect behavioral framing
- Can temporal flow be separated from timing, sequencing, and memory residue?
  - not yet cleanly
- Does flow strengthen A3, or merely rename temporal phenomenology?
  - it strengthens A3 as a target zone, but does not yet operationalize it

Current result: flow helps show that lived time should not be collapsed into
timing markers, but it does not yet explain lived duration.

## 10. Flow vs Valence / Reward

Pressure questions:

- Does autotelic reward add bridge-relevant mattering?
  - it may deepen the sense that the state matters
- Is positive valence central to flow's for-me structure?
  - often important, but not obviously constitutive
- Can valence explain why flow feels meaningful without explaining why it
  appears?
  - yes, and that is the main constraint

Current result: reward and valence are useful neighboring pressures, not a
bridge solution.

## 13. What Would Strengthen Flow As A3/Minimal-Self Pressure?

- reduced reflective self-awareness with preserved first-person task-presence
- dissociation from performance quality alone
- dissociation from explicit self-report
- dissociation from agency/control alone
- non-circular relation to pre-reflective for-me-ness
- ability to distinguish hidden self-model from minimal self
- predictions specific enough to fail

## 14. What Would Weaken Flow As Bridge Pressure?

- all evidence remains retrospective-report based
- flow reduces to performance smoothness
- flow reduces to agency/control coupling
- flow reduces to reward or valence
- flow reduces to hidden self-modeling
- temporal effects reduce to timing/report artifacts
- no prediction separates minimal self from self-model theory

## 15. Main Finding

Flow is valuable because it pressures reflective self-awareness without
obviously eliminating pre-reflective for-me-ness. But flow is also surrounded
by false positives: smooth performance, report residue, agency/control,
temporal distortion, reward, and hidden self-modeling.

Flow strengthens A3/minimal-self pressure only if those false positives are
explicitly blocked.

Current result: flow did not produce Level 2 progress. It sharpened the test
surface, strengthened the need for dissociation discipline, and improved the
map of what must be separated before flow can count as bridge pressure.
