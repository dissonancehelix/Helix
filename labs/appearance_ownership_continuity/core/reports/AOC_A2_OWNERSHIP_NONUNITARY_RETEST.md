## 1. Purpose

The Level 2 pathfinder selected ownership dissociation as the best immediate
route because it pressures body ownership, agency, self-modeling, report,
embodiment, and thin mineness more directly than flow.

Ownership may be nonunitary. Body ownership is not all ownership.
Agency/authorship is not all ownership. Self-modeling is not lived mineness.
Report is residue. The goal is Level 2 discrimination, not claim promotion.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> Ownership is nonunitary: body ownership, agency/authorship, self-modeling,
> reportable self-description, and thin mineness can partially dissociate. If
> so, minimal self gains pressure value only if thin for-me-ness can be
> separated non-circularly from the other ownership channels.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- ownership nonunitarity is not assumed established
- minimal self is not assumed to solve A0
- body ownership is not assumed identical to mineness
- agency is not assumed identical to ownership
- self-modeling is not assumed sufficient for lived subjectivity
- report is not assumed transparent access to original ownership structure

## 6. Depersonalization / Derealization Pressure

Pressure questions:

- Can perception and report persist while ordinary mineness thins or estranges?
  - yes, that is the main reason this case family matters.
- Does depersonalization show altered self-modeling, altered mineness, or both?
  - likely both. The open issue is whether mineness is fully captured by the
    altered model or only tracked by it.
- Can thin for-me-ness persist in estranged form?
  - plausibly yes. The reports suggest alienated or flattened ownership rather
    than simple zero-ownership.
- Does this favor Minimal Self, Self-Model Theory, or neither?
  - it favors Minimal Self as a target and Self-Model Theory as a mechanism
    candidate. It does not cleanly settle between them.
- What would be circular?
  - treating every report of estrangement as direct evidence of a surviving thin
    for-me-ness without independent contrast pressure.

Comparison:

- Minimal Self:
  - gains pressure value because ordinary ownership seems able to thin without
    perception and report disappearing.
- Self-Model Theory:
  - remains strong because depersonalization plausibly involves altered
    self-world representation.
- Affective/valence accounts:
  - gain relevance because estrangement often has strong affective flattening
    or aversive texture.
- No-Bridge restraint:
  - still useful as a caution, but not a substitute for discrimination.

Current result: depersonalization strengthens the case that ownership may have
multiple lanes, but it does not by itself decide whether thin mineness is
independent or only a representational residue.

## 7. Rubber-Hand / Body Ownership Pressure

Rubber-hand and related body-illusion cases can manipulate body ownership.
They also matter because ownership measures and proprioceptive or body-position
measures can dissociate rather than moving as one block.

Pressure questions:

- Can local body ownership shift without full collapse of thin for-me-ness?
  - plausibly yes.
- Do ownership reports, proprioceptive drift, threat response, and agency
  measures dissociate?
  - often yes, which makes this family useful for nonunitarity pressure.
- Does body ownership explain all mineness or only one ownership channel?
  - only one channel, unless overgeneralized.
- What would favor embodiment/body-model accounts?
  - if ownership disturbance patterns are captured cleanly by body-model and
    self-location remapping.
- What would favor Minimal Self?
  - if local body ownership shifts while a thinner first-personal presence
    remains stable enough not to track the body-model change one-to-one.
- What leaves all candidates unresolved?
  - if every relevant signal remains report-heavy or mixed.

Comparison:

- Minimal Self:
  - gains only if thin mineness remains more stable than body ownership.
- Embodiment:
  - gains if ownership shifts are best predicted by sensorimotor/body-model
    reconfiguration.
- Self-Model Theory:
  - gains if body ownership and ownership judgment changes track representational
    shifts without residual channel.
- Agency/control theories:
  - are constrained because many rubber-hand setups are weak on agency.

Current result: rubber-hand pressure supports ownership nonunitarity more than
it supports minimal self directly.

## 8. Full-Body / Self-Location Pressure

Pressure questions:

- Can global self-location shift without proving a change in thin mineness?
  - yes. Perspective relocation and owner relocation are not obviously the same.
- Does perspectival relocation imply ownership relocation?
  - not automatically.
- Can self-location and for-me-ness dissociate?
  - plausibly yes, though this remains underdetermined.
- What distinguishes body-centered perspective from lived subjectivity?
  - perspective gives a location-like frame; lived subjectivity asks whether the
    field is inhabited as mine.

Current result: full-body and body-swap style cases are strong against
body-location-equals-self shortcuts, but weaker than depersonalization or alien
limb for isolating thin mineness.

## 9. Alien Limb / Agency Dissociation Pressure

Pressure questions:

- Can agency/authorship fracture while body ownership or thin mineness persists?
  - plausibly yes, and this is the main reason alien-limb pressure matters.
- Does altered agency imply altered ownership?
  - not necessarily.
- Can agency/control theories predict the dissociation better than minimal
  self?
  - sometimes yes, because agency models are naturally built for authorship
    disruption.
- Does residual perspective-presence count as evidence or only report?
  - it counts as pressure, not transparent proof.

Current result: alien-limb style cases strongly reinforce `AGENCY_NOT_OWNERSHIP`
and make ownership look less unitary. They help minimal self only if the
remaining first-person channel is specified more sharply than "whatever is left
when agency fails."

## 10. Negative Control Pressure

Synthetic and LLM-style controls:

- fluent first-person report
- persona continuity
- forked persona/session identity
- simulated self-model labels
- task performance without owner evidence

Pressure questions:

- Which ownership channels can be imitated?
  - reportable self-description, narrative selfhood, persona continuity,
    self-model-like organization, and performance structure.
- Which channels remain unsupported?
  - thin mineness, owner binding, non-copy-trivial ownership, and same-owner
    continuity.
- Which theories are constrained?
  - Self-Model Theory, report-heavy theories, and any embodiment-light theory
    that overreads subject-language.
- What overclaims must be blocked?
  - that current negative controls disprove future AI consciousness
  - that current negative controls by themselves prove minimal self

Current result: negative controls are excellent for subtraction and theory
discipline, but they do not by themselves create Level 2 progress.

## 12. Level 2 Evaluation

Level 2 criteria:

- discriminates against at least two neighbor theories
- blocks known proxy collapses
- makes predictions specific enough to fail
- could change claim maturity
- pre-specifies overclaim routes
- does not rely only on report or subtraction

Evaluation:

- Minimal Self
  - strengthened if:
    - body ownership changes while thin mineness plausibly persists
    - agency/authorship changes while thin mineness plausibly persists
    - self-model accounts do not predict the full dissociation pattern
  - weakened if:
    - every ownership dissociation reduces cleanly to body-model, agency, or
      self-model changes
    - thin mineness remains only a remainder label
- Self-Model Theory
  - strengthened if:
    - depersonalization, body-illusion, and agency-dissociation cases map
      cleanly to representational changes without extra remainder
  - weakened if:
    - represented subjecthood repeatedly diverges from ownership-like pressure
      cases and negative controls stay too easy
- Embodiment
  - strengthened if:
    - body-ownership and self-location changes predict the ownership pattern
      better than thin-self language
  - weakened if:
    - body ownership shifts leave central ownership pressure relatively intact
- Agency/control theory
  - strengthened if:
    - agency/authorship disruption explains most ownership variation
  - weakened if:
    - authorship fractures while ownership-like pressure remains

Current result: ownership dissociation reaches the edge of Level 2 planning,
but not full Level 2 achievement yet. It is the strongest current route because
it can produce fail-able contrasts across at least Minimal Self, Self-Model
Theory, and Agency/control theory. What it still lacks is a sharper operational
criterion for thin mineness that does not reduce to "what remains."

## 13. What Would Strengthen Minimal Self?

- body ownership changes while thin mineness persists
- agency/authorship changes while thin mineness persists
- report remains strong while ordinary mineness thins
- self-model accounts fail to predict the dissociation pattern
- negative controls show represented subjecthood without owner evidence
- predictions become specific enough to fail

## 14. What Would Weaken Minimal Self?

- all ownership dissociations reduce cleanly to body-model changes
- all ownership dissociations reduce cleanly to agency/control changes
- all ownership dissociations reduce cleanly to self-model changes
- thin mineness remains only a residual label
- no case produces a discriminating pattern

## 15. What Would Strengthen Rival Theories?

- Self-Model Theory
  - gains if representational changes predict depersonalization, body illusion,
    and agency dissociation better than thin-self language does.
- Embodiment
  - gains if ownership disturbance mostly tracks body-model and self-location
    shifts.
- Agency/control theory
  - gains if authorship and control predict ownership disturbance with little
    leftover structure.
- Valence/stakes account
  - gains if estrangement, intimacy, and bodily stake explain ownership
    disturbance better than body-model or self-model terms alone.

## 16. Main Finding

Ownership dissociation is currently the strongest Level 2 route because it can
pressure multiple ownership channels at once. But it only helps minimal self if
thin for-me-ness can be separated non-circularly from body ownership, agency,
self-modeling, and report.

Otherwise nonunitarity becomes a taxonomy, not bridge progress.

Current conservative result: ownership appears at least partially nonunitary in
the pressure map, but that is not yet the same thing as an operational thin
mineness marker.
