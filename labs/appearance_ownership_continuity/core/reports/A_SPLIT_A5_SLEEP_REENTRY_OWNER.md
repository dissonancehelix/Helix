## 1. Claim Under Pressure

Ordinary sleep and anesthesia re-entry are stronger same-self continuity cases than copying because they preserve non-branching biological and dynamical context, but those channels do not by themselves establish owned continuity.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- waking memory is not owner preservation by itself
- biological continuity is not owner preservation by itself
- non-branching is not sufficient by itself
- sleep is not equivalent to destructive copying
- ordinary re-entry does not solve personal identity

## 2. Definitions From PRIMITIVES.md

- **ownership:** the mark by which an experience, action, body-state, memory, or perspective is mine
- **continuity:** identity-bearing preservation through time
- **transformation:** change absorbed by an ownership-stream while remaining recognizably the same self
- **branching:** a continuity threat where one prior stream yields multiple equally valid successor streams
- **reportability:** availability of information for speech, action, reasoning, or communicable output
- **appearance-from-within:** the A gap between modeled/reported/integrated activity and something it is like from within

Working local definitions:

- **re-entry:** return into waking access, responsiveness, report, memory, body-control, or ordinary self-model after interruption
- **owner preservation:** continuation of the same owned stream, not just restoration of memory, behavior, or report

## 3. Prior Pressure Anchors

The branching report showed that equal successors break the shortcut from shared memory to one continuing owner. When two successors inherit the same memory and pattern, memory cannot uniquely identify the original owner.

The memory-residue report showed that memory, report, narrative, and pattern are support channels or residues, not same-self evidence by themselves.

The DREAM report showed that waking report is residue after sleep, not direct access to the dream field.

The propofol report showed that dose, task timing, motor response, and recovery windows are separable traces, not direct consciousness or ownership markers.

This report asks why ordinary waking from sleep or anesthesia is still stronger than copying despite those cautions.

## 5. Why Re-entry Is Stronger Than Copying

Ordinary sleep and anesthesia re-entry are stronger than copying because they preserve several channels that copying either breaks or only simulates:

- one organism rather than two successor claimants
- continuous biological substrate rather than reconstructed pattern alone
- ongoing dynamical process rather than fresh instantiation from stored information
- no equal successor symmetry
- ordinary social and practical identity continuity
- body-control and world-relation returning through the same system

These channels explain why waking is not absurdly equivalent to destructive copying. Sleep does not create two equal successors, does not replace the whole organism with a separate pattern duplicate, and does not require a new claimant to inherit identity from the outside.

But these channels still do not directly expose owned continuity. They justify pragmatic preservation, not theoretical closure.

## 6. Why Re-entry Is Still Underdetermined

The A4 reports prevent overconfidence:

- DREAM shows that report after waking is residue, not the original dream field.
- Propofol shows that recovery timing and motor response are channels, not ownership markers.
- Memory-residue pressure shows that later autobiographical continuity does not by itself show the same owner crossed the gap.
- Branching pressure shows that non-branching avoids successor symmetry but does not define ownership.

The result is a two-level status:

| Level | Status |
| --- | --- |
| practical life | ordinary sleep/anesthesia re-entry counts as preserved identity |
| AOC/OCH theory | owner preservation remains underdetermined until an operational ownership-continuity marker exists |

## 7. Non-Branching In Re-entry

Non-branching matters because it blocks the successor symmetry problem. If the sleeper wakes as one organism with one ongoing process, there is no equal rival successor asking the same identity question.

Still:

- non-branching is not sufficient
- a single reconstructed copy can also be non-branching in the local successor count
- ordinary re-entry gains strength from non-branching plus biological/dynamical continuity, not non-branching alone
- AOC/OCH still needs a marker for owned continuity rather than just absence of branching

Best current status:

> Non-branching plus biological/dynamical continuity is a strong re-entry package, not an ownership proof.

## 10. Main Finding

Ordinary sleep/anesthesia re-entry is stronger than copying because it preserves non-branching biological/dynamical context, but those channels still do not by themselves establish owned continuity. AOC/OCH should treat ordinary re-entry as pragmatically preserved but theoretically underdetermined until an operational ownership-continuity marker is defined.

The strongest distinction from copying is not memory. It is the combined absence of branch symmetry, preservation of one body/process, and return of control/report through the same ongoing system.
