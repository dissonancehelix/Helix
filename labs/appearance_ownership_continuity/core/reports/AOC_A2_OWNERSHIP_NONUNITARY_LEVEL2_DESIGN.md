## 1. Purpose

The ownership nonunitary retest found that ownership appears provisionally
nonunitary but did not reach Level 2. This report designs the specific fail-able
contrasts needed to convert ownership dissociation from taxonomy into
discrimination.

Nonunitarity alone is not progress unless it discriminates theories. Thin
mineness cannot be defined merely as residue. The goal is to design tests that
could strengthen or weaken Minimal Self, Self-Model Theory, Embodiment,
Agency/control, and Valence/stakes accounts.

## 2. Level 2 Criteria

A Level 2 ownership-discrimination path must:

- discriminate against at least two neighbor theories
- block body ownership, agency, report, self-model, and valence shortcuts
- produce predictions specific enough to fail
- identify what would weaken Minimal Self
- identify what would strengthen rival theories
- change claim maturity only if those conditions are met
- avoid defining thin mineness as whatever remains

## 5. Operational Thin-Mineness Problem

Core obstacle:

Thin mineness cannot be:

- body ownership
- body location
- agency/authorship
- control fluency
- self-modeling
- first-person report
- narrative continuity
- valence alone
- field unity alone

What positive operational handle could remain?

### 1. Dissociation stability across body and agency disruptions

- What it tests:
  - whether a thin owner-like channel remains more stable than body ownership
    and agency under perturbation
- What would strengthen it:
  - the same thin-mineness pattern appears across rubber-hand and alien-limb
    style pressure
- What would weaken it:
  - the pattern tracks body-model or authorship changes one-to-one
- Circularity risk:
  - the stable remainder is simply renamed "thin mineness"

### 2. Altered-mineness reports with preserved perception/report and predictable structure

- What it tests:
  - whether estrangement reports in depersonalization have a structured pattern
    that is not exhausted by self-description or report fluency
- What would strengthen it:
  - estrangement covaries with ownership-like pressure more than with generic
    report richness
- What would weaken it:
  - all structure is captured by self-model disturbance and affect report alone
- Circularity risk:
  - reports of estrangement are taken as direct access to mineness

### 3. Mismatch pattern among ownership judgment, agency, body location, and affective estrangement

- What it tests:
  - whether a patterned mismatch map emerges instead of one bundled ownership
    channel
- What would strengthen it:
  - repeated cross-case semi-independence rather than one-off dissociations
- What would weaken it:
  - mismatch patterns collapse into one dominant channel
- Circularity risk:
  - the mismatch map becomes mere taxonomy

### 4. Negative-control subtraction paired with positive human dissociation pattern

- What it tests:
  - whether self-model-like structure can be subtracted away while a richer
    human ownership pattern still needs explanation
- What would strengthen it:
  - LLM/persona controls remove weak self-model evidence, while human
    dissociation cases still force a target beyond report/persona
- What would weaken it:
  - human cases look no more structured than the negative controls
- Circularity risk:
  - subtraction is mistaken for a positive mechanism

### 5. Cross-case invariance of thin for-me-ness under different ownership perturbations

- What it tests:
  - whether a recurring thin owner-like pattern shows up across
    depersonalization, body illusion, and agency dissociation
- What would strengthen it:
  - convergent invariance with theory-specific failure conditions
- What would weaken it:
  - no recurring pattern survives across cases
- Circularity risk:
  - invariance is asserted at too high a level of abstraction

## 6. Proposed Level 2 Contrast Families

### 1. Body ownership shift without thin-mineness shift

- Cases used:
  - rubber-hand illusion
  - full-body/body-swap variants
- Target ambiguity:
  - body ownership vs thin mineness
- Minimal Self prediction:
  - local or global body-ownership shift can occur while thin mineness remains
    more stable
- Self-Model prediction:
  - ownership disturbance tracks representational remapping with no extra lane
- Embodiment prediction:
  - body-model and self-location shifts dominate the ownership pattern
- Agency/control prediction:
  - weaker role here unless agency manipulation is present
- Valence/stakes prediction:
  - affect may modulate salience but not define the core pattern
- What result favors each:
  - Minimal Self: ownership judgment and body remapping shift while a thinner
    owner-like pattern does not move in lockstep
  - Self-Model/Embodiment: all salient changes follow body/self-model
    disturbance cleanly
- What result leaves all unresolved:
  - mixed results remain mostly report-level
- Forbidden overclaim:
  - body ownership does not equal all ownership

### 2. Agency/authorship fracture without thin-mineness collapse

- Cases used:
  - alien limb
  - anarchic hand
  - agency-dissociation pressure
- Target ambiguity:
  - authorship vs ownership
- Minimal Self prediction:
  - agency fractures while thin for-me-ness or perspective-presence persists
- Self-Model prediction:
  - altered agency-model explains the whole disturbance
- Embodiment prediction:
  - disruption may still reduce to sensorimotor control structure
- Agency/control prediction:
  - ownership disturbance should track authorship/control breakdown strongly
- Valence/stakes prediction:
  - estrangement may modulate the experience but is not central
- What result favors each:
  - Minimal Self: ownership-like pressure survives agency fracture
  - Agency/control: ownership disturbance is exhausted by authorship loss
  - Self-Model: model-level disturbance predicts the entire profile
- What result leaves all unresolved:
  - reports are too mixed to isolate a non-agency ownership lane
- Forbidden overclaim:
  - agency loss does not by itself expose a minimal-self primitive

### 3. Report preserved while ordinary mineness thins

- Cases used:
  - depersonalization/derealization
- Target ambiguity:
  - reportability vs mineness
- Minimal Self prediction:
  - ordinary mineness can thin or estrange while report remains strong
- Self-Model prediction:
  - representational self-world distortion explains the pattern
- Embodiment prediction:
  - bodily estrangement should track embodiment disturbance
- Agency/control prediction:
  - weaker unless agency disruption is central
- Valence/stakes prediction:
  - affective estrangement may explain much of the pattern
- What result favors each:
  - Minimal Self: a structured altered-mineness channel not reducible to report
    richness or body/agency shifts
  - Self-Model: report plus model alteration explain the syndrome cleanly
  - Valence: estrangement tracks affective flattening or aversive mattering
    more than ownership structure
- What result leaves all unresolved:
  - clinical narrative remains too interpretive
- Forbidden overclaim:
  - altered ownership reports are not transparent access to original structure

### 4. Self-model/persona present without owner evidence

- Cases used:
  - LLM self-report
  - persona continuity
  - forked sessions
- Target ambiguity:
  - represented subjecthood vs lived ownership
- Minimal Self prediction:
  - self-description can be present while owner evidence remains absent
- Self-Model prediction:
  - shallow self-model evidence fails, but richer self-model criteria may still
    survive
- Embodiment prediction:
  - lack of embodied coupling blocks owner evidence
- Agency/control prediction:
  - output fluency is not enough
- Valence/stakes prediction:
  - weak unless richer affective stake is present
- What result favors each:
  - Minimal Self: strong subtraction of report/persona from ownership
  - Self-Model: negative controls only defeat weak versions, not richer
    representational accounts
- What result leaves all unresolved:
  - no positive ownership pattern is identified on the human side
- Forbidden overclaim:
  - negative controls do not disprove future AI consciousness

### 5. Valence/estrangement change without body/agency change

- Cases used:
  - depersonalization/derealization
  - pain or affective bodily states
- Target ambiguity:
  - valence/stakes vs ownership
- Minimal Self prediction:
  - valence modulates but does not exhaust thin mineness
- Self-Model prediction:
  - altered affect follows self/world model changes
- Embodiment prediction:
  - bodily stake may dominate the pattern
- Agency/control prediction:
  - should matter little if body/agency stay relatively stable
- Valence/stakes prediction:
  - affective estrangement or mattering explains the ownership shift best
- What result favors each:
  - Valence/stakes: affective shift predicts ownership texture better than body
    or agency channels
  - Minimal Self: valence changes without fully determining the owner-like lane
- What result leaves all unresolved:
  - valence and model changes move together everywhere
- Forbidden overclaim:
  - valence does not explain appearance by itself

### 6. Field/appearance preserved while ownership texture changes

- Cases used:
  - depersonalization/derealization
  - alien-limb style dissociation
  - body-illusion pressure
- Target ambiguity:
  - fielded appearance vs ownership texture
- Minimal Self prediction:
  - field can stay present while ownership texture thins or shifts
- Self-Model prediction:
  - ownership texture tracks representational changes in the subject model
- Embodiment prediction:
  - field/ownership split is largely a body-model split
- Agency/control prediction:
  - field remains but ownership changes should follow control changes
- Valence/stakes prediction:
  - field remains while affective intimacy changes
- What result favors each:
  - Minimal Self: field-preservation plus patterned ownership change not
    exhausted by model/body/agency explanations
- What result leaves all unresolved:
  - field and ownership texture remain too coarsely described
- Forbidden overclaim:
  - preserved field does not prove a distinct thin-mineness mechanism

## 11. Main Finding

Ownership nonunitarity is ready for Level 2 design but not Level 2 promotion.
The best next work is not another broad survey; it is a set of fail-able
contrast families that would either strengthen Minimal Self as a thin-mineness
target or show that rival self-model/body/agency/valence accounts explain the
dissociations without remainder.
