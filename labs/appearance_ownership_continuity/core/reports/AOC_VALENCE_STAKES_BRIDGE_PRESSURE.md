## 1. Purpose

The ownership nonunitarity patch formalized a provisional A2 structural claim: ownership appears partially nonunitary across depersonalization, rubber-hand/body-illusion, and agency/authorship dissociation pressure.

This report asks whether valence, homeostasis, affective intimacy, bodily need, aversion, reward, and stakes provide a deeper ownership/appearance pressure route, or whether they are another neighboring proxy that must be blocked.

Valence is not treated as A0. Stakes are not treated as appearance. Mattering is not allowed to become a magic bridge word. The goal is to determine whether valence adds discriminating pressure that cannot be reduced to report, self-modeling, embodiment, agency/control, arousal, or task relevance.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> Valence / stakes may provide an affective-intimacy lane within ownership: some experiences feel more or less mine, alien, urgent, aversive, pleasant, meaningful, or bodily intimate because they carry organism-relative mattering.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- valence is not identical to A0
- valence does not by itself explain appearance-from-within
- pain or pleasure is not automatically a bridge
- affective intensity is not ownership
- arousal is not valence
- reward is not valence by itself
- homeostatic regulation is not lived mattering by itself
- depersonalization emotional numbing is not transparent access to mineness structure

## 5. Core Discrimination Question

Is valence/stakes best explained as:

A. affective intimacy within minimal self / mineness  
B. homeostatic or active-inference regulation  
C. self-model evaluation of bodily/world state  
D. embodiment and interoceptive coupling  
E. a neighboring proxy that tracks importance without explaining appearance

### A. Affective intimacy within minimal self

- Predicts: mineness can thicken, thin, alienate, or become urgent as felt stakes change.
- Strengthens if: depersonalization-like emotional numbing or bodily need changes ownership texture beyond report, self-model, and control.
- Weakens if: all apparent mattering reduces to arousal, reward, self-evaluation, or body-model update.
- Unresolved if: affect reports are rich but not separable from other channels.

### B. Homeostatic / active-inference regulation

- Predicts: valence reflects organism-relative regulation, prediction, and control of bodily needs.
- Strengthens if: interoceptive need, threat, or control precision predicts affective ownership patterns better than minimal-self language.
- Weakens if: lived alienation and felt intimacy vary without corresponding regulatory changes.
- Unresolved if: regulation and lived affect remain coupled but not discriminated.

### C. Self-model evaluation

- Predicts: valence changes are represented self/world evaluations.
- Strengthens if: reports of alienation, distress, and emotional numbing map cleanly to self-model content.
- Weakens if: affective intimacy shifts in ways not captured by explicit self-description.
- Unresolved if: self-model explanations absorb every case too broadly.

### D. Embodiment / interoception

- Predicts: affective mattering is rooted in body-world and interoceptive coupling.
- Strengthens if: bodily need and interoceptive disruption explain mineness shifts better than report or self-modeling.
- Weakens if: affective intimacy changes while embodiment markers remain stable.
- Unresolved if: embodiment is necessary but not sufficient.

### E. Neighboring proxy

- Predicts: valence tracks priority/importance but does not bridge appearance or ownership.
- Strengthens if: affect predicts behavior/report while failing to discriminate mineness from self-model, body, or agency channels.
- Weakens if: valence yields fail-able contrasts that rival theories cannot absorb.
- Unresolved if: valence remains indispensable but underoperationalized.

## 6. Valence vs Minimal Self

Valence may deepen minimal self by giving thin mineness a gradient: experience is not merely present-for-me, but can feel intimate, estranged, urgent, aversive, numbed, or meaningful. This is especially relevant to depersonalization/derealization, where perception and report may persist while affective intimacy and ordinary self-world belonging change.

What favors Minimal Self plus valence:

- altered mineness tracks affective intimacy rather than explicit self-description alone
- emotional numbing changes ownership texture without eliminating perception/report
- aversive estrangement produces a distinctive ownership profile not reducible to arousal or anxiety
- bodily need states intensify for-me-ness in a way not captured by report alone

What weakens Minimal Self plus valence:

- valence effects reduce to self-model evaluation
- valence effects reduce to interoceptive/body regulation
- valence effects reduce to reward/arousal/attention
- mineness remains a residual label after affect is described

Current result: valence is a serious candidate ingredient for ownership texture, but it does not rescue minimal self from the need for a positive operational handle.

## 7. Valence vs Self-Model Theory

Self-Model Theory can explain many affective reports as represented self-world evaluations. That makes it mechanically attractive. The pressure question is whether affective intimacy has a lived structure not exhausted by modeled evaluation.

What favors Self-Model Theory:

- depersonalization affect changes map cleanly onto self/world model content
- emotional numbing, distress, and alienation are predicted by altered self-representation
- no stable affective-mineness pattern remains outside model content

What weakens Self-Model Theory:

- affective intimacy changes while explicit self-description remains stable
- bodily need or pain changes ownership texture prior to reflective evaluation
- modeled emotion labels appear without owner evidence in negative controls

Current result: self-model accounts remain strong, but LLM/persona negative controls continue to block the shortcut from represented emotion to lived stakes.

## 8. Valence vs Embodiment / Homeostasis

Embodiment and homeostasis may be the strongest rival explanations. Pain, hunger, breathlessness, nausea, panic, fatigue, pleasure, and threat are not merely conceptual self-model states. They are organism-relative body states with action pressure and felt priority.

What favors embodiment/homeostasis:

- interoceptive need predicts urgency and ownership texture better than self-report alone
- bodily states alter felt intimacy without requiring explicit self-reflection
- affective disturbance tracks body-world regulation more than narrative self-description

What weakens embodiment/homeostasis:

- affective estrangement occurs without clear bodily/interoceptive change
- body regulation explains action but not felt intimacy
- regulation remains marker-level rather than bridge-level

Current result: valence/stakes likely needs an embodiment/homeostasis component. But regulation alone is not lived mattering unless the framework can explain why regulatory state is present from within.

## 9. Depersonalization / Emotional Numbing Pressure

Depersonalization remains the strongest valence-adjacent case already in the AOC arc.

Pressure shape:

- perception can persist
- report can persist
- agency may persist
- emotional intimacy can flatten
- self/world can feel unreal or estranged
- distress may increase in response to estrangement

This pressures three reductions at once:

1. report-first: report is preserved but ordinary mineness changes
2. self-model-only: self-world evaluation may change, but affective intimacy may be doing independent work
3. valence-only: affective flattening matters, but does not fully explain appearance, perception, or ownership channels

Current result: emotional numbing makes valence a live rival to minimal-self and self-model interpretations, but still report-heavy.

## 10. Pain / Pleasure / Bodily Need Pressure

Pain and bodily need are high-value pressure cases because they make mattering difficult to treat as decorative. Pain is not merely content; it usually carries aversive force, bodily localization, urgency, and a strong relation to the subject.

But pain is also dangerous as a proxy:

- pain is not all appearance
- pain intensity is not ownership
- nociception is not pain
- pain report is not pain itself
- suffering risk is not A0 closure

Pleasure and reward create the mirror problem:

- reward can guide behavior without establishing lived pleasure
- preference can be modeled without owner evidence
- positive valence may thicken intimacy but not explain appearance itself

Current result: pain/pleasure/bodily need should become future pressure sources, but not immediate ledger claims.

## 11. LLM / Synthetic Negative-Control Pressure

Current LLMs can produce:

- emotion labels
- preference statements
- distress-like language
- self-protective rhetoric
- continuity/persona narratives
- first-person affect reports

AOC guardrails block treating any of these as lived valence evidence by themselves.

What the negative control subtracts:

- reportability
- self-description
- apparent preference
- persona continuity
- simulated stakes-language

What it cannot subtract:

- future artificial systems with stronger ownership, continuity, boundary, and valence evidence
- non-linguistic or embodied artificial affect architectures

Current result: synthetic negative controls are now the best next route for preventing valence-language from being overcredited.

## 13. Level 2 Evaluation

Level 2 criteria:

- discriminates against at least two neighbor theories
- blocks report, self-model, embodiment, reward, arousal, and control shortcuts
- makes predictions specific enough to fail
- could change claim maturity
- pre-specifies overclaim routes
- does not rely only on report or subtraction

Candidate evaluation:

- Minimal Self: gains if affective intimacy changes predict mineness texture beyond self-model and embodiment. Weakens if valence absorbs the remaining thin-mineness lane.
- Self-Model Theory: gains if affective reports map cleanly to self/world model changes. Weakens if lived stakes shift independently of model content.
- Embodiment/Homeostasis: gains if interoceptive regulation predicts ownership texture better than self-report or self-model. Weakens if body-state accounts explain action but not intimacy.
- Valence/Stakes: gains as a bridge ingredient if it discriminates affective intimacy from arousal, reward, report, and self-modeling. Weakens if it becomes a synonym for importance.
- No-Bridge restraint: remains useful only as a brake, not a positive result.

Current result: valence/stakes produces strong pressure but not full Level 2 bridge progress. It is important enough to prioritize, but not ready as a standalone ledger claim.

## 14. What Would Strengthen Valence/Stakes As A Bridge Ingredient?

- affective intimacy predicts ownership texture better than report or self-modeling
- emotional numbing and aversive estrangement separate cleanly from ordinary anxiety or arousal
- pain/bodily need states show structured ownership pressure not reducible to behavior or report
- reward/preference negative controls fail to mimic lived stakes evidence
- valence yields predictions against at least two rival theories
- valence can be separated from arousal, attention, task relevance, and action tendency

## 15. What Would Weaken Valence/Stakes As A Bridge Ingredient?

- valence effects reduce to self-model content
- valence effects reduce to interoceptive/body regulation with no lived-stakes handle
- valence effects reduce to arousal, reward, or attention
- affect reports remain the only evidence
- LLM/synthetic controls reproduce all relevant markers without owner evidence
- valence becomes a poetic label for importance

## 16. Candidate Claim Decision

Recommended status for a possible future `VALENCE_STAKES_NOT_A0` or `VALENCE_STAKES_AS_OWNERSHIP_TEXTURE` claim:

- not ready as a positive bridge claim
- useful as a future guardrail or pressure target

Reason:

Valence appears important enough that it should not be treated as decorative. It may explain affective intimacy, alienation, urgency, pain, pleasure, and felt mattering better than purely report-first or field-first accounts. But it is still too easy to collapse valence into arousal, reward, self-modeling, interoceptive regulation, or report.

## 17. Main Finding

Valence/stakes is a serious ownership-texture and bridge-ingredient candidate, especially for depersonalization, emotional numbing, pain, bodily need, and affective intimacy. It does not currently close A0 and does not by itself operationalize thin mineness.

Its strongest role is as the next pressure axis: it tests whether ownership nonunitarity is merely structural, or whether ownership texture includes an affective intimacy / mattering channel.
