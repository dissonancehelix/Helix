## 1. Claim Under Pressure

Temporal flow / minimal self-presence may be a stronger continuity candidate than memory, report, narrative, persona, or copied pattern, but it still does not solve raw appearance-from-within.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- temporal flow is not established as the owner-marker
- minimal self is not established as A0
- flow state is not absence of self
- time coding is not lived duration
- AOC does not solve continuity through A3 alone

## 2. Definitions

- **temporal flow:** lived succession across moments, not merely ordered information or clock-time processing
- **minimal self:** thin first-personal presence beneath narrative identity, explicit reflection, or autobiographical report
- **pre-reflective selfhood:** self-presence built into experience before the subject thinks about itself as a subject
- **reflective self-awareness:** explicit self-directed thought, report, evaluation, or narrative self-modeling
- **memory residue:** stored, remembered, or reportable trace left after experience or transition
- **report residue:** communicable output left after a state, not the state itself
- **owned continuity:** selfhood as a continuity-bearing ownership-stream inside a conscious field
- **lived duration:** temporality as experienced from within, not just represented sequence
- **temporal coding:** computational, neural, behavioral, or symbolic tracking of order, interval, rhythm, or prediction
- **appearance-from-within:** the open A gap between modeled/reported/integrated activity and there being something it is like from within

## 3. Prior Anchor Summary

- **A2 ownership absent:** ownership is not reducible to report, body ownership, agency, self-modeling, or reflective self-awareness.
- **A5 memory:** memory is support/residue, not same-owner evidence by itself.
- **A5 branching:** copied memory cannot choose one owner when two successors inherit the same pattern.
- **A5 sleep/re-entry:** ordinary waking is stronger than copying because it preserves non-branching biological/dynamical context, but it is not owner-proof.
- **A5 upload/copy:** a copied self-story does not show that the owner transferred.

A3 is now the right seam because memory, report, pattern, persona, and narrative are too late, too public, or too copyable. Temporal flow may be closer to the active continuity process because it concerns the living passage from moment to moment rather than residue after the fact.

The pressure question is whether this helps OCH without becoming circular: if temporal flow means "the owner continuing," then it only renames A5. If it can be operationalized as a distinct channel, it may become a stronger continuity candidate.

## 5. Temporal Flow vs Memory

Memory is after-the-fact residue. It can represent sequence, reconstruct continuity, and support orientation, but it arrives as trace or report. A5 showed why that is not enough: memory can be degraded, copied, branched, restored, or narrated without selecting one continuing owner.

Temporal flow is a better candidate because it concerns active lived succession rather than stored evidence. If ownership continuity is carried anywhere, the best current candidate is not the memory of crossing but the crossing as lived through.

That advantage is not closure. Temporal flow can still fail as owner evidence if it is only inferred from behavior, reconstructed from report, or defined in terms of ownership from the start. A3 therefore relates to A5 as a candidate carrier rather than a closed answer: A5 asks whether the same owner survived; A3 asks whether lived succession is the channel through which such survival would have to occur.

## 6. Flow State Pressure

Flow is useful because reflective self-awareness can drop while skilled temporal continuity remains strong. The person may not be narrating themselves, evaluating themselves, or explicitly thinking "I am doing this," yet action, perception, anticipation, and task-world coupling remain highly organized.

Pressure questions:

- **Does flow weaken reflective self but preserve minimal self?** Yes as a plausible interpretation: reflective self-talk may recede while pre-reflective task ownership remains.
- **Does reduced self-referential thought imply absent ownership?** No. A2 already blocks the shortcut from reflection to mineness.
- **Does absorbed action show continuity without narrative self?** It supports that possibility. Flow can preserve temporal skill and agency without heavy narrative self-modeling.
- **What remains inaccessible?** Whether the lived stream is owned in a way that can be operationally separated from smooth action, report, and retrospective description.

Flow therefore pressures HOT and narrative-self shortcuts more than it pressures raw A0. It suggests reflective self-awareness is not the continuity carrier by itself.

## 7. Minimal Self Pressure

Minimal/pre-reflective selfhood is the strongest A3 neighbor because it points to a thin "for-me" structure beneath narrative, report, and explicit self-modeling.

Pressure questions:

- **Is there a thin for-me structure beneath narrative/report?** A2 and flow cases make this plausible, especially where reflection drops but experience and action remain organized.
- **Can A2 ownership and A3 flow be separated?** Partly. Depersonalization pressures A2 mineness while temporal passage may remain; anesthesia and dreamless sleep pressure A3 continuity while ordinary selfhood later resumes.
- **Does minimal self explain continuity or only describe it?** It may describe the phenomenological form of continuity without giving a mechanism or marker.
- **Can minimal self be operationalized without circularity?** Not yet. The risk is treating "minimal self" as whatever is left when report, memory, agency, and narrative are removed.

Current status: minimal self is a candidate bridge between A2 ownership and A3 flow, not an explanation of A0 and not a complete A5 identity criterion.

## 10. Main Finding

A3 temporal flow is a stronger candidate for continuity than memory/report/pattern because it concerns active lived succession rather than residue or copyable structure. But temporal flow still does not solve A0, does not automatically establish ownership, and risks circularity unless linked to operational markers.

The strongest positive result is that A3 identifies a better place to look: the ongoing passage of experience/action before it becomes report or memory. The strongest negative result is that the same place is hard to test without smuggling in the very ownership-continuity it is supposed to explain.
