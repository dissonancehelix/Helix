## 1. Purpose

This report tests whether Oparin-Haldane / origin-of-life thinking can help AOC conceptually.

The goal is not to import origin-of-life theory as a consciousness solution. The goal is to ask whether the transition from nonlife to life offers useful structural analogies for the transition from processing to appearance-from-within or from field to owned field.

The strongest candidate analogy is not `primordial soup = consciousness soup`. The useful analogy is transition architecture:

```text
chemistry -> bounded protocell -> metabolism / self-maintenance -> heredity -> evolution
processing -> bounded field -> stakes / self-maintenance -> ownership -> continuity
```

This analogy is pressure, not proof.

## 2. Background Compression

Oparin-Haldane theory, in broad historical form, proposed that life could emerge gradually from prebiotic chemistry through chemical evolution: simple molecules, energy sources, increasingly complex organic compounds, protocell-like bounded systems, and eventually self-maintaining/evolving life.

For AOC, the important structural motif is not the specific early-Earth chemistry. The important motif is that `life` may not require one magic ingredient appearing all at once. It may require a threshold organization of several individually nonliving processes:

- boundary / compartment
- metabolism / self-maintenance
- energy gradient
- internal regulation
- persistence through perturbation
- replication / heredity
- variation and selection

AOC can use this as a conceptual comparison for consciousness without claiming the same mechanism.

## 4. What This Helps

The origin-of-life analogy helps AOC in four ways.

### 4.1 It suggests transition thresholds, not magic switches

Life was not necessarily a single substance added to chemistry. It may have been a regime transition where boundary, energy, persistence, and replication became mutually reinforcing.

AOC analogue:

Appearance/ownership may not be one local marker. It may require a threshold organization where field, boundary, temporal continuity, stakes, self-maintenance, and ownership channels become mutually constrained.

This strengthens the methodological value of A-Split: do not ask one marker to do all the work.

### 4.2 It centers boundary as more than container

A protocell boundary is not decoration. It creates an inside where chemistry can become locally organized and self-maintaining.

AOC analogue:

Boundary may be necessary for ownership because without inside/outside distinction there is no stable candidate for `mine`, `not mine`, `self`, `world`, or continuity through perturbation.

Guardrail:

`FIELD_NOT_OWNER` and `FIELD_MARKERS_NOT_OWNER` still apply. Boundary can scaffold owner-structure without being ownership.

### 4.3 It links stakes to self-maintenance

Living systems matter to themselves functionally because they must maintain organization against entropy, damage, deprivation, and environmental perturbation.

AOC analogue:

Pain, hunger, breathlessness, fatigue, nausea, threat, relief, and bodily need may be ownership-texture pressure cases because they involve organism-relative stakes.

Guardrail:

Homeostasis is not lived mattering by itself. Stakes do not solve A0.

### 4.4 It supports an incremental bridge-search strategy

Origin-of-life research often evaluates partial systems: membranes without heredity, metabolism without genes, replication without cells, etc.

AOC analogue:

AOC should continue evaluating partial systems:

- report without owner
- self-model without A0
- field unity without mineness
- agency without ownership
- body ownership without thin mineness
- valence language without lived stakes
- nociception without pain
- memory pattern without owner continuity

This is exactly the current pressure-report workflow.

## 5. What This Does Not Help

The analogy does not explain:

- why anything appears from within
- why boundary plus regulation becomes experience
- why an owner exists rather than a self-maintaining process
- why valence is felt rather than merely used for control
- why a living cell is not necessarily conscious
- how to identify A0 directly

The biggest false positive is:

```text
life has boundary and self-maintenance
consciousness likely involves boundary and self-maintenance
therefore life explains consciousness
```

That is blocked.

## 7. AOC Payoff: Proto-Ownership Template

The most useful conceptual object is a `proto-ownership template`:

```text
bounded field
+ internally maintained state
+ perturbation-sensitive persistence
+ organism-relative stakes
+ temporal continuity
+ self/world boundary
+ non-branching trajectory
= candidate scaffold for ownership pressure
```

This is not a claim that the scaffold is conscious. It is a search template for where ownership-like structure may become plausible enough to test.

## 8. Relation To DCP / LIP / EIP

Origin-of-life thinking also fits the DCP/LIP/EIP arc.

- LIP: local state is insufficient before commitment; early systems face incomplete information and environmental uncertainty.
- DCP: commitments compress possibility into actual trajectory; boundary formation and metabolic closure reduce open chemistry into maintained organization.
- EIP: after commitment, the prior open state cannot be fully restored; lineage and residue appear.

This suggests a useful cross-domain analogy:

```text
life begins when chemistry becomes committed to self-maintaining trajectory
selfhood begins when appearance becomes owned continuity under irreversible time
```

This is only an analogy. It should not be promoted without empirical pressure.

## 10. Main Finding

Oparin-Haldane / origin-of-life thinking is conceptually useful for AOC because it models how a major category transition can depend on a bundle of partial precursors rather than one magic marker.

The best import is structural:

```text
bounded self-maintaining systems can create a new regime without any one component being sufficient alone
```

For AOC, this supports a scaffold view of ownership:

```text
boundary + self-maintenance + stakes + temporal persistence may be necessary scaffolding for owned continuity
```

But the analogy does not solve A0, does not prove valence, and does not prove ownership. It mainly sharpens the next test: boundary/self-maintenance/interoception should be pressured after pain and nociception guardrails.
