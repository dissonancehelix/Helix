## 1. Claim Under Pressure

Claim being tested:

> Interruption and re-entry can dissociate appearance, reportability, responsiveness, access, memory residue, and owned continuity.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- Delayed dream report is not direct access to the dream field.
- Imagery-task success is not raw A.
- Responsiveness is not identical to consciousness.
- Unresponsiveness is not identical to unconsciousness.
- Neural markers do not automatically establish owned experience.

This report tests whether A4 needs its own layer. It does not decide A0, and it does not promote any empirical marker into an owner.

## 2. Definitions From PRIMITIVES.md

- **Appearance-from-within:** the A gap between modeling/reporting/integrating/acting and there being something it is like from within.
- **Reportability:** availability of information for speech, action, reasoning, or communicable output.
- **Continuity:** identity-bearing preservation through time.
- **Ownership:** the mark by which an experience, action, body-state, memory, or perspective is mine.
- **Field:** an organized space of possible appearances, states, or relations that can support unified presentation.
- **Integration:** differentiated states bound into a coherent field or system.
- **Agency/control coupling:** a link between perspective, possible action, prediction, and consequence.
- **Interruption:** a disruption, blocking, or suspension of report, responsiveness, wakeful access, recall, field stability, or ordinary continuity.
- **Re-entry:** return into report, responsiveness, access, memory, control, or apparent continuity after interruption.
- **Residue:** what remains after the state: report, memory trace, behavioral trace, neural trace, timing marker, or altered future interpretation.

## 4. DREAM Pressure

DREAM is most useful as a residue machine. It turns private sleep mentation into labels after awakening: `Experience`, `No experience`, `Without recall`, `No experience or without recall`, and `Unknown`. The local pilot found 3,191 records and a usable metadata join across 20 Set IDs, with Tononi Serial Awakenings, Multiple awakenings, Zhang & Wamsley 2019, Oudiette_N1Data, and LODE as plausible next inspection targets.

DREAM strengthens report-insufficiency because its own categories prevent a clean collapse of experience into report. `Without recall` is not useless; it is exactly the category that says report can fail while the status of experience remains underdetermined. `No experience` also cannot be read as transparent absence, because the report arrives after sleep, filtered through memory, awakening, language, and study protocol.

DREAM pressures GNWT/HOT-style access/report accounts by making report timing and report content visible as residues. If access is defined only by later report, DREAM pushes the question backward: what was present before the report existed? If reflective report is treated as the target, DREAM leaves sleep mentation, no recall, and awakening transition underdescribed.

DREAM supports AOC's separation of A4 from A0/A2 because interruption/re-entry can be studied without deciding raw appearance or mineness. The useful A4 object is the transition: sleep state -> awakening -> label/report. A0 remains open; A2 remains mostly indirect.

To strengthen continuity claims, DREAM would need more than labels. It would need within-subject repeated awakenings, sleep-stage timing, report content structure, memory/recall reliability, and ideally signal features that track re-entry or mentation categories without treating the signal as the experience itself.

## 5. Propofol / ds006623 Pressure

ds006623 is the strongest local anesthesia pressure source because derivative/control files are already present without the heavy imaging payload. Local material includes participant/control metadata, LOR/ROR timing, propofol traces, squeeze-force traces, and instruction timing. That makes it suitable for a derivative-only interruption/re-entry timeline before any imaging analysis.

Propofol pressures the shortcut `responsiveness = consciousness` by staging graded loss/recovery. A subject can lose ordinary responsiveness without that alone deciding absence of appearance; a subject can recover task response without that alone deciding full owned consciousness. The dataset's value is the dissociation terrain: dose, loss/recovery timing, imagery tasks, hand squeeze, and trace changes can be compared without pretending any one channel is the target.

Covert task evidence supports report-insufficiency only cautiously. Imagery-task or hand-squeeze response can show that ordinary verbal report and overt behavior are not the whole space of cognition. It still does not identify raw A, ownership, or same-self continuity by itself.

The most useful re-entry object is a timeline: propofol concentration and LOR/ROR windows, then instruction periods, force traces, and recovery. That timeline can define candidate interruption/re-entry windows for later comparison against imaging or connectivity measures.

What cannot be inferred: raw A, ownership/mineness, field unity, or selfhood. Task success and responsiveness are pressure markers, not the thing itself.

## 6. No-Report Paradigm Pressure

No-report paradigms protect against the easy mistake of defining consciousness as what can be said, button-pressed, or globally reported. They are useful because they remove or minimize one output channel and ask whether other markers still distinguish conscious-like from non-conscious-like conditions.

They still assume that proxy markers can be meaningfully interpreted. That is the danger. Eye movement, neural pattern, physiology, timing, or task behavior may track access, attention, motor preparation, expectation, or arousal rather than appearance.

AOC should use no-report paradigms as subtraction tools, not closure tools. They can weaken report-first shortcuts and clarify A4/A0 boundaries, but they should not be used to say that proxy marker equals appearance. Their best role is investigative: remove report as an easy substitute and see what distinctions remain.

## 7. ds004902 Context

ds004902 is arousal/vigilance context only in this pass. The local material is top-level metadata: participant table, dataset description, participant JSON, and README. No raw EEG was downloaded.

Useful pressure:

- fatigue and sleep pressure,
- vigilance degradation,
- report/control reliability,
- homeostatic context,
- indirect temporal stability pressure.

Clear limits:

- ds004902 is not a consciousness-loss dataset.
- Sleep deprivation does not equal interruption/re-entry in the strong A4 sense.
- Arousal, fatigue, and vigilance are constraints around report/control, not raw A.

Its next use should remain small: one subject, one normal-sleep condition, one sleep-deprivation condition, and only basic spectral/context features after explicit approval.

## 10. Main Finding

A4 interruption/re-entry strengthens the need to separate appearance, report, access, responsiveness, memory residue, and continuity. DREAM and anesthesia pressure report/response-based accounts because they expose gaps between private state, later residue, task responsiveness, and recovery timing. They do not close A0 or establish owned continuity. They create better falsifier terrain for AOC/OCH by forcing future reports to say which channel survived: report, access, responsiveness, field marker, memory residue, ownership, or continuity.

Most conservative compression:

> A4 is where the field may disappear, persist, fragment, or return, but the evidence usually arrives as residue.

## 11. Gap Map

- Whether dream report preserves enough of the original field remains open.
- Whether covert cognition implies conscious appearance remains open.
- How to distinguish unconscious task processing from conscious task following remains open.
- Whether re-entry preserves ownership or only memory/report residue remains open.
- Whether field markers survive interruption independently of access remains open.
- Whether A4 can ever decide A0 remains open; current answer is no, only pressure.

## 14. Verdict

- **What got stronger:** A4 as a distinct layer; report-insufficiency; residue discipline; responsiveness/consciousness separation; the need for dataset-specific pressure lanes.
- **What got weaker:** any shortcut that equates later report, hand squeeze, imagery-task success, arousal, access markers, or integration markers with appearance or ownership.
- **What remains open:** raw A0, whether ownership survives interruption, and whether re-entry restores the same owner or only reconstructs a reportable continuity.
- **What should be tested next:** `A_SPLIT_A4_PROPOFOL_DERIVATIVE_TIMELINE`, because local ds006623 derivative/control files are already present and can produce a small interruption/re-entry timeline without heavy imaging.

Best compression:

> Interruption gives us traces, not the field itself; A4 is the discipline of not mistaking the trace for the return.
