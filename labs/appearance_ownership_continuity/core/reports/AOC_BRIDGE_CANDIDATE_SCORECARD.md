## 1. Purpose

The first A-Split arc earned a provisional guardrail layer before it earned a
mechanism layer. This scorecard asks which bridge candidates remain worth
testing after the guardrails are applied.

AOC has sharpened A0 by blocking false substitutes. The next step is not to
declare a winner. The next step is to rank bridge candidates by explanatory
contact, false-positive risk, testability, and next-test value.

## 2. Guardrail Layer From CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml

The current provisional guardrail layer constrains this scorecard in a very
specific way.

- `A0_UNRESOLVED`: A0 remains open after the first A-Split arc.
- `REPORT_NOT_APPEARANCE`: report cannot stand in for raw appearance.
- `RESPONSIVENESS_NOT_CONSCIOUSNESS`: behavioral responsiveness is not enough.
- `FIELD_NOT_OWNER`: field structure does not automatically establish mineness.
- `FIELD_MARKERS_NOT_OWNER`: neural or functional field markers are not owner
  markers.
- `OWNERSHIP_NOT_REPORT`: mineness is not reducible to report.
- `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`: self-modeling cannot be treated as bare appearance.
- `TEMPORAL_FLOW_NOT_MEMORY`: active temporal succession is not memory residue.
- `TEMPORAL_CODING_NOT_LIVED_DURATION`: timing signals are not lived time.
- `PATTERN_NOT_OWNER`: copyable pattern does not establish same-owner
  continuity.
- `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`: blocking substitutes is not itself a
  mechanism.
- `BRIDGE_REQUIRED_FOR_A0`: any serious A0 account must explain why appearance
  is expected rather than merely tracked.
- `NO_BRIDGE_NOT_CLOSURE`: restraint about A0 is valuable, but it is not a
  completed explanation.

These guardrails make the scoring stricter. A candidate loses credit when it
quietly collapses A0 into report, access, integration, ownership, self-model,
temporal coding, memory, behavior, or pattern persistence. A candidate gains
credit when it survives those blocks and still offers a plausible reason why
appearance-from-within would occur at all.

## 3. Scoring Criteria

Each bridge candidate is scored from 0 to 5 on these axes:

1. `A0 contact`
   Does it try to explain appearance-from-within itself, rather than only a
   neighboring function?
2. `proxy-resistance`
   Does it avoid collapsing A0 into report, access, integration, self-modeling,
   ownership, temporal coding, memory, behavior, or copyable pattern?
3. `operationalizability`
   Can it generate testable markers or contrast cases without pretending the
   marker is A0?
4. `cross-layer fit`
   Does it connect coherently to A1 through A5 without swallowing them?
5. `falsifier clarity`
   Can we state what would weaken it?
6. `empirical contact`
   Do existing datasets or paradigms meaningfully pressure it?
7. `explanatory lift`
   Does it explain why A0 is expected rather than merely correlated?

Overall status labels used here:

- `high_value_bridge_candidate`
- `useful_neighbor_theory`
- `strong_guardrail_but_not_bridge`
- `currently_too_proxy_bound`
- `descriptive_but_underoperationalized`
- `unresolved / no_bridge_position`

## 6. Candidate Notes

### IIT / intrinsic integration

- What it explains nearby: integration, structured unity, and some field-like
  organization on the A1 side.
- What it might bridge: why a sufficiently intrinsic causal structure could be
  expected to have a from-within character.
- What guardrails constrain it: `REPORT_NOT_APPEARANCE`, `FIELD_MARKERS_NOT_OWNER`,
  `FIELD_NOT_OWNER`, `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: integrated structure alone cannot be treated as solved
  appearance or owned mineness.
- Best pressure source: A1 COGITATE plus the A0 hard-gap audit.
- Main false-positive risk: integration mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: a discriminating test where integration-based
  predictions outperform access/report and self-model alternatives without
  collapsing into marker worship.
- What would weaken it: repeated cases where integration tracks neighboring
  functions but does not explain why appearance should be present.
- Verdict: currently one of the better bridge candidates to pressure, but still
  too correlation-vulnerable to promote.

### RPT / recurrent perceptual processing

- What it explains nearby: perceptual presence, content stabilization, and the
  A1 field/access neighborhood.
- What it might bridge: a perceptual route to presence before full report.
- What guardrails constrain it: `REPORT_NOT_APPEARANCE`,
  `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: recurrent stabilization is not full A0, ownership, or
  same-self continuity.
- Best pressure source: A1 COGITATE and no-report framing inside prior reports.
- Main false-positive risk: recurrence mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: better contrast cases where recurrent processing
  predicts presence while access/report varies independently.
- What would weaken it: strong cases outside perceptual vision where appearance
  persists or is discussed without recurrence carrying the explanatory weight.
- Verdict: strong neighbor theory for A1, weaker as a full A0 bridge.

### GNWT / global access

- What it explains nearby: broadcast, task relevance, access, reportability,
  and flexible control.
- What it might bridge: a route from access to availability, but not a clear
  route to appearance itself.
- What guardrails constrain it: `REPORT_NOT_APPEARANCE`,
  `RESPONSIVENESS_NOT_CONSCIOUSNESS`, `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: access or report availability cannot be treated as A0.
- Best pressure source: A1 COGITATE and A4 interruption/re-entry.
- Main false-positive risk: access mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: evidence that access structure explains why there
  is something from within rather than just why content becomes usable.
- What would weaken it: continued success as an access theory paired with weak
  A0 contact.
- Verdict: useful for neighboring functions, currently too proxy-bound for A0.

### HOT / higher-order representation

- What it explains nearby: reflective awareness, meta-representation, and some
  report/reflection phenomena.
- What it might bridge: a representation-of-representation route to conscious
  presence.
- What guardrails constrain it: `REPORT_NOT_APPEARANCE`, `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`,
  `OWNERSHIP_NOT_REPORT`.
- What it cannot claim: higher-order description cannot substitute for raw
  appearance or ownership.
- Best pressure source: A2 ownership absent and A1 field/access.
- Main false-positive risk: higher-order report mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: clear cases where higher-order structure explains
  presence better than access/report alone without collapsing into self-report.
- What would weaken it: repeated dissociations where reflection changes and
  appearance seems to remain.
- Verdict: pressure-worthy as a reflective-awareness theory, not yet strong as
  an A0 bridge.

### Predictive Processing / Active Inference

- What it explains nearby: boundary maintenance, control coupling, regulation,
  model updating, and embodied error-management.
- What it might bridge: an account where appearance emerges from regulated,
  self-maintaining engagement under uncertainty.
- What guardrails constrain it: `RESPONSIVENESS_NOT_CONSCIOUSNESS`,
  `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`, `TEMPORAL_CODING_NOT_LIVED_DURATION`,
  `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: prediction or control is not bare appearance.
- Best pressure source: A4 interruption/re-entry plus A2 ownership absent.
- Main false-positive risk: prediction/control mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: a tighter account of why self-maintaining inference
  should entail from-within presence rather than only adaptive regulation.
- What would weaken it: continued explanatory drift toward control, agency, and
  embodiment without A0 lift.
- Verdict: a good neighbor theory with some bridge ambition, but still
  vulnerable to control-as-appearance collapse.

### Self-Model Theory

- What it explains nearby: subject structure, perspective organization, and
  self-related modeling.
- What it might bridge: how a modeled subject-position could support something
  like conscious self-presence.
- What guardrails constrain it: `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`, `OWNERSHIP_NOT_REPORT`,
  `PATTERN_NOT_OWNER`.
- What it cannot claim: self-modeling does not by itself establish A0 or same
  owner continuity.
- Best pressure source: A2 ownership absent and A5 upload/copy negative control.
- Main false-positive risk: self-model mistaken for subject.
- What would strengthen it: a way to distinguish modeled self-structure from
  merely report-capable or forkable self-description.
- What would weaken it: repeated negative controls where self-model-like
  behavior occurs without any reason to credit owned presence.
- Verdict: conceptually relevant, but currently too vulnerable to proxy collapse.

### Minimal Self / phenomenology

- What it explains nearby: pre-reflective for-me-ness, thin subjectivity, and
  the descriptive contour of lived presence.
- What it might bridge: the closest descriptive approach to A0 without
  immediately reducing it to report or self-model.
- What guardrails constrain it: `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`,
  `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`, `TEMPORAL_FLOW_NOT_MEMORY`.
- What it cannot claim: description of minimal self is not yet a mechanism of
  appearance.
- Best pressure source: A2 ownership absent and A3 temporal flow/minimal self.
- Main false-positive risk: minimal-self description mistaken for mechanism.
- What would strengthen it: operationalization that distinguishes thin for-me-ness
  from report, reflection, and body ownership without becoming circular.
- What would weaken it: inability to generate sharper tests than ordinary
  phenomenological redescription.
- Verdict: high A0 contact, low mechanistic traction, still worth pressing.

### Valence / homeostasis / affective stakes

- What it explains nearby: why experience matters to the organism, why states
  feel consequential, and how bodily stakes shape perspective.
- What it might bridge: a route where appearance requires valenced stakes rather
  than cold information structure alone.
- What guardrails constrain it: `RESPONSIVENESS_NOT_CONSCIOUSNESS`,
  `FIELD_NOT_OWNER`, `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: stakes or affect do not automatically explain why there
  is appearance at all.
- Best pressure source: A4 interruption/re-entry plus A2 ownership absent.
- Main false-positive risk: valence or stakes mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: clean dissociations where valenced regulation adds
  A0-relevant lift beyond access, control, and embodiment.
- What would weaken it: cases where affective importance explains why a state
  matters without explaining why it appears.
- Verdict: useful neighbor theory that might sharpen bridge questions, but not
  yet a bridge.

### Temporality / temporal flow

- What it explains nearby: lived succession, continuity pressure, and the A3
  seam between residue and active experience.
- What it might bridge: an account where active temporal flow is closer to
  owner-carried presence than memory or copied pattern.
- What guardrails constrain it: `TEMPORAL_FLOW_NOT_MEMORY`,
  `TEMPORAL_CODING_NOT_LIVED_DURATION`, `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: temporal flow alone does not solve A0.
- Best pressure source: A3 temporal flow/minimal self and A5 sleep/re-entry.
- Main false-positive risk: temporal flow mistaken for A0.
- What would strengthen it: an operational marker distinguishing lived flow
  from mere timing structure across interruption and re-entry cases.
- What would weaken it: inability to separate lived succession from descriptive
  phenomenology or temporal coding proxies.
- Verdict: important and close to continuity, but underoperationalized as an
  A0 bridge.

### Biological / dynamical continuity

- What it explains nearby: why ordinary waking and re-entry are stronger than
  copy cases, and why non-branching embodied process matters.
- What it might bridge: mostly A5 continuity pressure, not raw appearance.
- What guardrails constrain it: `PATTERN_NOT_OWNER`,
  `NONBRANCHING_NOT_SUFFICIENT`, `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`.
- What it cannot claim: biological continuity is not itself proof of owner or
  A0.
- Best pressure source: A5 sleep/re-entry owner and A5 branching identity.
- Main false-positive risk: biological continuity mistaken for owner or
  appearance.
- What would strengthen it: evidence that dynamical continuity explains more
  than branch resistance and continuity pragmatics.
- What would weaken it: stable demonstration that biological persistence only
  strengthens practical identity judgments without explanatory A0 lift.
- Verdict: strong guardrail and continuity constraint, not a direct A0 bridge.

### Embodiment / sensorimotor coupling

- What it explains nearby: lived perspective through action, body-world
  coupling, and some ownership/agency structure.
- What it might bridge: a route where appearance depends on situated coupling
  rather than abstract computation alone.
- What guardrails constrain it: `AGENCY_NOT_OWNERSHIP`, `SELF_MODEL_NOT_A`,
  `RESPONSIVENESS_NOT_CONSCIOUSNESS`.
- What it cannot claim: coupling, action, or bodily embeddedness cannot be
  treated as raw appearance.
- Best pressure source: A2 ownership absent and A4 interruption/re-entry.
- Main false-positive risk: embodied control mistaken for appearance.
- What would strengthen it: clean cases where sensorimotor coupling explains
  more than agency, regulation, and world-orientation.
- What would weaken it: persistent inability to separate embodied competence
  from lived subjectivity.
- Verdict: important neighbor theory with useful guardrail pressure, but not a
  clean A0 bridge yet.

### No-Bridge / Primitive-A stance

- What it explains nearby: not a mechanism, but a disciplined refusal to fake
  one.
- What it might bridge: nothing directly; it holds the explanatory gap open.
- What guardrails constrain it: `PROXY_REJECTION_NOT_EXPLANATION`,
  `NO_BRIDGE_NOT_CLOSURE`.
- What it cannot claim: caution is not explanation, and hard-gap restraint is
  not victory.
- Best pressure source: A0 hard-gap audit.
- Main false-positive risk: no-bridge restraint mistaken for explanation.
- What would strengthen it: repeated failure of bridge candidates under clear
  guardrails.
- What would weaken it: a bridge candidate that survives proxy rejection and
  produces clear discriminating tests with genuine explanatory lift.
- Verdict: necessary discipline, not a bridge.

## 9. What Would Count As Bridge Progress?

Progress levels:

- `Level 0`: proxy rejection only.
- `Level 1`: candidate bridge has clear target and avoids obvious proxy collapse.
- `Level 2`: candidate bridge produces discriminating tests against at least two
  neighbor theories.
- `Level 3`: candidate bridge predicts presence/absence/structure of appearance
  better than report/access/ownership/temporal alternatives.
- `Level 4`: candidate bridge explains why the marker entails
  appearance-from-within rather than merely tracking it.

Current assignments for the strongest candidates:

- IIT / intrinsic integration: `Level 1`
  It has a clear bridge ambition and some empirical contact, but it still needs
  stronger discrimination against access and marker-collapse alternatives.
- Minimal Self / phenomenology: `Level 1`
  It clearly targets lived appearance and avoids some proxy collapse, but it is
  still weakly operationalized.
- RPT / recurrent perceptual processing: `Level 1`
  Strong enough to shape perceptual-presence tests, but still mainly an A1
  neighbor rather than a mature A0 bridge.
- Predictive Processing / Active Inference: `Level 1`
  Clear target around embodied regulation, but still too vulnerable to
  control-as-appearance collapse.
- Temporality / temporal flow: `Level 1`
  Important for continuity pressure, but still not discriminating enough to go
  beyond descriptive bridge interest.
- No-Bridge / Primitive-A stance: `Level 0`
  Valuable for restraint, but not a bridge candidate.

## 11. Main Finding

The strongest A0 bridge candidates are not the same as the theories with the
best empirical neighborhood evidence. AOC's guardrail layer makes bridge
evaluation stricter: a candidate must explain why appearance is expected, not
merely why information is integrated, accessed, represented, reported,
temporally ordered, embodied, or controlled.

At this stage, IIT and minimal-self/phenomenology look like the highest-value
bridge candidates to pressure next, but for opposite reasons. IIT is easier to
test and easier to overcredit. Minimal-self language stays closer to lived
structure, but remains weakly operationalized.
