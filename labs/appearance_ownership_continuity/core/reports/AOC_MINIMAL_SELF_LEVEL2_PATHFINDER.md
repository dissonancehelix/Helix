## 1. Purpose

The flow false-positive battery sharpened A3/minimal-self pressure but did not
produce Level 2 progress. This pathfinder tests which next route is most likely
to move minimal self / thin for-me-ness toward Level 2 discrimination.

Flow was useful but too false-positive dense. The next pass should not blindly
continue flow. The next pass should compare ownership dissociation,
valence/stakes, synthetic negative controls, and empirical interruption
pressure as possible Level 2 routes.

## 2. Level 2 Criteria

A route reaches Level 2 only if it:

- discriminates against at least two neighbor theories
- blocks known proxy collapses in advance
- makes predictions specific enough to fail
- changes claim maturity if successful
- pre-specifies overclaiming routes
- does not rely only on report, phenomenological vocabulary, or negative
  subtraction

## 5. Route 1: Ownership Dissociation / Nonunitary Ownership

Pressure questions:

- Can body ownership, agency, report, self-modeling, and thin mineness vary
  independently?
- Does altered body ownership imply altered for-me-ness?
- Does altered agency imply altered ownership?
- Can depersonalization or derealization show mineness thinning while
  perception and report persist?
- Can rubber-hand or full-body illusion show body ownership shifting while a
  thinner for-me-ness persists?
- Can alien-limb pressure show agency fracture without total mineness collapse?

Comparison set:

- Self-Model Theory
- Embodiment
- Agency/control theories
- Minimal Self

Why this route is strong:

- It pressures several neighboring impostors at once.
- It can force a split between body model, agency model, explicit self-report,
  and thin mineness.
- It is less dependent on retrospective flow narration.

Main risk:

- minimal self becomes circular if defined only as "what remains after body and
  agency are stripped out"

Level 2 evaluation:

- This route has the best current chance of Level 2 discrimination because it
  can generate fail-able contrasts between minimal self, self-model theory, and
  agency/embodiment accounts.

## 6. Route 2: Valence / Homeostasis / Affective Stakes

Pressure questions:

- Does valence add bridge-relevant mattering beyond access, control, and
  report?
- Does affective stake help explain why experience matters from within?
- Can valence be separated from arousal, reward, task engagement, and
  responsiveness?
- Can painful, anxious, pleasurable, or bodily-need states pressure A0 or A2
  more than neutral perception?
- Does valence explain appearance, or only why an appeared state matters?

Comparison set:

- Predictive Processing / Active Inference
- Embodiment
- Minimal Self
- IIT / field theories

Why this route is promising:

- It may expose a "mattering" component that neutral field theories often
  underspecify.
- It may pressure the difference between appearance and significance.

Main risk:

- valence explains why a state matters without explaining why it appears
- valence may collapse into reward, regulation, or homeostatic control

Level 2 evaluation:

- Medium. Worth doing soon, but weaker than ownership dissociation because the
  bridge risk is still high and the candidate target is less cleanly separated.

## 7. Route 3: Synthetic Negative Controls For A0

Pressure questions:

- Can we construct artificial or conceptual negative controls that preserve
  report, persona, self-model-like structure, agency-like output, and
  performance while lacking owner evidence?
- What does this subtract?
- Which theories are most constrained by these controls?
- Can negative controls distinguish minimal self from self-model theory?
- What would be overclaiming?

Included control family:

- LLM fluent task execution
- LLM persona continuity
- forked LLM sessions
- archived transcript / memory store
- simulated agent with self-model labels
- high-performance non-reporting system

Why this route is useful:

- It strongly constrains weak self-model, report, and pattern-based evidence.
- It can sharpen what minimal self must not be confused with.

Main risk:

- negative controls strengthen guardrails without generating positive
  discriminating structure
- overuse can turn subtraction into pseudo-explanation

Level 2 evaluation:

- Medium. Strong for discipline, weaker as a direct bridge pathfinder unless
  paired with a positive ownership target.

## 8. Route 4: Flow Continuation

Pressure questions:

- Is there any sharper flow test left after the false-positive battery?
- Can flow be separated from performance, agency/control, report, temporal
  distortion, reward, and hidden self-modeling?
- Would another flow pass deepen the next prompt, or repeat the same
  false-positive map?

Current reading:

- Flow still matters, especially for reduced reflection and temporal
  self-presence.
- But the recent battery already showed that flow is surrounded by too many
  impostors to be the best immediate Level 2 route.

Level 2 evaluation:

- Low for now. Flow should not be abandoned, but another immediate flow pass
  would likely repeat the same constraints without adding enough discrimination.

## 9. Route 5: Propofol / Interruption Derivative Alignment

Pressure questions:

- Could derivative-level interruption/re-entry analysis pressure minimal self
  or ownership better than conceptual cases?
- Can propofol timing, responsiveness, and re-entry help separate owner-presence
  from response?
- Does this route deepen A4/A2 or distract from the minimal-self path?

Why this route matters:

- It can sharpen the response-versus-presence boundary.
- It gives empirical discipline around interruption and recovery.

Main limits:

- derivative-level interruption work still speaks more naturally to A4 than to
  minimal self directly
- it risks drifting back toward response-marker interpretation

Level 2 evaluation:

- Low to medium. Valuable later, but not the best immediate route for minimal
  self discrimination.

## 12. What Would Count As Success In The Chosen Next Test?

Exact ambiguity targeted:

- whether thin mineness can be distinguished from body ownership, agency,
  self-modeling, and report

Theories pressured:

- Minimal Self
- Self-Model Theory
- Embodiment
- Agency/control theories

Expected discriminating structure:

- cases where body ownership shifts but thinner for-me-ness plausibly persists
- cases where agency fractures but ownership-like presence does not fully
  collapse
- cases where report remains strong while mineness thins

What would strengthen minimal self:

- non-circular evidence that a thin for-me-ness channel tracks differently from
  body model, agency, and explicit self-modeling

What would weaken minimal self:

- all target dissociations are fully captured by representational/body/agency
  model changes with no remaining contrast

What would strengthen rival theory:

- self-model or embodiment accounts predict dissociation patterns more sharply
  than minimal-self language

What result would leave all unresolved:

- cases remain too mixed or report-dependent to distinguish remainder from
  representation

Forbidden overclaims:

- ownership dissociation proves minimal self
- body ownership equals mineness
- agency/control equals ownership
- altered ownership reports transparently reveal original structure

## 13. Main Finding

Flow sharpened the minimal-self test surface but did not produce Level 2
progress. The most promising next route is ownership dissociation, because it
pressures body ownership, agency, self-modeling, report, and thin mineness more
directly than flow.

Valence/stakes and synthetic negative controls should be queued as parallel
pressure routes. Flow should remain available as support context, not as the
immediate front line.
