## 1. Purpose

The bridge scorecard ranked IIT as a high-value bridge candidate because it has
unusually direct A0 ambition and unusually strong empirical pressure routes.
This report tests whether that ambition survives AOC's guardrail layer.

IIT may be the best formal bridge candidate to pressure. IIT is not being
treated as confirmed. The pressure question is whether intrinsic integration
explains appearance, or only tracks structured unity.

## 2. IIT Bridge Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> Intrinsic integrated cause-effect structure can explain why
> appearance-from-within is expected rather than merely correlated with
> structured neural or functional organization.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- IIT is not assumed true
- IIT is not assumed false
- Phi is not assumed to be consciousness
- integration is not assumed to be appearance
- maximal substrate is not assumed to be owner
- IIT does not automatically solve A2 ownership or A5 continuity
- COGITATE does not automatically settle IIT

## 5. A0 Contact Analysis

IIT does directly target A0 more than most neighboring theories do. It does not
merely say that consciousness becomes reportable, globally available, or
recurrently stabilized. It tries to account for phenomenal existence in
physical and operational terms by deriving postulates from axioms of
experience.

That gives IIT unusually high A0 ambition. The pressure point is whether the
move from phenomenal axioms to physical postulates is explanatory, stipulative,
abductive, or operational.

Most conservative reading:

- IIT is stronger than a mere correlation theory because it explicitly tries to
  identify what physical structure would have to be present if experience is
  real.
- IIT is weaker than a completed explanation because the bridge can still look
  like a formal identity claim: experience is said to be intrinsic integrated
  cause-effect structure, but the reason that such structure should be lived
  appearance can remain underexplained.

If IIT is accepted only as a structure or field theory, then much of A0
remains. In that case IIT would still help explain why conscious fields are
unified, intrinsic, and irreducible, but not why there is anything it is like
from within rather than merely a system with those properties.

Current judgment: IIT has direct A0 contact, but its explanatory lift is still
partly contested because the formal identification of appearance with
cause-effect structure risks looking more like a bridge declaration than a
fully explained bridge.

## 6. A1 Field Pressure

IIT is strongest under AOC around A1 fielded appearance.

It gives a natural language for:

- structured unity,
- differentiated content,
- irreducibility,
- substrate-level organization,
- field-like character.

That makes it a better fit for A1 than access/report theories that lean toward
broadcast, task relevance, or flexible use.

COGITATE matters here because it directly compared IIT and GNWT using fMRI,
MEG, and iEEG and did not produce a clean victory story. The report-level AOC
take is:

- COGITATE supports using IIT as real pressure terrain.
- COGITATE constrains any easy claim that IIT has already won on field
  prediction.
- COGITATE does not refute IIT outright.

Content markers, sustained posterior/temporal activity, and structured
synchronization may be more naturally aligned with IIT than with pure access
models. But under AOC guardrails they remain markers, not A0 itself. They may
support A1 field/content organization without settling raw appearance or
ownership.

Current result: IIT remains strong on A1 field unity, but that success does not
automatically transfer to A0.

## 7. A2 Ownership Pressure

IIT does not presently contain a clear ownership marker in the AOC sense.

Pressure questions:

- Does a complex being intrinsic to itself explain mineness?
- Is intrinsic causal unity equivalent to ownership?
- Could a field be integrated without being owned?

Under AOC guardrails, the answer is that IIT may speak more naturally to field
unity than to mineness. A2 cases such as depersonalization, alien-limb style
pressure, body-ownership illusions, and LLM self-report negative controls all
show why field structure and owner structure cannot simply be merged.

The weak point is not that IIT says nothing valuable. It is that intrinsicality
is not yet the same as ownership. A system being causally self-related does not
obviously explain why the field is mine rather than simply integrated.

Current result: IIT is constrained at A2. It may help with the field side of
experience more naturally than with ownership.

## 8. A3 Temporal Pressure

IIT does not naturally explain lived temporal flow as strongly as it explains
structured unity.

Main pressure points:

- IIT risks treating temporal flow as causal-state structure or succession.
- AOC's temporal guardrails block the move from temporal coding to lived
  duration.
- It remains unclear whether lived continuity is intrinsic to the complex or
  only built from a sequence of momentary conscious states.

That matters because A3 is not just about state order. It is about lived
succession. IIT may offer a series of intrinsic structures across time, but
that does not automatically tell us why there is a flowing temporal field
rather than discrete structured moments linked externally.

Current result: IIT is constrained on A3. It does not obviously help OCH with
owner-carried temporal continuity, and it may partly bypass continuity rather
than explain it.

## 9. A4 Interruption / Re-entry Pressure

IIT is pressure-worthy in interruption cases because it at least tries to say
something about presence or absence independently of overt report.

That is an advantage over simple report-first theories.

Still, AOC guardrails force several cautions:

- IIT cannot equate report with field.
- IIT cannot equate responsiveness with consciousness.
- IIT cannot treat dose, hand squeeze, or dream narrative as the target.

In DREAM-style cases, IIT might help frame why a sleep state could support or
fail to support a structured conscious field. But later report remains residue.
In propofol-style cases, IIT may predict changes in conscious-supporting
structure better than raw responsiveness does. But motor dissociations and
covert cognition remain underdetermined without collapsing markers into
appearance.

Current result: IIT stays relevant to A4 because it is not inherently tied to
report or responsiveness, but it still needs better discriminating tests if it
is to outperform access/report alternatives without proxy collapse.

## 10. A5 Continuity / Branching Pressure

IIT appears weak at A5 by design.

Main pressure points:

- If two systems instantiate equivalent Phi-structures, IIT does not obviously
  tell us which one, if either, is the original owner.
- IIT seems better suited to identifying candidate conscious complexes at a
  time than to solving same-owner continuity across time.
- Branching and copyability threaten the idea that momentary structured
  consciousness alone can settle personal identity.

This does not count as an IIT failure on its own terms if IIT was never meant
to solve A5. But for AOC it is still an important limit. OCH and A5 need more
than momentary field structure.

Current result: IIT does not solve branching. It may identify multiple
conscious complexes without settling singular first-person continuation.
Copyability therefore limits IIT's relevance to OCH unless IIT explicitly
concedes that A5 lies outside its main scope.

## 13. What Would Strengthen IIT As A Bridge?

- integration-based predictions outperform access/report models in
  discriminating cases
- IIT generates a clear contrast case where field unity is predicted but
  report/access diverges
- IIT explains why intrinsic cause-effect structure entails appearance rather
  than merely tracking it
- IIT handles no-report, DREAM, anesthesia, and COGITATE pressure without
  proxy collapse
- IIT distinguishes field unity from ownership/mineness, or explicitly concedes
  that it does not address A2
- IIT states what would count as a failed bridge attempt

## 14. What Would Weaken IIT As A Bridge?

- integration tracks neural complexity but not appearance better than
  access/report alternatives
- IIT repeatedly explains A1 field structure but not A0
- IIT cannot avoid treating formal identity as explanation
- COGITATE-style tests keep finding mixed or non-specific support
- ownership and continuity remain external to the theory
- IIT cannot produce a discriminating test against neighbor theories

## 15. Main Finding

IIT remains one of the strongest formal A0 bridge candidates because it
directly tries to connect phenomenal structure to intrinsic physical structure.
But under AOC guardrails, IIT is constrained: integration, intrinsic
cause-effect power, and Phi-structure cannot simply be treated as appearance
itself.

IIT is strongest around A1 field unity and weakest around A2 ownership and A5
continuity. Its main advantage is that it at least tries to bridge A0 in a
testable way. Its main weakness is that the bridge can still look like formal
identification of structure with appearance rather than a satisfying account of
why appearance is expected.

It remains pressure-worthy, not promoted.
