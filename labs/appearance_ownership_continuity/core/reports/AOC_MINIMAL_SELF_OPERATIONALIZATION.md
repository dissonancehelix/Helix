## 1. Purpose

The bridge discrimination matrix identified IIT versus Minimal Self as the
strongest high-value contrast pair. IIT has already received a dedicated
pressure pass. This report asks whether minimal/pre-reflective for-me-ness can
become operational rather than remaining descriptive.

Minimal self may be closest to lived appearance. Closeness is not mechanism.
The question is whether for-me-ness can be separated from neighboring proxies.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> Pre-reflective for-me-ness / minimal self is a candidate bridge between bare
> appearance, ownership, and temporal flow, but it must be operationalized
> without collapsing into self-model, report, body ownership, agency, or
> narrative continuity.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- minimal self is not assumed to solve A0
- minimal self is not assumed to be identical with A0
- for-me-ness is not assumed to be a mechanism
- phenomenological description is not assumed to be explanation
- minimal self is not assumed absent when reflective self-awareness is reduced

## 5. Operationalization Target

What would count as an operational handle on for-me-ness?

### Dissociation from explicit self-report

- What it could test:
  - whether first-person report is optional rather than constitutive for
    minimal self
- What it cannot test:
  - raw A0 directly
- Overclaiming risk:
  - absence of report treated as proof of thin selfhood

### Dissociation from body ownership

- What it could test:
  - whether altered limb/body ownership leaves a thinner first-personal
    presence intact
- What it cannot test:
  - same-owner continuity or full A0
- Overclaiming risk:
  - every body-ownership disruption treated as pure minimal-self exposure

### Dissociation from agency/control

- What it could test:
  - whether authorship and control can vary while for-me-ness remains
- What it cannot test:
  - whether residual for-me-ness explains appearance
- Overclaiming risk:
  - passive experience treated as owner-evidence by default

### Persistence during reduced reflective self-awareness

- What it could test:
  - whether flow, absorbed action, or ordinary unselfconscious experience keep
    a thin self-presence beneath explicit reflection
- What it cannot test:
  - whether that thin presence is mechanism rather than structure
- Overclaiming risk:
  - low reflection mistaken for direct access to minimal self

### Alteration during depersonalization/derealization

- What it could test:
  - whether mineness can thin or distort while report and perception remain
- What it cannot test:
  - zero-mineness consciousness
- Overclaiming risk:
  - clinical reports treated as transparent access to underlying structure

### Relation to flow states

- What it could test:
  - whether reflective self drops while a task-bound pre-reflective for-me-ness
    remains
- What it cannot test:
  - whether flow uniquely identifies minimal self rather than agency/temporal
    absorption
- Overclaiming risk:
  - flow romanticized into explanation

### Relation to dream experience and no-recall reports

- What it could test:
  - whether first-person structure may persist when later report is degraded
    or absent
- What it cannot test:
  - direct dream-field ownership during no-recall cases
- Overclaiming risk:
  - no-recall treated as hidden evidence for minimal self

### Relation to LLM self-report negative controls

- What it could test:
  - whether first-person language and self-description can be subtracted away
    from genuine for-me-ness claims
- What it cannot test:
  - future artificial minimal self
- Overclaiming risk:
  - current LLM limits treated as universal proof

Current operational target:

> A thin for-me-ness channel that can be pressured by dissociations from
> report, self-model, body ownership, agency, and reflective narration without
> being reduced to any one of them.

## 6. Minimal Self vs Self-Model

Pairwise pressure:
Can pre-reflective for-me-ness be separated from modeled self-representation?

What would favor minimal self:

- altered-ownership or flow cases where lived "for-me" structure appears to
  vary without matching variation in explicit self-description
- cases where reflection decreases but experience remains organized as present
  to a subject

What would favor self-model theory:

- modeled subject structure predicts the relevant dissociations more sharply
  than phenomenological categories do
- pre-reflective for-me-ness always tracks self-representation changes

What would leave both unresolved:

- all evidence comes from report-level phenomenology with no independent
  operational contrast

What negative controls matter:

- LLM persona and self-report cases
- copied narrative/persona structures
- explicit self-description without ownership evidence

How LLM persona/self-report constrains self-model claims:

Current LLMs show that structurally coherent first-person description can exist
without owner evidence. That does not refute all self-model theories, but it
forces them to distinguish represented selfhood from inhabited selfhood. That
same pressure benefits minimal self only if minimal self can say what remains
after self-model-like structure is subtracted.

Current result: minimal self survives this comparison better than self-model
theory when the target is lived for-me-ness, but it still lacks a stable
operational marker.

## 7. Minimal Self vs Body Ownership

Pairwise pressure:
Can for-me-ness persist when body ownership is altered?

Prior A2 pressure suggests yes, at least provisionally.

- Depersonalization and derealization suggest perception and report may persist
  while ordinary mineness thins or estranges.
- Rubber-hand and full-body illusion work suggests body ownership can shift or
  destabilize without eliminating all first-person perspective.
- Alien-limb style cases show agency authorship can distort while body presence
  remains partly intact.
- Flow cases suggest strong task absorption with less reflective self-focus and
  plausible residual for-me-ness.

Pressure questions:

- Does altered body ownership imply altered for-me-ness?
  - sometimes, but not necessarily in a total or uniform way
- Can body ownership change while a thinner for-me-ness persists?
  - this is one of the strongest reasons minimal self remains interesting
- Can ownership be nonunitary?
  - yes, that remains a serious pressure possibility even if not yet promoted
    into the ledger
- What would count as too much circularity?
  - defining the "thinner for-me-ness" as whatever remains after body ownership
    changes, without an independent criterion

Current result: body ownership is a useful subtraction lane, but not a clean
definition of minimal self.

## 8. Minimal Self vs Reflective Self-Awareness

Pairwise pressure:
Can minimal self remain when reflective self-awareness is reduced?

The current best answer is yes, provisionally.

Flow states, absorbed action, and ordinary unselfconscious experience all
suggest that explicit self-referential thought can diminish while organized
first-person presence remains. Dream experience may also preserve a first-person
structure with reduced or unstable reflection. Depersonalization complicates the
picture by showing that reflective distress can increase while ordinary mineness
weakens.

Pressure questions:

- Does reduced self-referential thought imply absent mineness?
  - no; A2 and A3 both block that shortcut
- Does flow hide reflective self while preserving pre-reflective ownership?
  - that is one of the strongest current candidate interpretations
- How does this connect to A3 temporal flow?
  - minimal self may be the subject-side correlate of lived temporal flow,
    whereas flow is the succession-side candidate

Current result: reflective self-awareness is not the same thing as minimal
self. That separation strengthens minimal self as a target, even if it does not
yet turn it into a mechanism.

## 9. Minimal Self vs A0

A0 bridge pressure:
Does minimal self explain appearance or only describe the for-me structure of
appearance?

Pressure questions:

- Is for-me-ness part of A0, A2, or a bridge between them?
  - current best answer: bridge or border concept, not cleanly one or the
    other
- Could there be appearance without for-me-ness?
  - still open
- Could there be for-me-ness without reflective self?
  - plausibly yes
- Does minimal self explain why appearance exists, or only how it is structured
  once present?
  - currently it looks stronger on structure than on existence
- What would count as bridge progress?
  - a non-circular operational distinction from self-model, report, body
    ownership, and reflection that also changes how we discriminate candidate
    theories

Current result: minimal self currently describes the for-me contour of
experience more convincingly than it explains why appearance exists at all.

## 12. What Would Strengthen Minimal Self As A Bridge?

- operational distinction from self-modeling
- persistence beneath reduced reflective self-awareness
- dissociation from body ownership and agency
- non-circular relation to temporal flow
- ability to generate predictions against at least two neighbor theories
- ability to explain why for-me-ness is expected, not merely named

## 13. What Would Weaken Minimal Self As A Bridge?

- all evidence remains report-dependent
- for-me-ness cannot be distinguished from self-model, body ownership, or
  agency
- operationalization becomes circular
- minimal self cannot generate contrast cases
- it remains only phenomenological redescription
- it cannot explain A0 beyond naming lived structure

## 14. Main Finding

Minimal self remains one of the closest candidates to lived A0/A2/A3
structure, but it is not yet a mechanism. It becomes more useful if treated as
an operational target: a thin for-me-ness channel that must be separated from
self-modeling, report, body ownership, agency, and temporal narration.

Its next progress criterion is not elegance, but discriminating operational
handles.
