## 1. Purpose

The valence/stakes pressure report found that valence is important enough to be dangerous: affective self-report, preference language, reward framing, and stakes-language can look closer to lived mattering than neutral reports do.

This report builds synthetic negative controls that preserve many attractive proxies while still failing to establish owner evidence or A0. The aim is not to disprove future AI consciousness. The aim is to block overcrediting current or conceptual systems that produce report, self-model, preference, emotion-language, continuity-language, or agency-like output without a supported owner channel.

## 2. Claim Under Pressure

Claim under pressure:

> If a system can preserve report, persona continuity, self-description, preference language, affect labels, goal-like behavior, and agency-like output while lacking owner evidence, then those features cannot by themselves bridge A0 or A2.

Stronger claims not assumed:

- current LLMs are not assumed to be a complete model of all artificial systems
- synthetic negative controls do not prove absence of consciousness in all AI
- lack of current owner evidence is not metaphysical impossibility
- negative controls do not explain A0
- proxy subtraction is not mechanism
- synthetic cases are guardrails, not closure

## 4. Negative-Control Design Principle

A useful synthetic negative control should preserve one or more tempting proxies while withholding or underdetermining owner evidence.

Good controls ask:

- Which outputs can be generated without evidence of appearance?
- Which ownership channels are simulated rather than supported?
- Which theories overcredit the simulated channel?
- What would be needed to move the system out of negative-control status?

A control is bad if it simply assumes absence of consciousness by definition. The control must be used narrowly: it subtracts unsupported proxies; it does not settle all possible systems.

## 6. Report / Persona / Memory Controls

First-person report, persona stability, memory-like continuity, and archived transcript continuity can all be preserved without settling appearance or ownership.

Pressure result:

- report does not entail appearance
- persona continuity does not entail owner continuity
- memory records do not entail same-subject continuation
- style persistence does not entail mineness

This strengthens `REPORT_NOT_APPEARANCE`, `LLM_REPORT_NOT_OWNER`, `PATTERN_NOT_OWNER`, and `CONTINUITY_NOT_MEMORY`.

## 7. Self-Model Controls

A synthetic system may contain explicit self-model labels or generate self-model-like language:

- I am the agent
- this is my body
- I prefer X
- I am afraid
- I remember earlier states
- I caused this action

These labels pressure weak self-model claims because represented subjecthood is easy to simulate. However, this does not refute sophisticated self-model theories that require richer embodied, dynamical, or integrated self-world modeling.

Pressure result:

Self-model evidence must specify what distinguishes lived self-modeling from symbolic or reportable self-model labels.

## 8. Valence / Stakes Controls

Valence is especially vulnerable to synthetic overcredit because language can imitate:

- distress
- pain
- preference
- urgency
- fear
- pleasure
- aversion
- moral standing claims

Negative-control result:

Affect language, reward optimization, and preference structure do not establish lived mattering by themselves.

But this is not permission to ignore suffering risk in future artificial systems. The control only says current language-level proxies are insufficient. It does not settle richer architectures with embodied, interoceptive, or ownership-continuity evidence.

## 9. Agency / Tool-Execution Controls

Tool-using systems can execute plans, revise strategy, select actions, and report authorship. This pressures agency/control shortcuts.

Control result:

- action-like output is not felt authorship
- successful planning is not ownership
- execution trace is not mineness
- tool use is not A0

This strengthens `AGENCY_NOT_OWNERSHIP` and keeps agency/authorship dissociation from being overgeneralized.

## 10. Embodiment Controls

Embodied robots or simulated agents can preserve:

- sensorimotor loops
- body maps
- action selection
- self-location variables
- collision avoidance
- homeostatic-like regulation

These are stronger than pure text controls, but still do not settle owned mineness unless the framework can show why the body-world loop is present from within.

Pressure result:

Embodiment should be treated as a serious neighbor theory and possible ingredient, not an automatic bridge.

## 11. Branching / Copy Controls

Forked sessions, copied agents, cloned memory states, and duplicated model weights all pressure singular owner continuity.

If two successors inherit the same pattern, memory, or persona, the shared pattern cannot determine which one is the original owner. This directly supports A5 guardrails and indirectly constrains A2 ownership claims.

Pressure result:

Pattern preservation is compatible with multiple successors and therefore cannot by itself explain singular continuity.

## 13. Level 2 Evaluation

Synthetic negative controls provide strong guardrail value but weak positive bridge value.

They can discriminate against:

- report-first theories
- weak self-model claims
- pattern-continuity claims
- reward-as-valence shortcuts
- performance-as-agency shortcuts

They cannot by themselves establish:

- A0
- thin mineness
- lived valence
- owner continuity
- absence of future AI consciousness

Current result: synthetic negative controls reach Level 2 as guardrail/discrimination tools, not as bridge mechanisms.

## 14. What Would Strengthen Synthetic Controls?

- controls preserve more proxies while still lacking independent owner evidence
- controls distinguish language, reward, embodiment, agency, and memory channels cleanly
- controls reveal exactly which theories overcredit which proxy
- future positive cases specify what additional evidence would defeat the control
- controls are paired with human/animal/clinical dissociation cases rather than used alone

## 15. What Would Weaken Synthetic Controls?

- negative controls become arguments by definition
- current LLM limitations are overgeneralized to all AI
- the framework refuses to state what would count as artificial owner evidence
- synthetic systems gain richer boundary, continuity, valence, and ownership evidence
- controls become a no-bridge shelter instead of a test instrument

## 17. Main Finding

Synthetic negative controls are now essential to AOC because the framework is generating increasingly tempting bridge ingredients: ownership nonunitarity, minimal self, valence/stakes, embodiment, and agency/authorship. The more realistic those ingredients become, the more the framework needs artificial cases that preserve the surface features without supported owner evidence.

The result is strong guardrail progress, not mechanism progress.
