# A-Split: Appearance-from-Within Decomposition

## 0. Source Lineage

This harness comes from the A-Bridge / appearance-from-within calibration line and the current AOC/OCH gap calibration. Reports are pressure maps, not canon. This file does not solve A; it makes A harder to fake by separating neighboring problems that often get collapsed into one word.

Active parent orientation remains `labs/appearance_ownership_continuity/THEORY.md`. The split belongs to the core harness because it produces test targets, false positives, and claim boundaries.

## 1. Why Split A?

Appearance-from-within is too easy to protect as a single fog bank. If every nearby concept is called consciousness, the hard problem gets renamed rather than tested. If every nearby concept is rejected because it does not solve raw A, the research program loses useful pressure.

A-Split separates the target layers:

- A0 asks why anything is present at all.
- A1 asks why presence is fielded or unified.
- A2 asks why appearance is mine.
- A3 asks why appearance flows through lived time.
- A4 asks what survives interruption and re-entry.
- A5 asks when transformed continuity remains same-self.

The point is not to dissolve A into parts. The point is to stop report, access, memory, integration, self-modeling, temporality, or continuity from pretending to be the whole.

## 2. A0 - Bare Appearance

**Definition:** Bare appearance is the fact that there is anything present from within at all.

**Question:** Why is there something it is like, rather than only modeling, reporting, integrating, predicting, or acting?

**Evidence pressure:** no-report paradigms, anesthesia transitions, dream reports, minimal experience, pain, emotion, and candidate intrinsic-field accounts can pressure this layer only indirectly.

**False positives:**

- fluent report,
- access to information,
- successful behavior,
- self-reference,
- neural complexity,
- integration metrics,
- memory persistence.

**Unresolved hard gap:** A0 remains the raw A gap. AOC/OCH can name it, protect it, and prevent substitutes; it may not claim to solve it.

## 3. A1 - Fielded Appearance

**Definition:** Fielded appearance is appearance organized as a unified or structured field rather than disconnected fragments.

**Question:** Why does experience appear with unity, relation, background, foreground, and co-presence?

**Relation to IIT/RPT:** IIT is a candidate pressure source for intrinsic integration and field unity. Recurrent Processing Theory is a candidate pressure source for perceptual presence and local recurrent stabilization. Both may pressure A1 without solving A0 or A2.

**False positives:**

- any integrated system,
- any synchronized network,
- any complex map,
- any recurrent computation,
- global data structure mistaken for fielded appearance.

## 4. A2 - Ownership / Mineness

**Definition:** Ownership / mineness is the layer where an appearance is not merely occurring, but occurring as mine.

**Question:** Why is the field owned rather than merely present?

**Relation to self-modeling:** Self-modeling may help explain subject-structure, perspective, body ownership, and agency attribution, but a model of self is not automatically owned experience.

**Relation to body ownership and depersonalization:** rubber-hand illusion, full-body illusion, agency illusion, alien limb, depersonalization, and derealization pressure A2 because they alter ownership while report and perception may remain.

**False positives:**

- saying "I",
- having a profile or memory file,
- a body schema without mineness,
- task agency without owned agency,
- report of ownership without independent pressure.

## 5. A3 - Temporal Flow

**Definition:** Temporal flow is appearance persisting as lived succession: before, now, after, waiting, loss, anticipation, continuity, and change.

**Question:** Why does appearance unfold rather than merely exist as static state or ordered records?

**Relation to minimal self:** minimal self may require temporal ownership: not just a point of perspective, but a perspective that continues.

**Relation to time-consciousness:** temporal coding, sequence memory, clocks, and prediction are neighbors. Lived duration is the target.

**False positives:**

- timestamps,
- ordered records,
- sequence prediction,
- timers,
- memory lookup,
- planning without lived duration.

## 6. A4 - Interruption / Re-Entry

**Definition:** Interruption / re-entry asks what happens when appearance, report, memory, control, or ownership is blocked, suspended, altered, or restored.

**Question:** What survives sleep, anesthesia, blackout, depersonalization, or report-blocked states?

**Relevant pressure cases:**

- dreams with delayed report,
- anesthesia loss/recovery,
- no-report paradigms,
- locked-in or covert consciousness,
- blackout/fainting,
- depersonalization/derealization,
- dreamless awareness or objectless awareness as speculative pressure only.

**Continuity breaks:** A4 distinguishes no report, no recall, no access, no ownership, and no appearance. It blocks the shortcut where absence of report becomes absence of experience.

**False positives:**

- later report treated as the full state,
- no recall treated as no experience,
- responsiveness treated as consciousness,
- unresponsiveness treated as absence,
- recovered memory treated as same-owner proof.

## 7. A5 - Identity Preservation

**Definition:** Identity preservation asks whether transformed continuity remains same-self rather than copy, fork, role, reconstruction, or replacement.

**Question:** When does transformed continuity remain the same owned stream?

**Relevant cases:**

- branching,
- copying,
- destructive upload,
- non-destructive copy,
- backup restore,
- memory persistence,
- sleep/waking,
- dementia,
- traumatic change,
- gradual transformation.

**Memory persistence boundary:** memory can support continuity, but copied memory does not by itself settle same-self persistence.

**False positives:**

- same memories,
- same behavior,
- same narrative,
- same data pattern,
- same report,
- social/legal identity mistaken for first-person continuation.

## 10. Empirical Pressure Points

- **DREAM / sleep-dream data:** pressures A4 through delayed report, no recall, no experience labels, and sleep/waking re-entry.
- **Propofol / anesthesia data:** pressures A4 through interruption, responsiveness loss/recovery, dose transitions, and possible covert consciousness.
- **No-report paradigms:** pressure A0/A4 by separating report from appearance indicators.
- **Body ownership illusions:** pressure A2 by altering mineness and embodiment without necessarily removing perception.
- **Depersonalization:** pressures A2/A4 by separating appearance from normal ownership.
- **Split-brain / branching thought experiments:** pressure A5 by separating pattern, report, and identity preservation.
- **Dreamless awareness / objectless awareness:** speculative pressure only; useful for A0/A3 questions, not proof.
