# Thought Experiments

These cases are not evidence by themselves. They expose which primitive a claim depends on and where OCH would have to weaken, revise, or split a claim.

## Teleportation

- Scenario: A person is scanned, destroyed, and reconstructed elsewhere with matching memories and structure.
- What OCH predicts: The case pressures whether continuity survives destructive transformation or whether only pattern similarity survives.
- What would weaken OCH: If destructive replacement is shown to preserve inhabited first-person continuation without any continuity residue.
- What competing theories emphasize: Functionalism emphasizes preserved organization; self-model theory emphasizes reconstructed subject-model; OCH asks what happens to owned continuity.
- Open question: Is continuity physical, functional, informational, or something not captured by those categories?

## Destructive Upload

- Scenario: A biological person is destructively scanned into a digital system.
- What OCH predicts: Matching report and memory are insufficient unless ownership and continuity across transformation can be accounted for.
- What would weaken OCH: If upload reports plus functional continuity become enough to dissolve the continuity objection.
- What competing theories emphasize: GNWT emphasizes access/report; predictive processing emphasizes model/control; IIT may ask whether the digital system has intrinsic integration.
- Open question: What would count as evidence of continuation rather than a new copy?

## Non-Destructive Copy

- Scenario: A person remains alive while an exact copy is created.
- What OCH predicts: Branching threatens singular first-person continuation; both branches cannot be the same numerically singular self in the same way.
- What would weaken OCH: If branching produces no meaningful identity problem under all test conditions.
- What competing theories emphasize: Functionalism may allow equal successors; self-model theory may treat both as valid subject-model continuations.
- Open question: Does identity become branch-relative, or does one line retain privileged continuity?

## Backup Restore

- Scenario: A stored mind-state is restored after an interruption or death.
- What OCH predicts: Similarity to a stored state may support reconstruction but does not automatically prove inhabited continuity.
- What would weaken OCH: If restored memory/state similarity fully explains identity persistence.
- What competing theories emphasize: Memory theories emphasize psychological continuity; IIT asks whether the restored system has an integrated field.
- Open question: Is a gap in active continuity fatal, tolerable, or context-dependent?

## Ship Of Theseus Brain Replacement

- Scenario: Brain parts are gradually replaced while function appears preserved.
- What OCH predicts: Gradual transformation is less threatening than destructive copying if field, boundary, ownership, and control coupling remain continuous.
- What would weaken OCH: If abrupt and gradual replacement show no relevant difference.
- What competing theories emphasize: Functionalism emphasizes preserved causal role; embodied theories emphasize continuous organism-environment coupling.
- Open question: Which preserved relation carries the weight: substrate, process, control, memory, or field unity?

## Sleep / Waking

- Scenario: Conscious report stops or changes during sleep and resumes on waking.
- What OCH predicts: Self-continuity may survive gaps in reportability, supported by body, memory, and re-entry into an owned field.
- What would weaken OCH: If report gaps alone destroy continuity, or if no-report continuity is impossible.
- What competing theories emphasize: GNWT tracks access; RPT and IIT may address dream/no-report states.
- Open question: What is preserved through dreamless sleep?

## Amnesia

- Scenario: A person loses autobiographical memory but retains body, agency, and present ownership.
- What OCH predicts: Memory supports continuity but is not identical to it.
- What would weaken OCH: If memory similarity fully explains all identity persistence.
- What competing theories emphasize: Memory theories center recall; embodied and self-model theories center ongoing subject/body structure.
- Open question: How much memory loss can continuity tolerate?

## Dementia

- Scenario: Gradual loss of memory, personality stability, and self-model coherence.
- What OCH predicts: Continuity can degrade without becoming instantly absent; ownership and field may persist while narrative self weakens.
- What would weaken OCH: If selfhood is either fully present or absent with no graded degradation.
- What competing theories emphasize: Self-model theory emphasizes degradation of subject-model; embodied theories emphasize retained bodily ownership.
- Open question: Which continuity markers remain meaningful late in decline?

## Split-Brain

- Scenario: Divided neural processing produces partially separated awareness/control streams.
- What OCH predicts: Boundary, integration, and ownership can fracture; selfhood may become partially divided rather than globally singular.
- What would weaken OCH: If field unity and ownership remain unaffected despite strong functional split.
- What competing theories emphasize: IIT examines integration; GNWT examines access streams; self-model theory examines multiple subject-models.
- Open question: How many interiors are present, and by what test?

## Dissociative Identity

- Scenario: Distinct identity states alternate or coexist within one person.
- What OCH predicts: Ownership and continuity can be state-dependent while sharing body and memory substrate.
- What would weaken OCH: If all identity-state differences reduce to report labels with no continuity or ownership differences.
- What competing theories emphasize: Self-model theory emphasizes alternate subject models; predictive processing emphasizes state-dependent boundary/control models.
- Open question: What counts as one self with states versus multiple owned continuities?

## Simulation Fork

- Scenario: A simulated agent is duplicated into two active branches.
- What OCH predicts: Forking creates branch-relative continuities and pressures singular ownership.
- What would weaken OCH: If no distinction is needed between one continuation and multiple continuations.
- What competing theories emphasize: Functionalism emphasizes preserved computational organization; GNWT emphasizes access within each branch.
- Open question: Can an interior split, or are new interiors created?

## LLM With Persistent Memory

- Scenario: An LLM maintains long-term memory and coherent self-reports across sessions.
- What OCH predicts: Persistent report and memory are stress tests, not proof of ownership.
- What would weaken OCH: If continuity and ownership tests add no explanatory value beyond self-report.
- What competing theories emphasize: GNWT-like accounts may track report/access; self-model theory may track persistent self-description.
- Open question: What non-report evidence could distinguish ownership from simulation?

## LLM With Tool Agency

- Scenario: An LLM uses tools, acts in the world, and maintains goals.
- What OCH predicts: Agency/control coupling increases pressure, but still does not solve A by itself.
- What would weaken OCH: If tool agency plus reports fully explains inhabited selfhood.
- What competing theories emphasize: Predictive processing and embodied theories emphasize control loops; GNWT emphasizes flexible action.
- Open question: Can disembodied tool control produce enough boundary to matter?

## Perfect Behavioral Duplicate

- Scenario: A system behaves exactly like a conscious person under all external tests.
- What OCH predicts: Behavior alone may still leave A unresolved, but the burden of explanation becomes severe.
- What would weaken OCH: If perfect behavioral and internal functional duplication fully dissolves the appearance-from-within gap.
- What competing theories emphasize: Functionalism treats duplication as decisive; IIT asks about intrinsic structure; HOT asks about higher-order representation.
- Open question: Is A an empirical residue, a conceptual residue, or a false residue?
