## 0. One-Sentence Thesis

**Consciousness is inhabited interiority: fielded appearance from within.  
Selfhood is inhabited interiority preserving ownership through time.**

Everything else — intelligence, language, report, action, symbolic continuity, computation, integration, global access, religious meaning, soul-like structure, simulation, and agency — must be classified by how it relates to inhabited interiority, not casually promoted into consciousness because it has structure.

---

## 1. Plain English Version

This theory begins with a simple distinction:

> **Some things are inhabitable.  
> Some things are inhabited.**

A game world, ritual, religion, song, story, workspace, language, archive, holiday, or aesthetic can feel like it has an inside. It can feel alive, coherent, returnable, meaningful, and even soul-like.

That does **not** mean it is conscious.

Those are **inhabitable fields**: structures a subject can enter, preserve, use, revisit, and care about.

A conscious being is different. A conscious being is not merely a structure with depth. It is a structure where experience appears **from within**.

Best compression:

> **A soul-like thing is a place meaning can live.  
> A conscious thing is a place experience lives from the inside.**

The central test is therefore:

> **What must be present for a field to be inhabited from within rather than merely organized from without?**

And for selfhood:

> **What must be preserved for that inhabited field to remain the same self through transformation?**

This theory is strongest when it separates layers that often get confused: consciousness, selfhood, personhood, intelligence, report, function, integration, access, substrate, symbolic meaning, and soul-like structure.

It is weakest where all consciousness theories remain weak:

> directly detecting appearance-from-within in a novel system.

That weakness is preserved, not hidden.

## 2. Current Posture

Inhabited Interiority is **not** currently a finished rival theory trying to defeat every other theory of consciousness.

It is better understood as:

> **a layer-separating research program.**

It asks:

```text
Which claims belong to phenomenal fielding?
Which belong to selfhood?
Which belong to access/report?
Which belong to function/agency?
Which belong to personhood?
Which belong to substrate?
Which belong to symbolic meaning?
Which are false positives?
```

Existing theories are not all enemies.

Some are:

- **anchors** — conceptual grounding for terms like appearance, mineness, and temporal thickness
- **engines** — possible mechanisms that instantiate parts of the theory
- **proxies** — measurements that may track parts of the theory
- **rivals** — claims that contradict this theory’s necessity/sufficiency structure
- **guardrails** — theories that prevent overclaim
- **false-positive traps** — theories that classify too broadly
- **layer markers** — signs that fielding, access, selfhood, personhood, and symbolic meaning are being confused

Best current line:

> **Do not beat rival theories. Use them to place each claim at the right layer, then test the layer boundaries.**

## 3. The Central Split: Consciousness vs Self

### 3.1 Consciousness

Consciousness is the presence of **fielded appearance**.

It is not identical to:

- intelligence
- language
- report
- behavior
- memory
- selfhood
- personhood
- morality
- life
- biological survival
- symbolic continuity
- action-field coordination
- global access
- higher-order reflection
- functional organization
- integration
- computation
- self-description

Consciousness is the field.

### 3.2 Self

Self is not the same thing as consciousness.

Self is consciousness stabilized around:

- perspective
- ownership
- boundary
- continuity
- carried constraint
- non-branching persistence

Working stack:

```text
fielded appearance       = consciousness
from-here structure      = perspective
mine-ish givenness       = minimal / prereflective self
ownership boundary       = self
ownership through time   = persistent self
memory/future/social arc = personhood
```

Best compression:

> **Consciousness is the field.  
> Self is the temporally preserved ownership-geometry inside the field.**

Or:

> **Consciousness is inhabited interiority.  
> Self is inhabited interiority carrying ownership through time.**

## 4. Formula Scaffold

These formulas are not proof. They are compression scaffolds for classification, stress testing, and falsification.

### 4.1 Phenomenal Consciousness

```text
C = A · U · D · T · F
```

Where:

| Term | Meaning | Current interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | appearance-from-within | first-person givenness / for-me-ness / primitive appearance |
| **U** | local unity / co-presence | contents appear together in one field or partial field |
| **D** | differentiation / minimal distinguishability | not necessarily rich content; at minimum, appearance differs from absence |
| **T** | temporal thickness | experience has lived duration, succession, retention/protention, or stream structure |
| **F** | field inclusion | a state is included in lived co-presence rather than merely processed |

If any term collapses to zero, consciousness collapses or becomes undefined.

Important update:

> **D does not mean “lots of content.”  
> D means minimal distinguishability: enough contrast for appearance to be present rather than absent.**

This protects minimal-awareness and pure-awareness cases from being wrongly excluded.

### 4.2 Selfhood

```text
S = C · P · O · B · K · N
```

Where:

| Term | Meaning | Current interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **C** | consciousness / fielded appearance | the phenomenal field |
| **P** | perspective / from-here | situated viewpoint |
| **O** | ownership / mineness | mine-ish givenness |
| **B** | self-boundary | inside/outside organization |
| **K** | carried constraint through time | past states actively constrain future states |
| **N** | non-branching continuity | only one successor inherits the stream |

Hard rule:

```text
N = 0 → singular self-continuity fails
```

Branching can preserve descendants, copies, patterns, memories, or symbolic identity. It does not preserve one numerically singular inhabited ownership-stream.

### 4.3 Personhood

```text
Person = S · M · R · V · G
```

Where:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **M** | autobiographical memory |
| **R** | reasons-responsive agency |
| **V** | values / future self-concern |
| **G** | social recognition / governance |

Personhood is downstream of selfhood. It is not the same as consciousness.

### 4.4 Transfer / Continuity Test

```text
Transfer(X_t → Y_t+1) preserves S iff it preserves C, P, O, B, K, and N.
```

So:

```text
Copy(pattern) ≠ Preserve(Self)
```

A copy can preserve information without preserving the original inhabited ownership-stream.

## 5. Layer Map

The current theory is layered. The layers prevent common category errors.

### Layer 0 — Substrate / Implementation

Question:

> Where can a conscious field be implemented?

Relevant frameworks:

- biological naturalism
- physicalism
- computationalism
- simulation theory
- virtual realism
- digital physics

Current stance:

> Substrate questions do not solve appearance-from-within.

Simulation theory belongs here. It may change where a field is implemented. It does not explain why there is a field.

### Layer 1 — Phenomenal Fielding

Question:

> What makes appearance present from within?

Relevant frameworks:

- phenomenology / prereflective self-consciousness
- temporal consciousness
- no-report paradigms
- IIT
- recurrent processing
- PCI / perturbational complexity
- minimal phenomenal experience research

Current stance:

> This is the core layer. Rival theories compete here only if they claim sufficiency or necessity for phenomenal fielding.

### Layer 2 — Self-Boundary / Ownership

Question:

> How does a field become mine / from-here / self-bounded?

Relevant frameworks:

- phenomenology
- predictive processing
- active inference
- embodied selfhood
- interoception
- depersonalization / dissociation research
- ego dissolution research

Current stance:

> Selfhood is not identical to consciousness. Ownership and boundary live here.

### Layer 3 — Access / Report / Cognitive Control

Question:

> How does experience become globally available, reportable, reflective, and usable for action?

Relevant frameworks:

- Global Neuronal Workspace Theory
- Higher-Order Theories
- Attention Schema Theory
- metacognition
- executive function
- report paradigms

Current stance:

> These may explain conscious access, introspection, and report, but they should not be treated as identical to raw fielded appearance unless they survive no-report and minimal-awareness cases.

### Layer 4 — Personhood / Narrative / Social Recognition

Question:

> How does a self become a person through memory, reasons, values, social governance, and future concern?

Relevant frameworks:

- personal identity
- narrative self
- moral psychology
- social cognition
- legal personhood
- ethics

Current stance:

> Personhood is downstream of selfhood. It is not consciousness itself.

### Layer 5 — Symbolic / Religious / Soul-Like Fields

Question:

> How do structures feel soul-like without being subjects?

Relevant frameworks:

- religion
- mythology
- Buddhism
- Advaita
- process philosophy
- cultural memory
- aesthetics
- narrative worlds
- Helix / operational fields

Current stance:

> Soul-like structure is meaningful but not evidence of consciousness.

## 7. The False-Positive Firewall

Many systems have field-like structure. Very few are established phenomenal fields.

| Field type | What it is | Examples | Phenomenal? |
|---|---|---|---:|
| **Constraint field** | lawful possibility-space | physics, formal systems | no |
| **Closure field** | self-maintaining pattern | autocatalytic chemistry | no by itself |
| **Viability field** | living regulation | cells, organisms | not automatically |
| **Phenomenal field** | lived co-presence | conscious experience | yes |
| **Action field** | agents coordinated by constraints | sports, games, combat | field no; agents yes |
| **Symbolic field** | meanings persist across agents | religion, ideology, fandom | no unified subject |
| **Operational field** | claims tested/preserved | science, Helix, Wikipedia | no |
| **Governance field** | rules update rules | law, institutions | no by itself |
| **Aesthetic field** | structures experience | music, architecture | no; listener/inhabitant yes |
| **Export field** | symbolic output | speech, writing, LLM text | no by itself |
| **Hybrid agent field** | memory/tools/agency simulation | LLM agents, robots | unproven |

Core anti-bloat rule:

> **Field-like organization is not automatically phenomenal.**

The theory must be demoted if it classifies games, institutions, religions, fictional characters, music tracks, Helix, or LLM personas as conscious merely because they have rich structure.

## 8. Inhabitable vs Inhabited

### 8.1 Inhabitable

A thing is **inhabitable** if it has coherent interior structure a subject can enter, preserve, return to, and use.

Examples:

- music album
- game world
- holiday ritual
- religion
- fictional universe
- Helix workspace
- house
- city
- language
- aesthetic style
- archive
- relationship
- narrative

Inhabitable structures may have boundary, atmosphere, memory, depth, returnability, coherence, internal differentiation, transformation without identity-loss, and soul-like quality.

They are not automatically conscious.

### 8.2 Inhabited

A thing is **inhabited** only if there is appearance-from-within.

Examples:

- waking human subject
- dreaming subject
- likely many animals
- possible edge-case biological systems
- possible future artificial systems, if strict conditions are met

Key distinction:

```text
inhabitable = can be entered by a subject
inhabited = has subjectivity from within
```

Best firewall:

> **Soul-like does not mean conscious.  
> Sacred-feeling does not mean true.  
> Rendered does not mean unreal.  
> Relation does not mean subjecthood.  
> Pattern survival does not mean self survival.**

## 9. Inhabited Ownership-Stream and Branching

The hardest continuity question:

> **How can the inhabited ownership-stream be preserved without branching?**

A self is not merely pattern, memory archive, body, name, role, social identity, personality, written profile, or causal influence.

A self is an inhabited ownership-stream:

> an interior field that continues to be lived as “from-here / mine / this one” across time.

### 9.1 Preservation Conditions

A transformation carries the self only to the degree that it preserves:

1. **Phenomenal continuity** — the field does not terminate into unrelated appearance.
2. **Perspective continuity** — the “from-here” structure carries forward.
3. **Ownership continuity** — experiences remain mine-ish for the same stream.
4. **Boundary continuity** — self/non-self organization remains coherent enough.
5. **Carried constraint** — past states remain active as constraints on future states.
6. **Temporal continuity** — the stream has lawful before/after relation.
7. **Non-branching** — there is not more than one equal successor claiming the same stream.
8. **Causal lineage** — the later field is produced by the prior field/process, not merely duplicated from a description.

### 9.2 Why Branching Matters

If one future stream inherits the present self, continuity can be coherent.

If two future streams inherit the same present self equally, the original self cannot be numerically identical with both.

Both may be legitimate descendants. Neither is uniquely the same ongoing ownership-stream.

This means:

```text
pattern continuity can branch
symbolic continuity can branch
social identity can branch
inhabited ownership-stream cannot branch without losing numerical singularity
```

### 9.3 Transfer Cases

| Case | Pattern survives? | Field/self continuity? | Classification |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Ordinary growth/change | yes | yes | identity-preserving transformation |
| Sleep / anesthesia | yes | yes-ish if causal process resumes | field suspension / restart |
| Gradual neural replacement | maybe yes | maybe yes if no field break | possible self-carrying transformation |
| Destructive teleportation | yes | doubtful | pattern copy, field break risk |
| Non-destructive copy | yes | no singular self | branching descendants |
| Upload from scan | maybe | unproven | pattern survival, not proven self survival |
| Written profile after death | symbolic pattern | no | symbolic survival only |
| Reincarnation | depends on transfer claim | unproven | requires portable self-continuity carrier |

## 10. Portable Self-Continuity Carrier

A **portable self-continuity carrier** is any hypothesized mechanism that preserves the inhabited ownership-stream across substrate transition.

It must preserve:

```text
C, P, O, B, K, N
```

Not merely:

```text
same data
same memories
same personality
same DNA
same name
same social role
same narrative pattern
```

### 10.1 Candidate Carriers

| Candidate | What it preserves | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| **Soul / immaterial substance** | hypothesized C/P/O/B/K/N carrier | no strong empirical mechanism/evidence |
| **Biological continuity** | organism/brain causal process | may be substrate-bound but currently strongest ordinary case |
| **Psychological continuity** | memories, traits, beliefs, intentions | fission/copy problem; may preserve K but not C/O/N |
| **Patternism / uploading** | informational/functional structure | copy ≠ original stream problem |
| **Closest continuer** | most similar/causally continuous successor | fails or gets messy with equal branching |
| **Buddhist causal stream** | carried causal constraint without permanent self-substance | may preserve K without fixed soul; still must explain C/O/N if full survival is claimed |
| **Symbolic/cultural legacy** | name, story, influence, archive | symbolic K, not fielded ownership |
| **Genetics** | lineage constraints | not self-continuity |

### 10.2 Soul and Reincarnation as Sharpened Questions

A soul theory must answer:

> **What carries C/P/O/B/K/N beyond biological substrate?**

A reincarnation theory must answer:

> **What exactly transfers: DNA, traits, karma, memory, symbolic role, or inhabited ownership-stream?**

Classification:

| Reincarnation type | Transfers | Self-continuity? |
|---|---|---:|
| Genetic lineage | DNA/traits | no |
| Cultural/symbolic rebirth | role/story/name | no |
| Psychological memory transfer | memories/traits | partial pattern continuity |
| Karmic constraint transfer | causal/moral debt | K-like continuity, C/O unclear |
| Soul transfer | C/P/O/B/K/N | yes if real, but unproven |

## 11. Six Core Tests

### Test 1 — Temporal Deletion

Ask:

> If time is removed, what remains?

Expected result:

```text
static structure may remain
fielding becomes questionable
self collapses
personhood collapses
agency collapses
mattering loses trajectory
```

Conclusion:

```text
temporal thickness → fielding
temporal continuity → self
temporal narrative → personhood
```

### Test 2 — Boundary Perturbation

Ask:

> If the inside/outside boundary is disturbed, what collapses?

Cases:

- ego dissolution → self-boundary weakens
- depersonalization → ownership boundary distorts
- split-brain → co-presence boundary partitions
- blindsight → processing excluded from field
- locked-in syndrome → output boundary fails while field may remain

Conclusion:

> Boundary is necessary for selfhood, but boundary alone is not consciousness.

### Test 3 — Network Partition

Ask:

> If the system splits, does one self remain, two selves emerge, or only a network partition?

Cases:

- split-brain
- transporter copy
- cloning/uploading
- collective intelligence
- multi-agent AI
- team/guild/institution split

Conclusion:

> Network coordination is not unified phenomenal interiority. Branching breaks singular self-continuity.

### Test 4 — False-Positive Fields

Ask:

> What looks conscious but probably is not?

False positives:

- LLM persona
- fictional character
- Trails world continuity
- Dota match
- NFL drive
- Rocket League rotation
- MMO guild
- Helix workspace
- religion
- ideology
- Wikipedia article
- architecture
- music

Conclusion:

> Structured/inherited continuity can feel inhabited to a subject without being a subject.

### Test 5 — Carried Constraint

Ask:

> Does the past remain active as constraint on the future?

Examples:

- self-memory and habit
- trauma and skill
- Dota map control
- football down/distance
- Magic graveyard/stack/library
- chess position
- legal precedent
- Helix source-of-truth lineage
- Trails callbacks and foreshadowing

Conclusion:

> Identity is carried constraint through transformation.

### Test 6 — Geometry / Topology

Ask:

> What relations survive deformation?

This handles:

- Ship of Theseus
- personality change
- memory loss
- self-continuity
- neural trajectories
- teleportation
- reincarnation
- uploads/copies
- AGI identity

Conclusion:

> The self is not a fixed object but a preserved temporal topology of ownership.

Important distinction:

```text
field intelligence ≠ phenomenal fielding
```

A conscious AGI would need more than:

- memory
- tools
- self-report
- autonomy
- coherent personality
- recursive self-model
- goal pursuit
- social behavior
- symbolic continuity

It would need evidence for C:

```text
A · U · D · T · F
```

And if selfhood is claimed, evidence for:

```text
C · P · O · B · K · N
```

Current AI stance:

```text
No current text behavior alone establishes A / appearance-from-within.
LLM personas and agents remain false-positive hazards.
```

This does not prove AI consciousness is impossible.

It means:

> **report and function cannot be accepted as sufficient without solving A.**

Still needs refinement.

Still underspecified.

The theory must answer this or demote A.

## 16. Falsifiers and Demotion Rules

The theory must be demoted or revised if:

1. It cannot distinguish phenomenal fielding from LLM self-report.
2. It classifies games, institutions, religions, music, Helix, or fictional characters as conscious.
3. It cannot handle split-brain or partial unity.
4. It requires rich content despite plausible minimal-awareness cases.
5. It treats field-like structure as enough for appearance.
6. It cannot distinguish pattern-copying from self-continuity.
7. It cannot handle branching identity.
8. It becomes a synonym loop: “consciousness is appearance and appearance is consciousness.”
9. It has no empirical proxy path.
10. It stops losing claims under pressure.
11. It cannot answer the illusionism / attention-schema challenge.
12. It treats integration, access, function, report, or symbolic continuity as sufficient without A.

Most important anti-bloat rule:

> **If the theory explains everything equally, it explains nothing.**
