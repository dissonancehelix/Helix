# Hardness Protocol
## Inhabited Interiority Research Program

Version: 1.0  
Status: Binding  
Last revised: 2026-04-26

This document defines the hard constraints that govern classification, scoring,
claim status, and report generation. It cannot be overridden by plausibility
arguments, elegant architecture, or operator preference.

---

## Hard definitions

**Givenness (G)**: Appearance-from-within. Not derivable from intelligence, report,
behavior, memory, field structure, symbolic continuity, agency, optimization, social
coordination, or complexity. Not directly detectable. May be primitive.

**Phenomenal field**: A field in which states are given from within. Not equivalent
to information-processing field, action field, symbolic field, or governance field.

**Self-continuity**: Inhabited interiority carrying ownership through time with
non-branching causal continuity.

**Pattern continuity**: A structural pattern persisting across transformation without
established field continuity. Not self-continuity.

**Symbolic continuity**: Information, memory, or recorded identity persisting across
transformation without established ownership continuity. Not self-continuity.

**Branching**: One causal process becoming more than one. Fatal to singular self-continuity.
N = 0 when branching occurs. This cannot be reversed by arguing that "the original" has
a stronger claim — both resulting processes have N = 0 by definition.

**Portable carrier**: A substrate that, if transferred to, preserves inhabited ownership —
not merely information, memory, or pattern. Current candidates: unproven.

**Primitive residue**: The point where conceptual analysis reaches a term (G) that cannot
be further analyzed into more fundamental components. Analysis stops here. Empirical proxy
testing takes over, but cannot resolve the residue.

## Forbidden classifications

These outputs are **never valid** in this research program:

- **"proved"** / **"confirmed"** / **"established beyond doubt"** / **"demonstrated"**
  for any claim about consciousness or self-continuity. Use: candidate, weakened, broken,
  unresolved.

- **"conscious"** as a binary verdict for any novel or contested case. Use the C score
  with a classification label and confidence.

- **"G is present"** inferred solely from intelligence, language, self-report, memory,
  field-like structure, symbolic continuity, agency, optimization, social coordination,
  or complexity. G must be supported by convergent proxies or by established-conscious
  comparison cases.

- **"branching restored"** or **"singular self-continuity preserved despite branching"**
  for any case where N = 0. Branching is definitionally fatal.

- **"pattern continuity is sufficient"** for self-continuity. Pattern survival ≠ field
  survival ≠ self survival.

- **"the theory predicts this"** as a response to evidence that does not fit. If evidence
  does not fit, the options are: (a) mark a break, (b) mark unresolved, (c) revise the
  claim with demotion.

## Kill conditions

The research program must pause and reset if any of the following occurs:

**KL-1: Unfalsifiable absorption**  
Every disconfirmation is reinterpreted as evidence for the theory. The theory has
no distinct prediction from its negation on any tested case. → Mark all Tier 1 claims
as broken. Rebuild from scratch with a more restricted model.

**KL-2: Score inflation**  
Preserved breaks are being patched by inflating component scores rather than recording
the break. The scoring function is being used as a rhetorical tool rather than a
classification tool. → Audit all scores. Restore to neutral scoring; re-record breaks.

**KL-3: Structural expansion without case coverage**  
The architecture has grown (new terms, new formulas, new dimensions) without a
corresponding expansion of tested cases. The model is becoming more complex without
becoming more tested. → Freeze expansion. Fill existing cases before adding new structure.

**KL-4: False-positive atlas collapses**  
Cases that should be false positives are being reclassified as phenomenal candidates
under new interpretations of G. The false-positive firewall has been breached. →
Restore all false-positive verdicts. Audit the new interpretation for absorption.

## Claim status rules

| Status | Meaning | Conditions |
|--------|---------|------------|
| candidate | Claim is active and untested or partially tested | Default for new claims |
| weakened | Claim survives but with significant modifications | Failed at least one test; survived with reduced scope |
| broken | Claim does not survive the test battery | Failed a core test; no salvage via score revision |
| unresolved | Cannot be decided with current tools | Test applied; result underdetermined |
| promoted_for_testing_only | Moving to more rigorous testing | Never mark as confirmed; still under scrutiny |

**Claim promotion rules**:
No claim may move from candidate → promoted_for_testing_only without:
1. Surviving all six stress tests
2. Surviving rival theory comparison (performing better than at least 3 rivals)
3. Surviving false-positive scan (no false positive reclassified as supporting)
4. Having at least 3 edge cases classified correctly with preserved breaks intact

**Claim demotion rules**:
Any claim must be demoted if:
1. A preserved break cannot be resolved after 2+ research iterations
2. A rival theory handles the same case battery with fewer classification errors
3. A false positive collapses the firewall for that claim

## Empirical proxy map

**G / givenness**:
- Status: primitive_residue — no direct detection possible
- Indirect proxies: PCI/TMS-EEG elevation (Casali et al. 2013); covert consciousness
  detection (Owen et al. 2006); retrospective report from established-conscious comparison
  cases (locked-in syndrome); no-report paradigm dissociations (Tsuchiya et al. 2015)
- Forbidden proxies: self-report alone; intelligence; language production; behavioral
  output; information integration; symbolic continuity
- Scoring rule: G = null unless supported by convergent proxies. G = 0 is an inference
  from absence of proxies, not a derived conclusion.

**U / unity**:
- Proxies: co-presence reports (what is simultaneously given); integration metrics;
  split-brain partition evidence; thalamocortical coherence measures
- Ceiling case: waking human (G=1.0, U=0.9 ceiling to preserve split-brain caveat)

**D / differentiation**:
- Proxies: qualitative content richness; neural state complexity; discrimination
  capacity; EEG differentiation; PCI complexity component
- Zero case: absence seizure (spike-and-wave → D collapses)

**T / temporal thickness**:
- Proxies: specious present duration estimates; temporal integration; duration judgment
  tasks; continuous state maintenance
- Zero case: propofol (subjects report no passage of time)

**F / field inclusion**:
- Proxies: no-report paradigm dissociations; blindsight contrasts (processing without
  field inclusion); access vs. phenomenal consciousness dissociations
- Critical case: blindsight (visual processing, F = 0 for the scotoma)

**For self-continuity**:
- P / perspective: bodily anchoring; spatiotemporal orientation; disrupted in
  depersonalization and dissolution
- O / ownership: "mineness" reports; disrupted in depersonalization; absent in
  symbolic/pattern continuity cases
- B / boundary: self/other discrimination; body ownership; disrupted in ego dissolution
- K / carried constraint: behavioral constraint from past; memory; skill; value structure
- N / non-branching: causal lineage structure; branching detection via case structure
- R / restart continuity: same causal process resume; physiological continuity

**For perturbability / field robustness**:
- PCI response to TMS perturbation
- EEG complexity recovery after perturbation
- Response diversity and complexity under stimulation

## Promotion and demotion rules (summary)

Full rules in RESEARCH_PROGRAM.md. Brief:

- **Promote**: Survived full edge-case battery + 3 rival theories + false-positive scan.
  Mark as promoted_for_testing_only, not confirmed.

- **Demote**: Failed a core test (break cannot be resolved). Mark as weakened or broken.

- **Break preservation**: A break is a research output. It must be recorded in the
  relevant fixture's `breaks_preserved` field and in the claim's `hostile_cases` list.
  It may never be removed by score adjustment alone.

- **Score revision**: Scores may be revised if new evidence changes the classification.
  The revision must be logged with a rationale. The break must be preserved even if
  the score changes.
