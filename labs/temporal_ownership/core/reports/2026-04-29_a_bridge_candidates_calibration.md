## 4. IIT / Intrinsic Integration Bridge

Claim shape:

> Appearance-from-within may arise when a system has intrinsic causal/integrated structure sufficient to be a field from its own point of view.

What it claims to bridge:

- binding/integration -> intrinsic field -> possible appearance.

Explains well:

- why consciousness might require more than input/output behavior,
- why global report may not be enough,
- why anesthesia/sleep/integration changes matter,
- why field unity is not the same as a list of processed features.

Fails to explain:

- ownership,
- selfhood,
- temporal continuity,
- reportability,
- why intrinsic integration is appearance rather than structure.

Strongest support:

IIT-like approaches are at least aimed at intrinsic structure rather than external behavior. They may be the closest bridge from "organized system" to "field candidate."

Strongest false positive:

```text
integrated structure = appearance-from-within
```

Strongest falsifier:

Integration metrics fail to distinguish conscious/no-conscious states better than access, recurrent, or control accounts across hard cases.

Best dataset pressure point:

- COGITATE for IIT/GNWT comparison later.
- Anesthesia connectivity/integration later if derivative or imaging data becomes local.

Relation to TOM:

IIT can pressure the field/binding side. TOM still asks whether the field has ownership through time.

Relation to ICT:

Could help with consciousness-as-field, but not self-as-owned-continuity by itself.

Relation to DCP/LIP/EIP:

LIP blocks inferring A from integration alone. DCP may model state transitions in integration. EIP tracks post-transition residue. None proves the field is owned.

How/why status:

- Strong on how-binding.
- Potentially relevant to metaphysical-why.
- Still risks renaming A as integration.

Does it narrow A?

Yes, modestly, if treated as an intrinsic-field candidate. No, if treated as automatic solution.

## 5. RPT / Recurrent Presence Bridge

Claim shape:

> Appearance may require recurrent processing that stabilizes perceptual presence before or without full report.

What it claims to bridge:

- recurrent perceptual processing -> perceptual presence.

Explains well:

- visual masking,
- no-report perceptual presence,
- blindsight contrasts,
- why feedforward behavior may not equal experience.

Fails to explain:

- pain,
- emotion,
- minimal experience,
- dream selfhood,
- temporal identity,
- ownership beyond perceptual presence.

Strongest support:

It directly attacks the report substitute by locating possible experience before full global access.

Strongest false positive:

```text
recurrent perceptual processing = all appearance
```

Strongest falsifier:

Recurrent processing fails to predict perceptual presence better than access/report accounts or cannot generalize beyond vision.

Best dataset pressure point:

- COGITATE later for perceptual/access comparison.
- DREAM metadata only indirectly pressures recurrent presence through sleep/dream report categories.

Relation to TOM:

Useful for content/presence; weak on owned continuity.

Relation to ICT:

May help with consciousness side, not the self-continuity side.

Relation to DCP/LIP/EIP:

LIP blocks using report absence as absence. DCP may model stabilization of perceptual state. EIP tracks delayed report residue.

How/why status:

- Strong on how-perceptual-presence.
- Weak on functional-why outside perception.
- Does not solve metaphysical-why.

Does it narrow A?

Yes for perceptual A; not enough for general A.

## 6. Embodied Valence Bridge

Claim shape:

> Appearance may require valenced stakes: pain, hunger, fatigue, homeostasis, threat, body-state pressure.

What it claims to bridge:

- body-state pressure / stakes -> felt mattering -> appearance.

Explains well:

- pain,
- fatigue,
- affect,
- threat,
- why experience seems for-someone rather than neutral processing.

Fails to explain:

- neutral visual experience,
- abstract thought,
- minimal experience,
- whether valence appears or merely prioritizes control.

Strongest support:

Valence gives the strongest "why it matters" pressure. It also connects to actual body-state variables and affect measures.

Strongest false positive:

```text
stakes = feeling
```

Strongest falsifier:

Valence/homeostatic variables predict behavior but fail to distinguish experienced from non-experienced states.

Best dataset pressure point:

- ds004902 metadata has sleepiness, mood, fatigue-adjacent questionnaire fields.
- DREAM report labels may later be mapped to affect if underlying subsets include affect/dream content fields.

Relation to TOM:

Supports temporal ownership markers because valenced stakes make transformations matter to a system.

Relation to ICT:

May strengthen ownership evidence but does not prove appearance.

Relation to DCP/LIP/EIP:

LIP warns that behavioral fatigue is incomplete evidence. DCP models state-pressure transitions. EIP tracks mood/vigilance residue after sleep deprivation.

How/why status:

- Strong on functional-why.
- Medium on how-consciousness.
- Weak on metaphysical-why.

Does it narrow A?

It narrows the space by identifying stakes as a serious neighbor, but risks explaining mattering rather than appearing.

Relation to DCP/LIP/EIP:

LIP maps hidden-state uncertainty. DCP maps action/commitment under uncertainty. EIP tracks residue after learning/transition.

How/why status:

- Strong on how-control and functional-why.
- Weak on metaphysical-why.

Does it narrow A?

It narrows selfhood and agency; it may not narrow raw appearance.

LIP frames uncertainty before interruption/report. DCP models transition across sleep/anesthesia/decision boundaries. EIP tracks residue after re-entry.

How/why status:

- Strong on ownership-continuity how.
- Medium on functional-why.
- Weak on metaphysical-why.

Does it narrow A?

It narrows owned A, not raw A. It explains the owner better than the appearing.
