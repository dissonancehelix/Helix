# Roadmap to Serious Theory
## Inhabited Continuity Theory — Development Path

**Status:** Pre-scientific conceptual framework. Promising architecture, provisional claims, no empirical confirmation.

**Goal:** Develop ICT into a serious, competitive theory of conscious selfhood and owned continuity — not a complete theory of consciousness, which is a larger problem ICT does not yet address. The theory's current strength is the selfhood / ownership / identity-through-time cluster. That is the target.

**Core compression:**
- Consciousness is appearance.
- Self is owned continuity.
- The question ICT must answer: can owned continuity do explanatory work that memory, reportability, integration, and self-modeling alone cannot?

**Warning at the start:** The theory does not become serious by sounding deep. It becomes serious by making predictions that can fail. Every stage below is about sharpening exposure, not building insulation.

---

## Stage 1 — Conceptual Lockdown

**Goal:** Get the primitives right before building anything on them.

The theory currently uses several terms that do the work of multiple concepts without distinguishing them. This must be fixed before the theory can generate predictions.

**Required separations:**

*Consciousness vs selfhood.* These can dissociate. Consciousness (appearance-from-within, the fact that there is something it is like) may be present when selfhood is weak or absent. Selfhood (a self is a bounded, owned continuity-stream) requires more than consciousness. Getting this separation right is the single most important conceptual move in Stage 1.

*Appearance vs reportability.* What appears to the system is not identical to what the system can report. Locked-in syndrome is the clearest case: appearance may continue while reportability is catastrophically reduced. ICT must not use reportability as a proxy for appearance.

*Ownership vs memory.* Ownership (this experience is occurring as mine) is not the same as memory similarity (this experience is continuous with past states I can recall). A person with severe amnesia may still have owned experience of the present moment. ICT must specify what ownership requires beyond memory.

*Ownership vs integration.* High integration (globally broadcast information, IIT phi) does not guarantee ownership. A highly integrated system might produce experience without producing a self. ICT claims ownership is a distinct primitive, not a consequence of integration.

*Identity through time vs momentary selfhood.* A system can have instantaneous owned experience without having the temporal continuity that constitutes a self-over-time. These are different claims. ICT must not conflate them.

**Output of Stage 1:**
- Revised PRIMITIVES.md with each primitive independently defined and distinguished from adjacent concepts
- Updated CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml with every claim assigned a tier
- No undefined terms left in THEORY.md

## Stage 2 — Claim Ledger Discipline

**Goal:** No claim may remain unclassified. Speculation must be labeled before it spreads.

**Tiers in use:**
- `philosophical_primitive` — organizes intuitions, not directly empirically falsifiable
- `empirical_candidate` — makes predictions testable in principle with existing or near-future methods
- `analogy` — illuminates the structure of a principle from another domain; does not prove it applies here
- `speculation` — plausible extension of the framework with no current empirical anchor
- `open_question` — genuinely unresolvable with current tools; flagged to prevent speculative sprawl
- `empirical_pressure` — an external result or finding that directly challenges a claim; must be engaged, not ignored

**Rules:**
1. Every claim in CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml must have a tier, status, risks, and at least one falsifier.
2. Claims marked `philosophical_primitive` are not confirmed by intuition matching alone — they must correctly predict intuitions across a sufficient range of cases.
3. Claims marked `empirical_candidate` must have an operationalization path — how would you test this?
4. No claim may be promoted from `speculation` to `empirical_candidate` without a proposed test design.
5. `empirical_pressure` entries must have a `response_required` field. Ignoring empirical pressure is not permitted.

**Current ledger state:** 15 claims (cl_001–cl_015) plus 6 new claims (cl_016–cl_021). See CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml.

## Stage 3 — Theory Comparison

**Goal:** Know exactly what ICT explains better, equally, or worse than existing theories, and why.

This is not a competition. It is a map. ICT needs to know where it stands relative to:

**Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT)**
Strong on: access consciousness, the broadcasting of information. Weak on: owned experience, selfhood, identity through time. ICT's contribution: GNWT does not explain why globally broadcast information feels like *mine*. Ownership is not global access.

**Integrated Information Theory (IIT)**
Strong on: intrinsic causal power, integrated differentiated structure. Weak on: selfhood, temporal continuity, branching. IIT phi does not predict whether branching occurs or whether two integrated systems share a prior self. ICT's contribution: integration may be necessary for consciousness but is not sufficient for selfhood.

**Predictive Processing / Active Inference**
Strong on: self-model as prediction machine, ego dissolution as precision loosening. Weak on: owned continuity over time, why the self-prior feels owned rather than merely predicted. ICT's contribution: the self-prior must be not just accurate but *bound* — it must be mine, not merely a model of me.

**Higher-Order Theories (HOT)**
Strong on: the difference between first-order states and states one is aware of having. Weak on: temporal continuity, ownership, identity. ICT's contribution: a higher-order representation of a state does not make that state owned — ownership requires more than meta-representation.

**Recurrent Processing Theory**
Strong on: phenomenal consciousness as recurrent feedback loops. Weak on: selfhood, temporal continuity. ICT engages this less directly; recurrent processing addresses the structure of appearance more than ownership.

**Self-Model Theory (Metzinger)**
Closest competitor to ICT on selfhood. Strong on: transparent self-model, phenomenal self-model disruptions. Weak on (from ICT's perspective): Metzinger denies that anyone is there — the self is a process with no entity behind it. ICT's position differs: it treats owned experience as real, not as a transparent model mistaken for reality. The disagreement is substantive and must be specified precisely.

**Embodied / Enactive Theories**
Strong on: consciousness as sensorimotor engagement, not inner representation. Weak on: abstract selfhood, temporal continuity, identity through transformation. ICT borrows embodiment/control coupling as a component but does not reduce selfhood to sensorimotor activity.

**Output of Stage 3:** Updated THEORY_COMPARISON.md with specific predictions that differ between ICT and each theory, and experimental designs that could distinguish them. See also ADVERSARIAL_PREDICTIONS.md.

## Stage 4 — Case Matrix

**Goal:** Test the theory against a comprehensive set of clinical, experimental, and philosophical cases.

A theory of selfhood and owned continuity must handle the full range of cases where selfhood is disrupted, absent, ambiguous, or surprisingly preserved. The matrix serves two functions: it reveals where the theory makes strong predictions (high confidence, specific claims) and where it produces only qualitative gestures (low confidence, vague claims).

**Case categories:**
- Clinical disruptions: anesthesia, disorders of consciousness, locked-in syndrome, amnesia, dementia
- Phenomenological alterations: ego dissolution, depersonalization, dissociation, lucid dreaming
- Experimental: rubber hand illusion, virtual embodiment, split-brain
- Philosophical thought experiments: teleportation (copy or no copy), brain upload, backup restore, simulation fork, gradual neuron replacement
- Artificial systems: LLM with persistent memory, autonomous AI agent, simulation fork
- Developmental: infant consciousness, animal consciousness

For each case: what does ICT predict? What does that prediction require? What would falsify it? See CASE_MATRIX.md.

## Stage 5 — Formal Scoring

**Goal:** Develop the Owned Continuity Index (OCI) into a tool that produces testable, comparable scores.

The OCI is currently a rough 0–5 dimensional scoring tool defined in OWNED_CONTINUITY_INDEX.md. It is provisional. It is not validated. It must not be treated as a measurement instrument until it has been tested against cases where the predictions can be independently verified.

**Development path for the OCI:**
1. Define all dimensions operationally — each dimension must have a measurable proxy or behavioral indicator
2. Score a set of known cases (ordinary waking, ego dissolution, locked-in syndrome, LLM) and check whether the scores match existing empirical findings
3. Identify which dimensions can be probed with existing experimental paradigms (body ownership illusions, no-report paradigms, PCI)
4. Specify what a validated OCI would require: what data would need to be collected, what statistical tests would need to pass

**Stage 5 is the bridge between the conceptual framework and real measurement.** It does not need to be completed before other stages — but nothing from Stage 5 should be cited as evidence until it is validated.

## Stage 6 — Adversarial Predictions

**Goal:** Make ICT risk being wrong against other theories.

A theory that can accommodate any result is not a theory. For each of the cases in Stage 4, ICT must specify:
1. What it predicts
2. What at least one competing theory predicts differently
3. What result would favor ICT
4. What result would weaken or falsify ICT

The strongest adversarial predictions are cases where ICT and a competitor predict opposite directions — not just different magnitudes. See ADVERSARIAL_PREDICTIONS.md.

**Current highest-value adversarial target:** Split-brain. ICT and GNWT make different predictions about what kind of unity split-brain patients should show, and whether that unity is genuine or constructed. If Pinto et al.'s results hold, ICT's non-branching prediction needs revision. GNWT's access-based prediction is not obviously threatened by the same results. This asymmetry is testable.

## Stage 7 — Empirical Contact

**Goal:** Connect the theory to real experimental and clinical literature with honest accounting.

Each claim that purports to be `empirical_candidate` must cite at least one existing empirical result that bears on it — either supporting it, threatening it, or being neutral but adjacent. This is not citation for credibility. It is mapping: where does the theory touch the world?

**Priority empirical contacts:**
1. Split-brain (cl_004 pressure — highest urgency; see EMPIRICAL_CONTACTS.md)
2. Body ownership illusions (cl_002, cl_003 — most tractable experimental paradigm)
3. No-report paradigms (cl_009 — distinguishes appearance from reportability)
4. Disorders of consciousness (cl_003, cl_008 — tests selfhood without report)
5. Depersonalization/dissociation (cl_002, cl_011 — tests ownership fragmentation)

See EMPIRICAL_PRESSURE_POINTS.md for full list with risk ratings.

## Stage 8 — External Critique

**Goal:** Prepare the theory for review by someone who has no investment in it being true.

This is the hardest stage and the most important. A theory that has only been developed by the person who holds it is not yet a theory — it is an extended self-argument. External critique requires:

1. A written statement of what the theory claims that competing theories do not
2. A list of cases where the theory makes predictions that could fail
3. A list of the theory's weakest points and how it would respond to challenge
4. Preparation for the most likely objections: (a) "this is just restating Parfit in new vocabulary," (b) "owned continuity is just self-model theory with different words," (c) "you haven't operationalized ownership," (d) "the split-brain data does not support your branching claim"

See LEADING_THEORY_REQUIREMENTS.md for what the theory must prove, and FAILURE_MODES.md for what would kill it.
