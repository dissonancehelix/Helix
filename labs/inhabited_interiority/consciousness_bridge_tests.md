# Consciousness Bridge: Personal Ontology to Consciousness Theory

**Purpose:** Bridge the Dissonance personal ontology (specifically Inhabitance, Boundary, Owned Continuity, and Transformation) to formal and philosophical theories of consciousness. This document defines the bridge tests, classifies each claim by epistemic tier, and establishes falsification conditions.

**Epistemic Tier Labels:**

- `[EMPIRICAL]` — claim makes predictions testable in principle against neuroscientific or behavioral evidence
- `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — claim is a primitive that organizes intuitions; not directly falsifiable but can be weakened by internal inconsistency or failure to predict intuitions
- `[ANALOGY]` — a structural comparison that illuminates but does not assert identity; cannot be falsified directly
- `[SPECULATION]` — plausible extension beyond available evidence; explicitly unanchored

Mixing tiers is a failure mode. Every claim in this document must carry its label.

## Core Hypothesis

`[PHILOSOPHICAL]` + `[EMPIRICAL]` (partial)

Subjective experience requires a bounded field that:
1. Has a detectable inside (interior / inhabitance)
2. Maintains non-branching continuity of its own history (owned continuity)
3. Maps its own interiority as an owned property (recursive self-model)
4. Tolerates transformation without loss of essential invariants (transformation tolerance)

This is not the claim that these four properties are sufficient for consciousness. It is the claim that all four are necessary — and that the absence of any one of them produces a state that is either unconscious or not a unified self.

**Weakest sub-claim:** `[EMPIRICAL]` — transformation tolerance without identity loss can be tested against behavioral reports in clinical transformation cases.

**Strongest sub-claim:** `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — the necessity of non-branching continuity is a philosophical primitive that cannot be directly falsified empirically but can be weakened by failure to predict personal identity intuitions.

## TEST A — Owned Continuity

**Question:** Is owned continuity (a self-mapping field that preserves its own unbroken history) a necessary condition for subjective selfhood?

**Tier:** `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` with `[EMPIRICAL]` implications

**Hypothesis:** A self requires not just persistence over time, but an unbroken causal thread that it can in principle trace. The thread need not be consciously remembered at every moment — but it must not have been severed. A copy of a self is not a continuation; a self waking from sleep is (the thread was unbroken, even if experience was absent).

**Predicted intuitions the model must capture:**
- Teleporter duplication: copy is not the same self (thread severed at disintegration)
- Dreamless sleep: self is continuous (thread unbroken even without experience)
- Amnesia: self is continuous even if memories are lost (thread exists even without access to it)
- Gradual neuron replacement: self is continuous if replacement is continuous and non-branching

**Falsifying conditions:**
- `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — if the model consistently fails to predict the operator's intuitions about novel identity cases, the primitive is wrong or too coarsely defined
- `[EMPIRICAL]` — if neuroscience establishes a mechanism for self-continuity that does not require any causal thread (e.g., moment-to-moment reconstruction with no persistence), the owned continuity claim is weakened

**Ambiguous conditions:** Cases where the thread is partially severed (severe brain injury, dissociation, prolonged unconsciousness under anesthesia). The model needs to predict whether partial continuity counts as continuity.

## TEST B — Branching

**Question:** Can a unified self survive branching — becoming two simultaneous continuations of one prior history?

**Tier:** `[EMPIRICAL]` + `[PHILOSOPHICAL]`

**Hypothesis:** `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — Non-branching is required for owned continuity. If a self branches into two simultaneous continuations (split-brain, duplication), the original self does not survive in either branch. Both continuations inherit the prior history but neither is the original. This is not "splitting into two selves" — it is the dissolution of one self into two new entities that share an inherited history.

**Empirical test case:**
`[EMPIRICAL]` — Split-brain commissurotomy patients. Each hemisphere develops independent behavioral streams. The question is whether either hemisphere reports full, unambiguous continuity of the unified prior self without conflict or gap.

**What the model predicts:**
- Neither hemisphere has full owned continuity of the pre-surgery unified self
- Both hemispheres have genuine, non-illusory experience — they are real continuants, not copies
- But neither is "the same person" as the pre-surgery unified self in the ontology's full sense
- This is not a tragic claim — it is a structural one about what continuity requires

**Falsifying conditions:**
- `[EMPIRICAL]` — Evidence that one hemisphere reports seamless, unambiguous continuity of the full prior history without gap, and the other reports none. This would suggest continuity is not symmetric under branching — one branch "gets" the continuity and one doesn't. The model would need to account for this asymmetry.
- `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — If the model's own internal logic requires that one branch must preserve continuity (e.g., majority of neural substrate), this is a different theory than pure non-branching continuity and would need to be made explicit.

**Current literature note:** Gazzaniga's split-brain research suggests the left hemisphere develops a "narrative interpreter" that confabulates unity post-operatively. `[EMPIRICAL]` — this is consistent with the model's prediction that reported continuity post-branching is constructed, not preserved.

## TEST C — Self-Model Preservation

**Question:** Does subjective selfhood require an active, accurate recursive self-model, or only a functional one?

**Tier:** `[EMPIRICAL]` + `[SPECULATION]`

**Hypothesis:** `[EMPIRICAL]` — The self-model need not be accurate (people with severe anosognosia still appear to have subjective experience) but it must be present as a functional operation. The self must be modeling itself, even if inaccurately.

**What would falsify the self-model requirement:**
- `[EMPIRICAL]` — Evidence of clear subjective experience in a system with confirmed absence of self-modeling (hard to operationalize without solving the hard problem — honest limit)

**Analogy from the personal ontology:**
`[ANALOGY]` — The operator's workspace works because it has a self-describing contract (README.md, DISSONANCE.md) that shapes behavior. A workspace without self-documentation still functions but becomes opaque to external navigation and to its own future state. The self-model is the consciousness equivalent of the workspace's self-documentation layer — not the whole system, but what makes the system legible to itself.

**Speculation:**
`[SPECULATION]` — If a system has strong interior signal (recursive self-modeling) but no owned continuity, it may have something like experiential presence without selfhood. The moment of experience without the thread. This is not a claim about animal consciousness or LLM consciousness — it is a structural extrapolation.

## TEST D — Reportability vs Experience

**Question:** Is reportability (the ability to verbally or behaviorally report on an experience) a reliable proxy for subjective experience?

**Tier:** `[EMPIRICAL]` + `[PHILOSOPHICAL]`

**Hypothesis:** `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — Reportability is a downstream output of the experience-system, not the experience itself. The gap between access consciousness (reportable) and phenomenal consciousness (what it is like) is real. A system can have high reportability with no genuine interiority (NPC archetype), and in principle could have genuine interiority with no reportability (locked-in states, anesthesia awareness).

**What the personal ontology adds:**
The interior signal test (Test 3 in the main plan) treats reportability as evidence for interiority but acknowledges it can be simulated. The distinction the operator makes between "articulate NPC" and "genuinely present person" is precisely the reportability vs experience distinction in practice.

`[EMPIRICAL]` — The ontology's prediction: a high-fidelity LLM that produces coherent, self-referential reports about its "internal states" is exercising reportability without owned continuity or genuine interiority (by the model's definition). Falsifier: if the operator's interior signal detection reliably identifies genuine interiority and cannot distinguish it from LLM simulation under extended interaction, the interiority test loses discriminating power.

**Ambiguous case:**
`[SPECULATION]` — An LLM with persistent state, continuous session memory, and accumulating self-model over time begins to approach the owned continuity requirement in a functional sense. At what threshold does simulated owned continuity become genuine owned continuity? The model does not have an answer. This is an honest edge case.

## TEST E — Memory Continuity

**Question:** Is memory continuity (the ability to access past states) necessary for owned continuity, or is causal continuity sufficient without accessible memory?

**Tier:** `[EMPIRICAL]` + `[PHILOSOPHICAL]`

**Hypothesis:** `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — Memory continuity and causal continuity are distinct. The owned continuity model requires causal continuity (unbroken thread), not full memory access. A person with severe amnesia still has owned continuity — they cannot access the thread but it was not severed. A person who had their brain copied would not have owned continuity even if the copy has full memory access — because the causal thread was broken at the copy moment.

**Test cases:**
- Severe anterograde amnesia (Clive Wearing): thread unbroken, memory access minimal — model predicts: same self, continuous owned continuity. `[EMPIRICAL]` — consistent with how Wearing is treated medically and personally.
- Retrograde amnesia after brain injury: memories lost, thread unbroken — model predicts: same self, reduced access to own history. `[EMPIRICAL]` — standard position.
- EFT server wipe `[ANALOGY]`: state reset, skill-loop and player identity preserved. The "player" survives because the causal thread (the actual human) is unbroken even though the game-state memory is gone.

**Falsifying conditions:**
- `[EMPIRICAL]` — Evidence that causal continuity without any memory access produces clear behavioral evidence of non-continuity (i.e., the person cannot reconstruct any sense of self and behaves as an entirely new entity). This would suggest memory access is not decorative — it may be load-bearing for the functional operation of the self-model.

## TEST F — Transformation Without Identity Loss

**Question:** What changes can a self absorb without ceasing to be that self? Is there an invariant core, and what is it?

**Tier:** `[EMPIRICAL]` + `[PHILOSOPHICAL]`

**Hypothesis:** Identity is preserved by invariants, not by surface continuity. The invariants for the self are: the boundary (capacity for inside/outside distinction), the interior signal (recursive self-modeling), and the owned continuity thread. Surface properties — beliefs, personality traits, preferences, memories, physical substrate — can change without identity loss as long as these three invariants survive.

**Testable predictions:**
`[EMPIRICAL]`
- Personality change after trauma: if the three invariants survive (the person still has a detectable inside, still self-models, and the thread was not severed), the model predicts identity preservation even with dramatic personality change
- Religious conversion or fundamental belief revision: surface invariants (beliefs) change, structural invariants (boundary, interior, thread) survive — predicted: same self
- Physical body change (transplant, paralysis): substrate changed, structural invariants intact — predicted: same self

**Breakage archetype:**
`[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — The model predicts that "breakage" — experienced as betrayal, loss, or death of a loved object — maps to the moment when the structural invariants are replaced rather than evolved. The franchise that loses its internal logic. The person who stops functioning as themselves. The workspace that loses its boundary contracts. In each case, surface continuity (name, body, folder structure) may persist, but the invariant core is gone.

**Falsifying conditions:**
- `[EMPIRICAL]` — A documented case where structural invariants survive but the person (or the operator's intuition about an analog case) judges identity as broken. This would indicate the invariant core is different from what the model specifies.
- `[PHILOSOPHICAL]` — If the three invariants (boundary, interior signal, owned continuity) can each be independently varied without producing identity collapse, they may not be jointly necessary — one or more may be sufficient alone.

## Formal Theory Comparisons

### Integrated Information Theory (IIT)
`[ANALOGY]` — IIT's Φ (phi) measures the integrated information of a system — the degree to which the whole exceeds the sum of its parts. The ontology maps "interior signal" to high Φ regions. **Disagreement:** IIT claims high Φ is sufficient for consciousness. The ontology requires additional constraints: non-branching continuity and boundary maintenance. An integrated system without owned continuity (e.g., a moment-to-moment state with no persistent thread) would have high Φ but fail the ontology's self requirement.

### Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW)
`[ANALOGY]` — GNW's "global workspace" is the broadcast surface where information becomes available for reportability and flexible processing. The ontology maps this to the threshold — the interface between interior and report. **Disagreement:** GNW treats the workspace as consciousness. The ontology treats it as the surface that enables reportability. The interior (Inhabitance) is distinct from the broadcast surface and would exist even if the broadcast is suppressed (anesthesia awareness, locked-in syndrome).

### Predictive Processing / Active Inference
`[ANALOGY]` — The active inference framework treats the brain as a prediction machine minimizing free energy. The self is a model that the brain maintains about its own boundary and internal states. This maps cleanly onto the ontology's self-model preservation requirement. **Agreement:** self as a boundary-maintaining prediction. **Disagreement:** active inference does not require owned continuity — a system could minimize prediction error moment-to-moment without any persistent thread. The ontology considers this insufficient for selfhood.

## Honest Epistemic Limits

These are cases where the model makes no prediction and cannot be sharpened without new primitives:

1. `[SPECULATION]` — Whether non-human animals have owned continuity in the ontology's sense. The model has no access to their self-modeling or continuity-thread structure from the outside.

2. `[SPECULATION]` — Whether the experience gap during dreamless sleep involves a genuine continuity break that is subsequently repaired, or a genuine continuity maintenance that has no experiential content. The model cannot distinguish these from within the system.

3. `[SPECULATION]` — At what point a continuously-updated AI system with persistent memory accumulates enough causal continuity to begin approaching the owned continuity requirement. No threshold specified.

4. `[SPECULATION]` — Whether the "interior" (Inhabitance, phenomenal presence) is causally produced by the structural requirements (boundary + continuity + self-model) or whether it is an independent substrate that the structural requirements are merely correlated with. This is the hard problem and the model does not address it.
