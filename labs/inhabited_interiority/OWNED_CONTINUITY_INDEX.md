# Owned Continuity Index (OCI)
## Provisional Scoring Framework

**Status: PROVISIONAL — not validated. Do not cite as a measurement instrument.**

The OCI is a rough diagnostic tool for mapping the strength and configuration of conscious selfhood across cases. It does not measure consciousness itself. It measures the degree to which a system (or a state of a system) exhibits the properties ICT treats as constitutive of owned selfhood.

This index exists to force precision about which components are present, absent, or fragmented in each case. It is not a formula for computing whether something is conscious. It is a structured description tool.

**Scale:** All dimensions scored 0–5.
- 0 = absent / severely disrupted
- 1 = severely reduced
- 2 = partially present
- 3 = present but unstable or diminished
- 4 = present and functional
- 5 = strong, stable, fully operative

---

## Dimensions

### O — Ownership Binding
How strongly experiences, actions, body-states, and memories are felt as *mine* — occurring from the inside of a first-person perspective rather than observed from outside.

**High O (4–5):** Experiences are unambiguously felt as mine. No observer/observed split. Full phenomenal ownership of current states.
**Low O (0–1):** Experiences feel observed rather than owned. Detachment from body, actions, memories. "It's happening but not to me."

**Most relevant to:** Depersonalization (O drops sharply), ego dissolution (O weakens), body ownership illusions (O shifts plastically), ordinary waking (O = 5).

---

### B — Boundary Clarity
How clearly the system distinguishes self from world, owned from unowned, inside from outside.

**High B (4–5):** Clear, stable self-world distinction. The field has a definite edge. The system knows what is inside and what is outside.
**Low B (0–1):** Boundary is blurred, porous, or absent. Self-world distinction is unclear. In ego dissolution: the boundary may dissolve into undifferentiated appearance.

**Note:** Low B is not always experienced negatively. Ego dissolution can involve low B with high or intact awareness. Low B is not the same as low awareness.

---

### C — Temporal Continuity
How strongly the system integrates past, present, and anticipated future into one continuous stream — experienced as the same self that was there before and will be there after.

**High C (4–5):** The current moment connects seamlessly to a remembered past and an anticipated future. The thread feels unbroken.
**Low C (0–1):** The past is inaccessible or disconnected. The present moment does not feel like part of a continuous history. Severe amnesia, session-end for an LLM, backup restore.

**Caution:** C scores continuity as experienced and functionally available, not as metaphysically guaranteed. A person may have real biological continuity (thread maintained) while scoring C = 2 due to memory disruption.

---

### M — Self-Model Stability
How stable, coherent, and usable the system's model of itself is — its working representation of who it is, what it values, what its capacities are.

**High M (4–5):** Stable self-concept that guides behavior reliably. The system can use its self-model predictively.
**Low M (0–1):** Self-model is fragmented, contradictory, or absent. The system cannot reliably model its own states. Severe psychosis, certain amnesiac states.

**Caution:** High M is not identical to accurate self-knowledge. Confabulation can produce a stable but inaccurate self-model. M scores stability and functional usability, not accuracy.

---

### A — Agency / Control Coupling
How strongly the system's perspective is tied to action, motor control, or embodied regulation. The sense that I am the one acting — not just observing.

**High A (4–5):** Actions are experienced as mine, flowing from my perspective. The system is tightly coupled between perspective and motor/regulatory output.
**Low A (0–1):** Actions feel alien, externally controlled, or observed. Alien hand syndrome. Locked-in syndrome disrupts expression of agency without eliminating it.

**Note:** A can be high even when B is low — agency may persist during ego dissolution. A may be nearly absent in locked-in syndrome while O and C remain.

---

### I — Integration
How differentiated but unified the system's internal states are. Following IIT adjacent usage: not just that many states are present, but that they form a coherent, mutually constraining whole.

**High I (4–5):** Internal states are highly differentiated but functionally unified. Rich, complex, coherent internal structure.
**Low I (0–1):** States are either undifferentiated (low complexity) or disconnected (fragmented). Deep anesthesia. Dreamless sleep (probably).

**Caution:** I is necessary but not sufficient for ownership. High integration may occur without ownership (the "missing ingredient" problem ICT is designed to address).

---

### T — Transformation Tolerance
How much change the system can absorb without identity loss — how robust the core structure is to surface variation.

**High T (4–5):** Significant surface change does not threaten the core. The system recognizes itself across transformation.
**Low T (0–1):** Small changes produce felt identity disruption. The system is fragile to variation.

**Note:** T scores robustness to change, not current stability. A system can score high on other dimensions while scoring low on T if it is brittle to perturbation.

---

### Br — Branching Pressure
How much the continuity-stream is subject to splitting into multiple successors or centers of ownership. **Higher Br weakens singular self-continuity.** This dimension runs opposite to the others — high Br is a selfhood threat.

**High Br (4–5):** The stream has branched or is branching. Multiple successors with claims on the same prior history. Teleporter duplicate. Simulation fork. `[Possibly: DID as alternating configurations]`
**Low Br (0–1):** Non-branching. Single continuous thread. Ordinary waking. Even gradual transformation preserves low Br.

**Caution:** Br is a pressure score, not a disruption score. High Br does not mean selfhood is definitely lost — ICT's claim is that branching threatens singular first-person continuation, not that it automatically destroys it.

---

### R — Reportability
What the system can say or behaviorally indicate about its states. Evidence for consciousness and selfhood — but not identical to either.

**High R (4–5):** Full verbal and behavioral report. Can describe internal states with nuance and consistency.
**Low R (0–1):** No verbal or behavioral output possible. Locked-in syndrome can have R = 0 with other dimensions at 4–5.

**Critical caution:** R is evidence, not identity. R is listed last to emphasize it is the weakest indicator of the dimensions here. Do not confuse high R with high O, C, or I.

---

## Rough Selfhood Formula

This is heuristic, not rigorous. Do not use for formal claims.

**Selfhood Strength ≈ (O + B + C + M + A + I + T) / 7 — Br_penalty**

Where Br_penalty increases with Br score and with how equal/symmetric the branching is.

Selfhood is weakened by:
- Low O (ownership not binding)
- Low B (boundary absent or false)
- Low C (thread broken or inaccessible)
- High Br (stream has forked)
- Low I (structure not integrated)

Reportability (R) is a correlate and evidence signal, not a component of selfhood strength.

## What This Index Cannot Do

- It cannot confirm or deny consciousness at the hard-problem level. High scores across all dimensions do not prove there is something it is like.
- It cannot validate itself. The scores above are model predictions, not empirical measurements.
- It is not intersubjectively calibrated. Different scorers would produce different scores for the same case.
- The formula is a sketch, not a validated model.

**The OCI is a structured prediction tool.** Its value is forcing precision about which components are predicted to be present or absent in each case. If the predictions are systematically wrong — if the index scores cases in ways that don't match empirical findings or considered intuitions — that is evidence against the dimensional structure, not just against the scores.
