# Falsifiers

What would weaken or break the Inhabited Continuity Theory. Divided into hard falsifiers (would require substantial revision or abandonment of a core claim) and soft weakening cases (would require sharpening, demotion of a claim, or explicit scope limitation).

Every theory needs this document. If no falsifiers can be named, the theory is not doing real intellectual work.

---

## Hard Falsifiers

These cases, if confirmed, would require revising or abandoning a core primitive or the central thesis.

---

**Selfhood without temporal continuity**

If a system clearly demonstrates owned selfhood with no temporal continuity — no causal thread, no memory overlap, no persistent process — the continuity requirement is broken. Each moment would be a complete new self that nonetheless experiences ownership. This is the hardest direct falsifier.

*What it would look like:* A reliable experimental or clinical case where causal and memorial continuity are fully severed, yet the subject reports full, stable, non-confabulated owned selfhood. Not merely continuity of behavior but owned experience of being the same self.

*Current status:* No confirmed case. Severe amnesia cases retain causal continuity. Hypothetical cases (backup-restore, teleportation) remain thought experiments.

---

**Ownership without any boundary function**

If owned experience can be demonstrably shown in a system with no detectable self-world distinction, no inside/outside structure, and no boundary function, the boundary requirement fails.

*What it would look like:* Confirmed cases of owned experience in ego-dissolution or psychotic states that are genuinely boundaryless — not just boundary-weakened or boundary-confused. Or a model of consciousness that produces owned experience without any boundary-like structure.

*Current status:* Reports of "boundaryless experience" in meditation or psychedelics are ambiguous — they may describe altered boundary experience, not genuine absence. The claim cannot be resolved from verbal report alone.

---

**Branching preserving singular first-person identity**

If a branching case produces two simultaneous successors that are both continuously and unambiguously the same first-person self (not two successors who each believe they are, but genuinely identical ownership streams), the non-branching requirement fails.

*What it would look like:* A split-brain or duplication case where both streams report not just "I was the original" but exhibit no discontinuity, no identity-conflict, and identical non-confabulated first-person perspectives — and this is stable under pressure and consistent with each other. This is nearly impossible to operationalize but sets the bar for falsification.

*Current status:* Split-brain literature shows conflict and confabulation between hemispheres, consistent with the theory's prediction. True duplication cases do not exist empirically.

---

**Reportability fully explaining ownership**

If a complete, rigorous account of owned experience can be derived from reportability alone — without any additional primitives (boundary, continuity, self-model, field) — then owned continuity is redundant. The theory collapses to a sophisticated theory of reportability.

*What it would look like:* A complete mechanistic account of what makes experiences "mine" that reduces fully to access consciousness processes, with no remainder. All ownership phenomena (bodily ownership, memorial ownership, perspective) explained entirely by report-enabling mechanisms.

*Current status:* No such account exists. Owned experience reports in anesthesia awareness and locked-in syndrome resist pure reportability reduction.

---

**Integration alone explaining selfhood**

If integration of sufficient complexity produces owned selfhood in every case — with no exceptions for highly-integrated non-conscious systems — then the additional requirements (owned continuity, boundary, non-branching) add nothing.

*What it would look like:* Every identified high-integration system is found to have genuine owned experience (including trained neural networks, organoids, weather patterns at sufficient complexity). No high-integration system lacks experience. Or: a rigorous proof that high Φ guarantees owned experience.

*Current status:* IIT claims this but faces counterexamples (feedforward networks, cerebellum). The jury is out.

---

**Memory similarity fully explaining continuity**

If identity-preservation intuitions track memory overlap alone — with no remainder for causal continuity, non-branching, or other structural requirements — then the richer continuity account reduces to psychological connectedness (Parfit's position).

*What it would look like:* In every thought experiment, identity-preservation intuitions exactly track memory overlap, regardless of causal history, branching, or substrate. The teleporter case: if memories survive perfectly, identity is preserved — full stop.

*Current status:* Many people have this intuition. Parfit defended it systematically. The theory rejects it on the basis that memory alone allows branching (two copies with same memories), which cannot preserve singular self-continuity. The conflict is real and ongoing.

---

**Self-modeling being unnecessary for selfhood**

If selfhood is confirmed in systems with no self-model whatsoever — no representation of themselves as the subject or center — the self-model requirement fails.

*What it would look like:* Clear owned experience in a system with confirmed absence of any self-representational process. Infants may approach this (minimal self-model). Whether they have owned experience is itself contested.

*Current status:* No confirmed case. Severe anosognosia shows inaccurate self-modeling but not absent self-modeling.

---

**Appearance-from-within fully explained without field/boundary/ownership concepts**

If a complete explanation of why a system has appearance-from-within (not merely functional self-representation but the felt quality) is provided without using field, boundary, or ownership concepts, the theory's primitive set is unnecessary.

*What it would look like:* A solution to the hard problem of consciousness that does not reference any concept that maps onto field, boundary, or ownership. Given that the hard problem is unsolved, this is a long-horizon falsifier.

*Current status:* The hard problem remains unsolved. No current theory achieves this.

---

## Soft Weakening Cases

These cases would not falsify the theory but would require demotion, scope limitation, or explicit sharpening.

---

**Theory becomes unfalsifiable**

If, under pressure, every potential falsifier is re-described as compatible with the theory (e.g., "that's just a disrupted ownership stream, not an absence of ownership"), the theory becomes a framework that can absorb any result. This is self-sealing and must be resisted.

*Response protocol:* Each primitive must be defined sharply enough that there is a clear case that would confirm its absence. If boundary, ownership, and continuity are defined so broadly that they are always present in any experiencing system, they are not explanatory primitives — they are labels.

---

**Primitives become too vague**

If "field," "boundary," "interior," and "ownership" cannot be operationalized even in principle — even at the level of "what evidence would convince me this is absent" — they are losing their load-bearing function.

*Response protocol:* PRIMITIVES.md must be updated with specific failure conditions. Any primitive that has no failure condition must be either sharpened or demoted to [ANALOGY].

---

**Analogies are mistaken for evidence**

The personal ontology analogies (workspace boundaries, music groove as continuity, game topology as terrain) illuminate the theory's concepts but do not support them empirically. If these analogies start being cited as evidence rather than illumination, the theory degrades.

*Response protocol:* Every analogy in THEORY.md, CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml, and THOUGHT_EXPERIMENTS.md must be tagged [ANALOGY]. No analogy should appear in an empirical claim position without conversion to a genuine empirical prediction.

---

**Personal ontology bridge overreaches**

The bridge from personal taste preferences to consciousness theory may be illuminating but the bridge cannot be the theory. If the theory's claims only make sense from within the personal ontology — if they do not generalize beyond one person's cognitive style — then this is a personal research harness, not a consciousness theory.

*Response protocol:* Every claim should be testable in principle with subjects other than the operator. If a claim cannot be stated without reference to Dissonance's specific preferences or cognitive style, it must be demoted to personal-ontology notes, not consciousness theory.

---

**Metaphysics replaces empirical contact**

If the theory develops primarily through philosophical elaboration of its primitives — generating increasingly sophisticated definitions and thought-experiment analyses — without maintaining any tether to empirical contact points, it becomes metaphysics dressed as consciousness research.

*Response protocol:* EMPIRICAL_CONTACTS.md must grow at least as fast as PRIMITIVES.md and CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml. Any expansion of the philosophical claims must be paired with identification of an empirical hook, even if that hook is currently out of reach.
