## Primary empirical anchors

These papers provide the most direct empirical constraints on the research program.
They are treated as fixed reference points, not merely supporting citations.

**Casali et al. 2013** — "A theoretically based index of consciousness independent of
sensory processing and behavior." *Science Translational Medicine.*  
→ PCI/TMS-EEG as behavioral-report-independent proxy for consciousness.  
→ Relevance: G-proxy; distinguishes ketamine (high PCI) from propofol (near-zero PCI).

**Owen et al. 2006** — "Detecting awareness in the vegetative state." *Science.*  
→ Imagining tennis activates motor cortex in a behaviorally unresponsive patient.  
→ Relevance: G-independence from behavioral output; verification gap for locked-in and
vegetative states.

**Tsuchiya et al. 2015** — "No-report paradigms: Extracting the true neural correlates of
consciousness." *Trends in Cognitive Sciences.*  
→ Neural correlates of consciousness can be contaminated by report-related processes.  
→ Relevance: Empirical anchor for G-independence from report.

**COGITATE Consortium 2023** — "An adversarial collaboration protocol for empirically
adjudicating between theories of consciousness." *eLife.*  
→ Preregistered adversarial predictions between IIT and GNWT. Mixed results.  
→ Relevance: Methodological model for this program. The adversarial format is the target.

**Tononi et al. 2016** — "Integrated information theory: From consciousness to its
physical substrate." *Nature Reviews Neuroscience.*  
→ IIT (Phi); key rival theory.  
→ Relevance: Rival theory comparison; market-system false-positive problem for IIT.

**Dehaene & Changeux 2011** — "Experimental and theoretical approaches to conscious
processing." *Neuron.*  
→ Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT).  
→ Relevance: Rival theory comparison; GNWT overclassification risk for access vs. phenomenal.

## Clinical cases

**Weiskrantz 1986** — *Blindsight: A Case Study and Implications.*  
→ Establishes blindsight as dissociation of visual processing from visual experience.  
→ Relevance: blindsight.yaml; G-absence despite processing.

**Schilder & Stengel 1931** — First description of pain asymbolia.  
**Berthier et al. 1988** — Neuropsychological analysis of pain asymbolia.  
**Grahek 2007** — *Feeling Pain and Being in Pain.*  
→ Relevance: pain_asymbolia.yaml; G-without-full-valence challenge.

**Sierra & Berrios 1998** — "Depersonalization: A conceptual history." *History of Psychiatry.*  
**Simeon et al. 2000** — Depersonalization disorder with intact reality testing.  
→ Relevance: depersonalization.yaml; G present, O reduced.

**Gazzaniga, Bogen & Sperry 1965** — Split-brain experimental paradigm.  
**Gazzaniga 1995** — "Principles of human brain organization derived from split-brain studies."  
**Bayne 2010** — *The Unity of Consciousness.*  
→ Relevance: split_brain.yaml; U-scoring ambiguity.

**Holmes 1927** — Early description of absence seizures.  
**Penfield & Jasper 1954** — Thalamocortical origin of absence seizures.  
→ Relevance: absence_seizure.yaml; G-collapse mechanism.

## Phenomenology and philosophy of mind

**Husserl, E.** — *Ideas I* (1913); *On the Phenomenology of the Consciousness of Internal
Time* (1928).  
→ Temporal thickness (T) and the specious present. Primary conceptual source for T.

**Metzinger, T. 2003** — *Being No One.*  
→ Self-model theory (rival). Phenomenal self-model as source of selfhood.  
→ Relevance: rival theory comparison; depersonalization.

**Gamma, A. & Metzinger, T. 2021** — "The minimal phenomenal experience questionnaire."
*Frontiers in Psychology.*  
→ MPE questionnaire; minimal awareness empirical anchor.  
→ Relevance: minimal_awareness.yaml.

**Josipovic, Z. & Miskovic, V. 2020** — "Nondual awareness and minimal phenomenal
experience." *Frontiers in Psychology.*  
→ Nondual awareness as candidate for minimal G.  
→ Relevance: minimal_awareness.yaml.

**Chalmers, D. 1995** — "Facing up to the problem of consciousness." *Journal of
Consciousness Studies.*  
→ Hard problem; G as primitive residue is a response to this framing.

**Nagel, T. 1974** — "What is it like to be a bat?" *Philosophical Review.*  
→ Phenomenal character as "what it is like." Closest verbal formulation of G.

**Parfit, D. 1984** — *Reasons and Persons.*  
→ Personal identity, branching, and pattern continuity. Critical for S-formula.  
→ Relevance: non_destructive_copy.yaml; destructive_teleportation.yaml; N-override.

## Action fields, games, and sport (false-positive controls)

These sources are used to understand the structural properties of action fields — not as
evidence that action fields are phenomenal.

**Dreyfus, H. 1991** — *Being-in-the-World.* (Embodied skilled action; field structure)  
**Gibson, J.J. 1979** — *The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception.* (Affordance fields)  
**De Bono, E. 1971** — *Lateral Thinking.* (Problem-space topology; useful for false-positive
 distinction between structured problem spaces and phenomenal fields)
