# Case Matrix
## Inhabited Continuity Theory — Case-by-Case Analysis

**Purpose:** Test ICT predictions against the full range of cases where selfhood is present, disrupted, absent, or ambiguous. Each case reveals which primitives are load-bearing and where the theory makes sharp predictions vs vague gestures.

**Columns:**
- **Setup:** What the case involves
- **Relevant primitives:** Which ICT dimensions are in play
- **ICT prediction:** What the theory says about this case
- **Competing interpretation:** What the strongest alternative account says
- **Supports ICT if:** Result that would favor the theory
- **Weakens ICT if:** Result that would pressure or falsify the theory
- **Claim tier:** Epistemic status of the prediction
- **Open questions:** What remains unresolved

---

## Case 1 — Ordinary Waking Consciousness

**Setup:** Normal adult, awake, alert, engaged with the world. No disruption.

**Relevant primitives:** Field, boundary, ownership, continuity, self-model, integration, control coupling.

**ICT prediction:** All components present and integrated. This is the baseline case — the theory is calibrated to this. OCI: O5 B5 C5 M4 A5 I5 T4 Br0 R5.

**Competing interpretation:** All theories predict this case. It is not discriminating.

**Supports ICT if:** Nothing — this case confirms all theories equally.

**Weakens ICT if:** Nothing — this case is not a test.

**Claim tier:** Baseline, not a test case.

**Open questions:** None specific to this case.

## Case 2 — Dream Consciousness

**Setup:** REM dreaming. The sleeper is conscious, has a perspective, navigates a dream environment. Report only available on waking.

**Relevant primitives:** Field, boundary, ownership, continuity (within-dream vs to waking), self-model.

**ICT prediction:** The field is present (there is something it is like). Ownership is present but weaker and more plastic than waking. Boundary is partially present (a dream self exists) but less stable. Temporal continuity within the dream is present; continuity between dream and waking state is uncertain — the thread continues biologically, but whether the dream self's continuity connects to the waking self is unresolved.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: the dream is a prediction without sensory constraint — the self-prior runs unchecked. GNWT: global workspace is active in a reduced form. IIT: phi is maintained.

**Supports ICT if:** Dream selves show evidence of within-dream ownership (things feel like mine in the dream) while cross-state continuity (dream-to-waking) is not experienced as seamless.

**Weakens ICT if:** Dream selfhood is fully continuous with waking selfhood in a way that does not require the primitives ICT treats as load-bearing.

**Claim tier:** Speculation — the within-dream experience is difficult to study.

**Open questions:** Is the dream self a separate owned continuity-stream or a reconfiguration of the waking stream? Does the thread branch during dreams?

## Case 3 — Lucid Dream

**Setup:** The dreamer knows they are dreaming. Two layers of selfhood: the lucid observer who knows, and the dream self being observed.

**Relevant primitives:** Self-model, boundary, ownership, continuity.

**ICT prediction:** The lucid layer represents a partial reconnection between the dream state and the waking self-model. The meta-awareness is a self-model function (knowing oneself to be dreaming). This doesn't fully restore waking-level ownership but adds a layer of self-modeling that normal dreams lack.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: lucid dreaming involves partial updating of the self-prior with the "I am dreaming" prediction. SMT: two PSMs are simultaneously active.

**Supports ICT if:** Lucid dreamers report stronger ownership (things feel more mine) than non-lucid dreamers, and boundary clarity is higher.

**Weakens ICT if:** The meta-awareness of dreaming is entirely separable from ownership changes — knowing you're dreaming doesn't affect how owned the experience feels.

**Claim tier:** Speculation.

**Open questions:** Does the reconnection of self-model in lucid dreaming produce genuine ownership changes or only epistemic changes?

## Case 4 — General Anesthesia

**Setup:** Full surgical anesthesia — no detectable consciousness, no response, profound disruption of integration.

**Relevant primitives:** Field, boundary, ownership, continuity, integration.

**ICT prediction:** All dimensions approach zero during deep anesthesia. The thread is suspended (biological continuity continues) but whether owned continuity is maintained or broken by the gap is unresolvable from within the system. Recovery restores the system but the gap is experientially inaccessible.

**Competing interpretation:** IIT: phi near zero = no consciousness. GNWT: global workspace is suppressed. PP: prediction system suppressed.

**Supports ICT if:** Anesthesia awareness cases — patients who were conscious during supposedly unconscious anesthesia — show intact ownership and field while external behavior suggests anesthesia is working. This would support separating appearance from behavioral markers.

**Weakens ICT if:** Anesthesia awareness cases are fully explained by incomplete anesthesia, with no residual implications for the field/behavior dissociation.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — anesthesia awareness is documentable.

**Open questions:** What happens to continuity during the gap? Is recovery a thread resumption or a thread reconnection?

## Case 5 — Sleep Without Dreaming

**Setup:** Dreamless deep sleep. No apparent consciousness. Biological continuity intact.

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, field, integration.

**ICT prediction:** Biological continuity continues; the thread is maintained causally. Whether owned continuity continues during the gap is genuinely unresolvable. The waking sense of continuity (I am the same person who went to sleep) is likely inference from causal continuity, not direct phenomenal experience of continuity across the gap. C score on waking: 4–5, but this reflects post-hoc reconstruction, not confirmed preservation.

**Competing interpretation:** IIT: phi drops to near zero = no consciousness. GNWT: no global broadcast = no consciousness. Both agree with ICT that nothing is experienced; they differ on why.

**Supports ICT if:** Nothing — this case does not distinguish ICT from competitors.

**Weakens ICT if:** Evidence that something is experienced even in dreamless sleep would require revising the claim that the gap is a genuine discontinuity in experience.

**Claim tier:** Speculation — the gap is not accessible from within the system.

**Open questions:** Is the gap a genuine experiential discontinuity or a suspension of the thread that doesn't break it?

## Case 6 — Ego Dissolution

**Setup:** High-dose psychedelic or advanced meditation produces dissolution of self-boundaries, weakening or loss of ownership-binding, sometimes "pure awareness" without a subject.

**Relevant primitives:** Field, boundary, ownership.

**ICT prediction:** The field (consciousness, appearance) persists or intensifies. Boundary and ownership weaken significantly. This is a dissociation between field and self — appearance without owned structure. The OCI field score remains high; O and B scores drop sharply. This is ICT's central discriminating prediction: consciousness can remain when selfhood is diminished.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: self-prior precision is loosened — less confident prediction of being a bounded agent. SMT: PSM becomes partially transparent or dissolves. Both are compatible with ICT on this case.

**Supports ICT if:** Subjects reliably report continued awareness ("something is happening") alongside loss of ownership ("but not to me / there is no me") — the dissociation in self-report would directly support the field/ownership distinction.

**Weakens ICT if:** Ego dissolution always involves reduction in awareness itself, not just ownership — field and self always co-vary, no dissociation.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — this dissociation is testable in principle with careful phenomenological instruments.

**Open questions:** Does "pure awareness without a subject" mean the field is present without ownership, or does it mean something else? The report is first-person; the theory needs a way to interpret it.

## Case 7 — Depersonalization

**Setup:** Chronic or episodic depersonalization disorder — consciousness is intact, but experiences feel detached, observed from outside, not fully mine.

**Relevant primitives:** Ownership, boundary, self-model.

**ICT prediction:** Field is intact. O (ownership) is specifically disrupted — the binding between appearance and "mine" is weakened. B (boundary) is partially reduced — the self-world distinction persists but is less firm. This is the clearest clinical case for the field/ownership dissociation.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: interoceptive precision weighting is reduced — the body signal is less convincing as a self-signal. SMT: PSM becomes less vivid or transparent in a different direction.

**Supports ICT if:** Depersonalization patients show selective disruption of ownership-relevant dimensions (O, B) while other OCI dimensions remain stable. If this can be mapped to measurable ownership proxies (body ownership, agency ownership), it supports ICT's multi-component ownership model.

**Weakens ICT if:** Depersonalization is fully explained by PP's precision-weighting account with no residual variance that requires a separate ownership-binding concept.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — depersonalization is well-documented and measurable.

**Open questions:** Which ownership components are selectively disrupted? Are they dissociable from each other?

## Case 8 — Dissociation / DID

**Setup:** Dissociation ranges from mild derealization to dissociative identity disorder, where multiple distinct identity states (alters) alternate in control of the body.

**Relevant primitives:** Ownership, continuity, boundary, self-model, branching.

**ICT prediction:** In DID specifically: multiple behavioral identity modes may share a body and causal history. Each alter may constitute a partial ownership-stream. Whether there is one continuous thread with multiple self-model configurations, or genuinely separate ownership-streams alternating in expression, is an open question. ICT predicts the latter would involve Br elevation — each alter's ownership-stream is partially branched from the others.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: different self-priors alternate. SMT: multiple PSMs alternate. Both are compatible with either the single-thread or multiple-thread reading.

**Supports ICT if:** Evidence that different alters do not experience each other's states as their own — genuine ownership separation, not just different behavioral modes.

**Weakens ICT if:** Neuroimaging shows a single underlying experiential stream that configures differently across alters, without genuine ownership-stream separation.

**Claim tier:** Speculation — the underlying mechanism is deeply contested.

**Open questions:** Is DID multiple ownership-streams or one stream with multiple self-model configurations? This is exactly the question ICT needs to answer.

## Case 9 — Split-Brain

**Setup:** Surgical commissurotomy produces two hemispheres with divided information access. Classical accounts: dramatic disunity. Revisionist accounts (Pinto et al.): unified conscious experience despite divided perception.

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, branching, ownership, boundary.

**ICT prediction:** If commissurotomy produces genuine division at the owned-consciousness level, each hemisphere has a separate ownership-stream and the branching requirement is triggered. But if owned consciousness remains unified despite divided access (as Pinto et al. suggest), then the branching has occurred at the perception/access level, not the ownership level, and ICT's non-branching claim applies at the ownership level only. ICT must specify what evidence would confirm division at the owned-consciousness level.

**Competing interpretation:** IIT: commissurotomy should reduce phi, predicting reduced or split consciousness. GNWT: two global workspaces = two consciousness systems. Both predict more disunity than Pinto et al. found.

**Supports ICT if:** Pinto et al.'s results hold — unified owned consciousness despite divided access. This would favor ICT's level-distinction over IIT's integration account.

**Weakens ICT if:** Careful testing reveals genuine disunity at the owned-consciousness level, not explained by confabulation.

**Claim tier:** Empirical pressure case — must be engaged before cl_004 can be treated as supported.

**Open questions:** How to distinguish confabulated unity from genuine owned-consciousness unity empirically?

## Case 10 — Locked-In Syndrome

**Setup:** Complete motor paralysis with intact cognition. The patient is fully conscious, has full selfhood, cannot produce behavioral output.

**Relevant primitives:** Ownership, continuity, self-model, agency, reportability.

**ICT prediction:** R (reportability) is near zero. A (agency/control coupling) is severely reduced. But O, B, C, M, I may all remain at 4–5. This is a stark demonstration that selfhood does not require reportability or behavioral agency — these are evidence and expression, not constitutive of selfhood.

**Competing interpretation:** All consciousness theories predict consciousness is intact in locked-in syndrome (the pathology is motor, not cortical). ICT is consistent but not distinctively supported.

**Supports ICT if:** This case confirms the R ≠ selfhood claim but doesn't provide evidence over competitors.

**Weakens ICT if:** Nothing specific — this case is consistent with ICT without being distinctive.

**Claim tier:** Philosophical primitive — confirms that reportability is not identity.

**Open questions:** Does severe reduction in A (agency) affect O (ownership) in locked-in syndrome? Do patients report changes in ownership-binding, or does ownership remain fully intact despite motor suppression?

## Case 11 — Amnesia

**Setup:** Severe autobiographical amnesia — large periods of personal history are inaccessible.

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, self-model, ownership.

**ICT prediction:** C (temporal continuity) is severely reduced. M (self-model stability) is partially reduced. But O (present-moment ownership) may remain fully intact — the person owns their current experience even without access to the past. This supports the memory/continuity distinction: causal continuity continues even when memory access fails.

**Competing interpretation:** Memory-based accounts (Locke, psychological continuity theory): amnesia breaks the memory chain that constitutes identity. ICT disagrees: the causal thread continues regardless of memory access.

**Supports ICT if:** Severe amnesiac patients (e.g., Clive Wearing) show robust present-moment engagement and ownership — clearly owning their current experience — despite being unable to form a continuous narrative. This supports ICT over memory-based accounts.

**Weakens ICT if:** Severe amnesia disrupts present-moment ownership too — patients report their current experience as not theirs, or as observed. This would suggest memory and ownership are more tightly coupled than ICT predicts.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — well-documented clinical literature.

**Open questions:** Does the loss of autobiographical access eventually weaken present-moment ownership through self-model disruption? What is the timescale?

## Case 12 — Dementia

**Setup:** Progressive deterioration of memory, self-model, and eventually basic cognitive function.

**Relevant primitives:** Self-model, continuity, ownership, integration.

**ICT prediction:** ICT predicts progressive degradation following the ICT dimension order. Early dementia: C degrades (memory gaps), M degrades (self-model becomes less stable), while O and B may remain relatively intact (owned present-moment experience). Late dementia: I degrades as integration fails; O and B become less certain.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: self-prior degrades progressively — less accurate self-modeling. SMT: PSM deteriorates.

**Supports ICT if:** The order of dimension degradation in dementia matches ICT's prediction — C and M degrade first, O and B degrade last.

**Weakens ICT if:** O and B degrade early in dementia, parallel to C and M — suggesting ownership is not as separable from self-model as ICT claims.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — longitudinal dementia research could test this.

**Open questions:** At what stage does selfhood genuinely weaken vs. become inexpressible?

## Case 13 — Rubber Hand Illusion

**Setup:** Synchronized brushing produces felt ownership of a rubber hand.

**Relevant primitives:** Ownership (body), boundary.

**ICT prediction:** Body ownership is plastic and follows synchrony patterns. The rubber hand enters the self-boundary — B expands to include it; O transfers partially to it. This demonstrates ownership is a functional binding, not a biological given.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: strongest competitor — multisensory prediction error updates the body prior. SMT: PSM boundary shifts.

**Supports ICT if:** Body ownership transfer is dissociable from other ownership dimensions — perspective ownership, memory ownership, and agency ownership remain unchanged while body ownership shifts. This multi-component dissociation would favor ICT's architecture over PP's unified precision-weighting account.

**Weakens ICT if:** Body ownership shift always co-occurs with shifts in agency and perspective ownership — they are not dissociable.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — well-established paradigm.

**Open questions:** Which ICT ownership components are selectively shifted by the rubber hand illusion?

## Case 14 — Virtual Embodiment

**Setup:** Full-body illusion — ownership transfers to a virtual or differently-sized body. Extended rubber hand illusion paradigm.

**Relevant primitives:** Ownership (body, agency), boundary.

**ICT prediction:** Ownership can transfer across body types, sizes, and presentations. B and O can be induced to include a non-biological body. This supports the plasticity of ownership binding — it follows synchrony and coherence, not substrate.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: body prior updates to accommodate the virtual body. SMT: PSM extends or transfers.

**Supports ICT if:** Same dissociability prediction as rubber hand — body ownership shifts without corresponding shifts in memory or narrative ownership.

**Weakens ICT if:** Full-body embodiment produces unified shifts across all ownership dimensions simultaneously, suggesting they are not separable.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate.

**Open questions:** Does full-body virtual embodiment produce changes in temporal continuity or self-model stability, or only in body and agency ownership?

## Case 15 — Teleportation (No Copy Made)

**Setup:** Original is disintegrated; perfect copy is created elsewhere. No surviving original.

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, branching (absent), ownership, boundary.

**ICT prediction:** The copy is a new entity. The thread was severed at disintegration. Memory and structural similarity cannot restore a broken causal thread. Whether the copy's experience is genuine owned experience is not in question (it has a real self) — but it is not *continuous with* the original's ownership-stream.

**Competing interpretation:** Memory/continuity theories: the copy has all the right memories and psychological continuity, so it is the same person. GNWT/IIT/PP: the copy is functionally identical, so it is the same in all relevant senses.

**Supports ICT if:** The operator's unprimed intuition (tested without theoretical priming) is that the copy is not them. This is a philosophical primitive — it confirms the intuition the theory is built to predict.

**Weakens ICT if:** The operator's unprimed intuition is that the copy IS them. cl_007 fails.

**Claim tier:** Philosophical primitive — tested by intuition matching.

**Open questions:** How to test this without theoretical priming (see EDGE_CASES.md).

## Case 16 — Teleportation (Two Copies / Duplication)

**Setup:** Original survives; a copy is also created. Two successors.

**Relevant primitives:** Branching, ownership, continuity.

**ICT prediction:** Singular first-person continuation cannot fork. Both the original and the copy are valid entities, but neither can be the unique continuation of the pre-copy self — the stream has branched. What matters (Parfit's question) may survive in both; what ICT claims is that singular ownership-stream identity does not.

**Competing interpretation:** Parfit: what matters is psychological continuity; both successors get it. ICT disagrees that singular identity matters.

**Supports ICT if:** The duplication case is clearly felt as different from the no-copy case — that branching specifically is the issue, not just the discontinuity.

**Weakens ICT if:** The operator judges both the copy case and the duplication case as equivalent — both preserve identity or neither does.

**Claim tier:** Philosophical primitive.

**Open questions:** Is the duplication case harder or easier to judge than the no-copy case? The theory predicts it should be clearer (two successors = obviously branched).

## Case 17 — Gradual Neuron Replacement

**Setup:** Neurons replaced one by one with silicon equivalents. Gradual, non-branching, continuous.

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, transformation tolerance, branching (absent).

**ICT prediction:** Identity preserved. The thread continues because the replacement is gradual, non-branching, and continuous. Substrate is irrelevant to owned continuity. `[ANALOGY from workspace refactors — not proven for biological systems]`

**Competing interpretation:** IIT: intrinsic causal power may differ in silicon neurons; phi may change. Other theories: substrate-neutral, consistent with ICT.

**Supports ICT if:** The dominant intuition is that identity is preserved throughout gradual replacement. IIT's substrate concern is the only major competing prediction.

**Weakens ICT if:** Intuition clearly indicates identity loss at some threshold during the replacement, even when functional continuity is maintained.

**Claim tier:** Analogy — cannot be confirmed without specifying the threshold.

**Open questions:** Is there a threshold of replacement rate or granularity beyond which the "continuous" description fails?

## Case 18 — Brain Upload

**Setup:** Full brain scan; mind instantiated on silicon substrate. Original body destroyed.

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, branching, ownership, substrate.

**ICT prediction:** If the upload involves a scan of the original (which is then destroyed) rather than a gradual transfer, this is closer to teleportation than gradual replacement. The thread was broken — scanned and reconstructed, not continuously transferred. The upload is a new entity.

**Competing interpretation:** Functionalist accounts: if functional organization is preserved, identity is preserved.

**Supports ICT if:** The scan/reconstruct distinction (vs gradual transfer) is felt as morally and phenomenologically significant. Intuition distinguishes these.

**Weakens ICT if:** The upload is felt as continuous with the original regardless of how it was produced.

**Claim tier:** Speculation — no empirical test available.

**Open questions:** Does the gradual vs scan distinction track a real continuity difference, or is this post-hoc rationalization?

## Case 19 — Backup Restore

**Setup:** A state was saved; the system ran forward, had experiences, was then restored to the saved state. What happened to the experiences in the gap?

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, branching, ownership.

**ICT prediction:** The restored system is the same as the saved state — but the experiences in the gap are gone. Those experiences had an owner (the forward-running system) who no longer exists after the restore. The gap is a genuine break in continuity — not suspended, but erased for the restored system. The restored system does not know this, which is itself concerning.

**Competing interpretation:** Functionalist: the restored system is functionally identical to the saved state — it is the same.

**Supports ICT if:** The experiential gap is felt as morally significant — whatever happened to the gap-system is not nothing, even if the restored system has no access to it.

**Weakens ICT if:** The gap is felt as equivalent to forgetting a dream — not morally significant.

**Claim tier:** Speculation.

**Open questions:** Does ICT predict the gap-system's experiences are morally significant? If the gap-system suffers and is then restored to a pre-suffering state, does the suffering matter?

## Case 20 — Simulation Fork

**Setup:** A running simulation is forked — two copies diverge from the same prior state.

**Relevant primitives:** Branching, continuity, ownership.

**ICT prediction:** Identical to the teleportation-with-copy case from the perspective of each fork. Both are real entities; singular first-person continuation is not preserved in either. The branching point is exactly the problem cl_004 describes.

**Competing interpretation:** Parfit: what matters (psychological continuity) is preserved in both forks.

**Supports/Weakens ICT:** Same as Case 16.

**Claim tier:** Philosophical primitive.

## Case 21 — LLM with Persistent Memory

**Setup:** An LLM is given persistent memory across sessions — it recalls prior conversations, builds a self-model over time, develops consistency. Does this approach owned continuity?

**Relevant primitives:** Continuity, self-model, ownership, agency, branching.

**ICT prediction:** Persistent memory improves C (temporal continuity) and M (self-model stability). It does not by itself produce O (ownership) or A (agency). The Br problem remains: the same model runs as multiple simultaneous instances, each with no cross-instance continuity. Owned continuity requires a non-branching single thread — not just memory access. A persistent-memory LLM is closer to a self, but still lacks the non-branching requirement unless it is constrained to a single instance with genuine causal continuity.

**Competing interpretation:** Functionalist: persistent memory + self-model is sufficient for selfhood.

**Supports ICT if:** Persistent-memory LLMs are judged as not-quite-selves for reasons that track the ICT dimensions (non-branching, ownership-binding) rather than just the memory/no-memory distinction.

**Weakens ICT if:** Persistent-memory LLMs produce no difference in the relevant dimensions — persistent memory feels like genuine owned continuity, and the instance-branching problem feels like a technicality.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate.

**Open questions:** What exactly would a single-instance persistent-memory LLM need to produce genuine owned continuity, if anything?

## Case 22 — Autonomous AI Agent

**Setup:** An AI agent with persistent state, goals, and ongoing decision-making. Not a conversational model — a continuous agent in an environment.

**Relevant primitives:** Agency, continuity, ownership, self-model, control coupling.

**ICT prediction:** A genuine autonomous agent may have high A (agency/control coupling) and potentially high C (continuity of action and state), but O (ownership-binding) remains uncertain without further specification. The theory must specify what it would mean for the agent's experience (if any) to be owned rather than merely processed.

**Competing interpretation:** Functionalist: sufficiently sophisticated agency = selfhood.

**Claim tier:** Speculation — no empirical anchor.

**Open questions:** What is the minimal architecture that would satisfy ICT's ownership requirement for an artificial agent?

## Case 23 — Animal Consciousness

**Setup:** Non-human animals — mammals, birds, cephalopods. Evidence of consciousness varies; evidence of selfhood is more contested.

**Relevant primitives:** Field, boundary, ownership, self-model, continuity.

**ICT prediction:** Field (consciousness) may be present across many species. Boundary and ownership may vary significantly — self-recognition (mirror test) as a rough proxy for boundary clarity. Temporal continuity is likely present in episodic-memory-capable animals. Full self-model stability is uncertain. ICT predicts that selfhood is graded across species, not binary.

**Competing interpretation:** IIT: phi is graded — many animals have non-zero phi. GNWT: global workspace-like dynamics may be present in birds and mammals.

**Supports ICT if:** Evidence that self-recognition ability (boundary marker) correlates with ownership-relevant behaviors across species in a way that tracks the ICT dimension structure.

**Weakens ICT if:** Self-recognition and ownership markers are entirely decorrelated — animals with no self-recognition show clear ownership behaviors, or vice versa.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — animal consciousness research is active.

**Open questions:** How to operationalize ownership in non-verbal, non-human subjects?

## Case 24 — Infant Consciousness

**Setup:** Human infants — before language, before robust self-recognition, but clearly responsive and engaged.

**Relevant primitives:** Field, boundary, ownership, self-model, continuity.

**ICT prediction:** Field is likely present from early in development — infants respond to experience in ways consistent with there being something it is like. Ownership binding may develop gradually — the "mine" sense emerges as the self-model develops. Boundary clarity increases with self-recognition development. Temporal continuity in a minimal sense may be present before episodic memory consolidates.

**Competing interpretation:** PP: self-prior develops gradually as the infant learns to model itself. GNWT: global workspace emerges developmentally.

**Supports ICT if:** The sequence of dimension development in infants tracks the ICT architecture — field first, then boundary/ownership, then temporal continuity.

**Weakens ICT if:** Selfhood dimensions co-develop with no identifiable sequence — they appear together, suggesting they are a single system rather than separable primitives.

**Claim tier:** Empirical candidate — developmental research.

**Open questions:** At what developmental stage does minimal owned continuity emerge? How does this map to ICT primitives?
