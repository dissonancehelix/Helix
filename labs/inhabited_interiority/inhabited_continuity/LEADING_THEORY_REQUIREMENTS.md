# Leading Theory Requirements
## What ICT Must Prove to Become Field-Leading

**Current position:** ICT is a conceptual framework with a promising architecture. It is not a leading theory. It becomes leading only by meeting the requirements below — not by claiming to meet them.

**Scope:** ICT is strongest as a theory of conscious selfhood, ownership, and identity-through-time. It is not yet a complete theory of consciousness. These requirements apply within that scope. Meeting them within this domain is ambitious enough.

**Central requirement:** ICT must show that owned continuity is a real explanatory variable — that it does work which cannot be done by memory similarity, reportability, global access, integration, narrative, or self-modeling alone.

---

## Requirement 1 — Consciousness and Selfhood Must Be Shown to Be Separable

**The claim:** Consciousness (appearance-from-within, there being something it is like) can remain while selfhood weakens or fragments. Selfhood requires more than consciousness — it requires owned continuity of the appearance.

**Why this matters:** If consciousness and selfhood are always co-present and co-absent, then ICT's selfhood framework adds nothing to existing consciousness theories. The separability claim is what gives ICT its foothold.

**What must be shown:**
- Documented cases where consciousness is plausibly present but ownership is disrupted: depersonalization (appearance persists but "mine"-ness weakens), ego dissolution (appearance transforms but selfhood fragmenting)
- Documented cases where selfhood-relevant behavior is intact but consciousness is uncertain: vegetative state patients who track commands
- Analysis of whether the dissociation is real or apparent — whether the cases can be explained without separating consciousness from selfhood

**Current status:** Plausible but not demonstrated. The dissociation cases exist; the analysis is incomplete.

---

## Requirement 2 — Ownership Must Be Operationalized

**The claim:** Ownership — the mark that experience is occurring as mine — is a real functional property that can be measured, not just reported.

**Why this matters:** A concept that can only be verified by self-report is scientifically tractable but vulnerable to confabulation and the hard problem simultaneously. ICT needs measurable proxies for ownership if it is to generate empirical predictions.

**Required proxies (at minimum):**
- **Body ownership** — captured by rubber hand illusion, full-body illusion; well-established paradigm
- **Agency ownership** — the sense that actions are mine; disrupted in alien hand syndrome, certain psychotic states
- **Memory ownership** — that past experiences are recalled as mine, not as a third-person story
- **Affective ownership** — that emotions are felt as mine, not observed
- **Attentional ownership** — that the direction of attention is mine, not experienced as externally controlled
- **Narrative ownership** — that the personal history coheres as one story from a single perspective
- **Perspective ownership** — that the current viewpoint is mine, not observed from outside
- **Control coupling** — that the system's actions are tightly coupled to its perspective (disrupted in depersonalization)

**Not all of these need measurable proxies immediately.** But at least two must be operationalized before the theory can make empirical claims about ownership. Body ownership and agency ownership are the most tractable entry points.

**Current status:** Body ownership is operationalized (rubber hand illusion paradigm exists). Others are conceptually specified but not operationalized.

---

## Requirement 3 — Continuity Must Outperform Memory Similarity as a Predictor

**The claim:** Owned continuity is not the same as memory similarity. A person with severe amnesia may retain genuine owned continuity (the thread continues even if access is lost). A teleporter copy may have perfect memory similarity but no owned continuity (the thread was severed).

**Why this matters:** Memory similarity is the most natural alternative account of personal identity. If ICT cannot show that its continuity concept predicts identity judgments better than memory similarity, it has no advantage over standard memory-based accounts (Locke, Parfit).

**What must be shown:**
- Cases where memory similarity is high but continuity is broken (teleportation/duplication thought experiments; not empirically testable but intuition-mapping is the proxy)
- Cases where continuity is preserved but memory access is severely disrupted (amnesia, certain disorders of consciousness)
- That the theory's predictions for these cases differ from memory-based predictions, and that the operator's intuitions (and ideally others') track the ICT predictions rather than the memory-similarity predictions

**Current status:** The teleporter case is correctly classified as a philosophical primitive — it predicts an intuition, not an empirical result. The amnesia case needs more careful analysis.

---

## Requirement 4 — Branching Must Be Shown to Matter, Not Assumed

**The claim:** Branching — the division of an ownership-stream into multiple successors — threatens singular first-person continuity.

**Why this matters:** This is the most contested and most consequential structural claim in the theory. If branching is benign (as Parfit argues — what matters survives in both branches), then the non-branching requirement is wrong and the theory's predictions about teleportation, split-brain, and duplication all fail.

**What must be shown:**
- That the non-branching requirement produces intuitions the operator actually endorses (live teleporter test — see EDGE_CASES.md)
- That the theory's prediction differs from Parfit's "what matters" account in a specific, articulable way
- That the split-brain literature does not refute the claim (see cl_004 empirical_pressure section)
- A response to Parfit's argument that survival without identity is what matters — not whether the chain is singular

**Current status:** High-risk. cl_004 has active empirical pressure. The philosophical debate is unresolved. Do not treat this as confirmed.

---

## Requirement 5 — Split-Brain Must Be Handled Explicitly

**The claim:** The split-brain case either supports, threatens, or is neutral to the non-branching requirement — and ICT must specify which and why.

**Why this matters:** If split-brain patients show genuine unified owned consciousness after commissurotomy (as Pinto et al. suggest), then anatomical branching does not produce ownership-stream branching. This either weakens the non-branching requirement or forces a distinction between levels of branching.

**What must be shown:**
- Explicit distinction between divided perception, divided report, divided access, divided agency, and divided owned consciousness
- A specification of which level ICT's branching claim applies to
- A prediction for split-brain cases that is distinguishable from "confabulation explains whatever unity is reported"
- Engagement with Pinto et al. (2017) and Schechter (2018) directly

**Current status:** Partially addressed in CLAIM_LEDGER.yaml and EMPIRICAL_CONTACTS.md. The five-level distinction is defined. The specific prediction distinguishing ICT from confabulation-based accounts has not been written.

---

## Requirement 6 — AI Self-Report Must Be Sharpened

**The claim:** AI self-report is insufficient evidence for owned continuity. A system can have high-fidelity self-representation without having owned temporal continuity.

**Why this matters:** The AI case is increasingly practically relevant. ICT's analysis of LLMs needs to be precise enough to predict what would change if a system had persistent memory, continuous state, or genuine agency — and what would not change even then.

**What must be shown — distinctions ICT must draw:**
- **Self-talk** vs **self-model**: reporting on one's states vs maintaining a stable model of oneself over time
- **Self-model** vs **persistent memory**: having a representation of oneself vs having access to prior states
- **Persistent memory** vs **agency**: remembering the past vs acting on a continuous perspective
- **Agency** vs **owned interiority**: acting from a perspective vs that perspective feeling like mine
- **Owned interiority** vs **temporal ownership-stream**: having owned experience in a moment vs owning a continuous history

ICT predicts that current LLMs satisfy self-talk and partial self-model, but fail at persistent memory (cross-session), agency (no genuine goal-directed perspective), owned interiority (uncertain), and temporal ownership-stream (definitionally absent across sessions).

What would a persistent-memory LLM with continuous agency satisfy? The theory needs to specify this before such systems exist and are used as test cases.

**Current status:** cl_008 is classified as empirical_candidate. The distinctions above are implied by the theory but not written out explicitly.

## Requirement 7 — Adversarial Predictions Must Be Generated

**The claim:** ICT must make predictions that differ from at least GNWT, IIT, and self-model theory in a direction that can be tested.

**Why this matters:** If ICT always agrees with existing theories, it is not a theory — it is a vocabulary. If it always disagrees, it is probably wrong. The goal is specific points of divergence where the theories predict different results.

**What must be produced:**
- At least 5 cases where ICT predicts a different direction from at least one competing theory
- For each: the experimental design that could distinguish them
- For each: what result would favor ICT and what result would weaken it

See ADVERSARIAL_PREDICTIONS.md.

**Current status:** Drafted. Predictions are cautious and marked where they are weak.

---

## Requirement 8 — The Theory Must Have Hard Falsifiers

**The claim:** A theory that explains everything after the fact is not a theory. ICT must specify results that would require revision or rejection.

**Why this matters:** The theory's current architecture makes it easy to accommodate anomalies post-hoc. If owned continuity is disrupted, the theory attributes it to ownership failure. If continuity seems to survive branching, the theory attributes it to confabulation. This pattern must be broken by specifying in advance what results would count as evidence against the theory, not just against one claim.

See FAILURE_MODES.md for the full list.

**Current status:** FALSIFIERS.md exists with 8 hard falsifiers and 5 soft failure modes. These need to be tested against the case matrix.

## Summary

For ICT to become a leading theory of conscious selfhood, it must demonstrate:

**Owned continuity explains conscious selfhood better than memory, reportability, integration, or self-modeling alone.**

This requires separability (Req 1), operationalization (Req 2), predictive advantage over memory accounts (Req 3), empirical engagement with branching (Req 4), explicit split-brain handling (Req 5), a sharpened AI analysis (Req 6), adversarial predictions (Req 7), and falsifiers (Req 8).

Meeting all eight within the selfhood domain — not the full consciousness domain — is the target. A theory that successfully explains owned selfhood, fragmentation, and identity-through-time is a serious contribution even if it does not resolve the hard problem of consciousness.
