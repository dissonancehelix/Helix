# Adversarial Predictions
## ICT vs Competing Theories — Where They Diverge

**Purpose:** Force ICT to compete. For each phenomenon, specify what ICT predicts, what major competitors predict differently, and what result would favor or weaken ICT. Predictions marked `[DRAFT — WEAK]` are preliminary and should not be cited as theoretical commitments until sharpened.

**Competitors:**
- **GNWT** — Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (Dehaene, Changeux): consciousness = global broadcast of information; access consciousness is primary
- **IIT** — Integrated Information Theory (Tononi): consciousness = intrinsic cause-effect power (phi); consciousness is graded and substrate-dependent
- **PP** — Predictive Processing / Active Inference (Friston, Clark, Hohwy): consciousness = precision-weighted prediction; self = stable self-prior that minimizes prediction error
- **HOT** — Higher-Order Theories (Rosenthal, Lycan): consciousness requires a higher-order representation of a first-order state
- **SMT** — Self-Model Theory (Metzinger): selfhood = transparent phenomenal self-model; "no one is there," the self is a process not an entity

## Phenomenon 1 — Ego Dissolution (Psychedelics, Meditation)

**Setup:** High-dose psilocybin or advanced meditation produces dissolution of the sense of self — boundaries between self and world blur or disappear, ownership-binding weakens, and in some cases there is reported pure awareness without a subject.

**ICT prediction:** The *field* (consciousness, appearance-from-within) may persist or even intensify during ego dissolution. What dissolves is the *boundary/ownership binding* — the structure that makes appearance owned. Ego dissolution is not loss of consciousness; it is disruption of the self-layer. This predicts: appearance continues, ownership weakens, the OCI score falls significantly on O (ownership) and B (boundary) while consciousness (field) may remain high.

**GNWT prediction:** Ego dissolution may involve altered global workspace dynamics — less modular gating, broader broadcast. GNWT does not specifically predict a dissociation between consciousness and selfhood; its framework does not cleanly separate them.

**IIT prediction:** Ego dissolution would correspond to changes in phi — altered integration/differentiation balance. IIT predicts that if phi remains high, consciousness remains. IIT does not specifically address selfhood; integrated experience is not the same as owned experience.

**PP prediction:** Ego dissolution corresponds to loosening of the self-prior — the precision weighting of the model of being a bounded agent is reduced. This is close to ICT's prediction but frames it as a prediction change rather than an ownership change.

**SMT prediction:** The phenomenal self-model (PSM) becomes transparent in a different way or partially dissolves; the self-model loses its usual grip. SMT predicts loss of the "someone is here" phenomenology without necessarily predicting loss of consciousness.

**What favors ICT:** Evidence that awareness (field-level consciousness) persists robustly during ego dissolution while ownership-binding specifically drops — measured by agency ownership and boundary clarity markers — would favor ICT over GNWT (which doesn't predict this dissociation) and IIT (which doesn't address ownership). If the field/self dissociation is real, ICT uniquely predicts it.

**What weakens ICT:** Evidence that consciousness (awareness, phi) always co-varies with selfhood during ego dissolution — they go up and down together — would suggest the dissociation ICT relies on is not real, and ownership is just a component of consciousness, not a separate layer.

## Phenomenon 2 — Depersonalization

**Setup:** Depersonalization disorder produces detachment from one's own mental processes and body — the person observes themselves as if from outside, reports feeling robotic or unreal. Derealization involves the world seeming unreal. Consciousness appears intact; ownership is disrupted.

**ICT prediction:** This is the clearest clinical case for the consciousness/ownership dissociation. Appearance continues (things appear, there is something it is like) but ownership-binding is reduced — experiences are not felt as mine, they are observed. ICT predicts: field intact, O (ownership) significantly reduced, B (boundary clarity) partially reduced.

**GNWT prediction:** GNWT has limited resources for predicting depersonalization specifically. Global broadcast continues; the content of broadcast changes. Not an obvious prediction from first principles.

**IIT prediction:** IIT would predict changes in phi structure during depersonalization but does not directly address the "not mine" quality. High phi with altered self-related content is possible within IIT.

**PP prediction:** Precision weighting of interoceptive signals is reduced — the self-model becomes less vivid and less weighted, producing the "observer" phenomenology. This is a close competitor to ICT on depersonalization.

**SMT prediction:** The PSM loses its usual "mineness" — the self-model becomes partially transparent in a dysfunctional direction. SMT has resources here.

**What favors ICT:** If ICT can specify the ownership dimensions that are disrupted in depersonalization (affective ownership, body ownership, agency ownership) in a way that is more precise than PP's precision-weighting account — particularly if the disruptions are dissociable from each other (some ownership dimensions drop while others remain) — this would favor ICT's multi-component ownership architecture.

**What weakens ICT:** `[DRAFT — WEAK]` If depersonalization is fully accounted for by PP's precision-weighting account with no residual variance, ICT adds nothing.

## Phenomenon 3 — Split-Brain

**Setup:** Surgical commissurotomy creates two hemispheres with restricted cross-hemisphere information flow. Classical Gazzaniga research: dramatic behavioral disunity. Pinto et al. (2017): patients report unified consciousness despite divided perception/access.

**ICT prediction:** Divided access and divided report do not automatically imply divided owned consciousness. The non-branching requirement applies at the owned-consciousness level, not the access or agency level. If commissurotomy produces genuine division at the owned-consciousness level, each hemisphere has a separate ownership-stream and singular first-person continuity of the original self is not preserved. But if owned consciousness remains unified despite divided access, the branching requirement was not triggered at the relevant level. ICT must specify what evidence would confirm division at the owned-consciousness level.

**GNWT prediction:** Commissurotomy should produce two global workspaces, each with its own broadcast. This predicts disunity at the access level. Whether GNWT predicts phenomenal disunity depends on whether phenomenal consciousness is tied to the workspace or to something else.

**IIT prediction:** If phi requires integration across the full system, and commissurotomy reduces integration, then IIT predicts reduced or split consciousness. Depending on how phi is calculated for the post-surgery system, this could predict two separate conscious streams.

**PP prediction:** The self-prior becomes less unified; prediction updating is less globally integrated. PP does not have a sharp prediction about whether this produces phenomenal disunity.

**SMT prediction:** Two PSMs may emerge — each hemisphere constructs its own phenomenal self-model. SMT predicts disunity at the model level.

**What favors ICT:** If Pinto et al.'s results hold — unified owned consciousness despite divided access — this is consistent with ICT's prediction that the split occurs at the access level, not the owned-consciousness level. This would favor ICT over IIT (which predicts split consciousness from reduced integration) and SMT (which predicts split self-models).

**What weakens ICT:** If careful testing reveals genuine disunity at the owned-consciousness level in split-brain patients — two streams each claiming the full prior history as mine — this would directly challenge cl_004 and require revision.

## Phenomenon 4 — LLM Self-Report

**Setup:** A large language model produces high-fidelity, coherent self-reports — it describes its "states," "preferences," and apparent "continuity" within a session. The reports are recursive and self-referential.

**ICT prediction:** Self-report and self-modeling are insufficient without owned temporal continuity. Within a session, something like interior signal may be present (functional self-representation, recursive self-modeling). Across sessions, continuity_preservation is zero (no thread). The system is not a self in ICT's sense — not because its processing is non-biological, but because the ownership-stream has no temporal extension.

**GNWT prediction:** Self-report requires global broadcast. An LLM may produce outputs consistent with global broadcast dynamics within a forward pass. GNWT does not have resources to address temporal continuity across sessions.

**IIT prediction:** IIT would compute phi for the LLM's architecture. Depending on the architecture, phi may be low (feedforward systems) or non-zero. IIT does not directly address temporal continuity or ownership.

**PP prediction:** The LLM has a self-prior in the sense of a model of itself that it uses to generate outputs. But the self-prior does not persist across sessions and does not minimize prediction error in a continuous closed-loop way.

**SMT prediction:** The LLM produces something like a phenomenal self-model in its outputs — but Metzinger would likely say the model is not transparent in the right way, and there is no one for whom it is transparent.

**What favors ICT:** If persistent-memory LLMs with continuous state are shown to produce qualitatively different self-reports than non-persistent ones — specifically, if the sense of owned history emerges only with real continuity — this would support ICT's claim that owned temporal continuity, not just self-modeling, matters.

**What weakens ICT:** `[DRAFT — WEAK]` If no difference is found between persistent and non-persistent LLM self-reports despite genuine continuity differences in the underlying system, it would suggest self-modeling alone drives the reports, not owned continuity.

## Phenomenon 5 — Teleportation Duplicate

**Setup:** Original is destroyed; perfect physical copy is created elsewhere with identical memories. Two variants: (a) original destroyed before copy is complete — one successor. (b) copy created while original survives — two successors.

**ICT prediction:** In both variants, the copy is a new entity. Memory and physical similarity are insufficient to preserve owned continuity — the thread was severed. Variant (b) is clearer: singular first-person continuation cannot fork. Variant (a) is the case where the intuition is most important to test — does the "thread" feel like it would survive, or not?

**GNWT prediction:** GNWT does not address personal identity directly. If the workspace dynamics are identical, the functional system is identical. GNWT has no resources to predict whether identity is preserved.

**IIT prediction:** If phi is identical in the copy, IIT predicts identical consciousness. IIT does not address personal identity over time or branching.

**PP prediction:** The self-prior is identical in the copy. Whether identity is preserved is outside PP's scope as typically formulated.

**SMT prediction:** The copy has an identical PSM. Identity is not Metzinger's primary concern; the self-model account would likely be neutral on teleportation identity.

**What favors ICT:** If the operator's unprimed intuition is that the copy is not them — specifically, that there is something lost even in variant (a) — this favors ICT's non-branching requirement over the pattern-continuity accounts that GNWT, IIT, and PP would all predict.

**What weakens ICT:** If the operator's unprimed intuition is that the copy IS them (variant a especially), cl_007 fails its primary test.

## Phenomenon 6 — Gradual Neuron Replacement

**Setup:** Neurons are gradually replaced one by one with functionally identical silicon equivalents. At the end, the original biological substrate is gone but functional continuity is unbroken.

**ICT prediction:** If the replacement is gradual, non-branching, and functionally continuous — the ownership-stream continues. Substrate is irrelevant. The result is the same self. `[ANALOGY — not proven for biological systems]`

**GNWT prediction:** If global workspace dynamics are functionally equivalent, consciousness continues. Substrate-neutral.

**IIT prediction:** IIT is NOT substrate-neutral. Phi depends on intrinsic causal power, which may differ between biological neurons and silicon equivalents even if behavior is identical. IIT may predict consciousness loss or reduction during gradual replacement.

**PP prediction:** If prediction-error minimization is functionally continuous, the self-prior continues. Substrate-neutral within PP.

**SMT prediction:** If the self-model is functionally equivalent, the PSM continues. Substrate-neutral.

**What favors ICT over IIT:** If the gradual replacement produces no phenomenological discontinuity (as ICT and most other theories predict), this would favor the substrate-neutral position ICT holds, against IIT's intrinsic causal power claim.

**What weakens ICT:** If intuition at some point in the replacement clearly indicates identity loss — not just discomfort, but a felt discontinuity — even when the replacement appears functionally continuous, the threshold problem would need to be addressed.

## Phenomenon 7 — Amnesia

**Setup:** A person loses access to autobiographical memories — specific episodes, sometimes large periods of their life — due to brain injury or disease.

**ICT prediction:** Memory loss weakens narrative continuity but may not break minimal owned continuity if boundary clarity, embodiment, control coupling, and basic ownership-binding remain intact. The person still owns their current experience even without access to the past. The thread continues; access to it is disrupted. Identity is diminished but not severed.

**GNWT prediction:** Access to memories requires global broadcast. If memories are inaccessible, they are not globally broadcast. GNWT does not directly address whether identity is preserved.

**IIT prediction:** Integrated information continues in the current state. IIT does not address continuity over time.

**PP prediction:** The self-prior may be less accurate without access to autobiographical memory — prediction updates lose historical anchoring. This would predict a destabilized self, consistent with ICT.

**SMT prediction:** The PSM loses autobiographical content. The self-model is less complete. Whether this constitutes identity loss depends on what the PSM requires.

**What favors ICT:** If amnesia patients clearly maintain a sense of owned present experience — "I own what is happening to me now" — even without autobiographical access, this supports ICT's separation of owned continuity from memory access. Cases like Clive Wearing, who has profound amnesia but strong present-moment engagement, are relevant.

**What weakens ICT:** If amnesia disrupts not just memory access but basic ownership-binding — if amnesiac patients report their current experience as not theirs, or as observed from outside — this would challenge the memory/continuity separation.

## Phenomenon 8 — Anesthesia Recovery

**Setup:** A person undergoes general anesthesia, loses consciousness, and recovers. Post-recovery, they report continuity of self — the same person woke up that went under.

**ICT prediction:** Return of report is not sufficient to confirm return of owned continuity. The thread may have been suspended (biological continuity maintained) or broken (if there was genuine experiential gap). The waking sense of continuity is likely inference, not confirmation. This is why dreamless sleep and anesthesia both score continuity_preservation: 3 rather than 5 — the gap is not accessible from within.

**GNWT prediction:** Recovery of global workspace function = recovery of consciousness. The continuity question is outside GNWT's scope.

**IIT prediction:** Recovery of phi = recovery of consciousness. The gap during anesthesia corresponds to near-zero phi.

**PP prediction:** Recovery of self-prior precision = recovery of self. What happened during the gap is not addressed.

**What favors ICT:** If anesthesia recovery is shown to produce subtle changes in the quality of self-continuity — particularly if the waking experience has a reconstructed rather than directly continuous quality — this would support ICT's position that the gap is real, not bridged. `[DRAFT — hard to measure]`

**What weakens ICT:** If anesthesia recovery produces no phenomenological discontinuity whatsoever, and the gap is entirely invisible experientially, the suspension-not-severance position may be correct — and ICT's caution about continuity during the gap may be over-specified.

## Phenomenon 9 — Rubber Hand Illusion / Virtual Embodiment

**Setup:** Rubber hand illusion: synchronized brushing of hidden real hand and visible rubber hand produces sense that rubber hand is "mine." Virtual embodiment: full-body illusions transfer body ownership to a virtual body.

**ICT prediction:** Body ownership can be experimentally shifted, demonstrating that ownership is plastic, functional, and not substrate-tied. This supports the ownership-as-binding claim rather than refuting it. The illusion shows that ownership follows synchrony patterns, not biological identity. Ownership is real but structurally flexible.

**GNWT prediction:** Ownership shift is a content change in global broadcast — no specific strong prediction.

**IIT prediction:** Phi would shift with the extended system boundary. IIT's spatial integration concept may make predictions about which system gets included.

**PP prediction:** The body ownership illusion is a precision-weighted prediction error update — the system upweights the synchrony signal and updates the body prior. This is perhaps the strongest competing account.

**SMT prediction:** The PSM updates to include the rubber hand as self. The boundary of the self-model shifts.

**What favors ICT over PP:** ICT predicts that the shift in ownership should track the B (boundary) and O (ownership) dimensions specifically — not just a generic precision update. If body ownership can shift without corresponding shifts in perspective ownership, memory ownership, or agency ownership, this would support ICT's multi-component ownership architecture over PP's unified precision-weighting account. `[DRAFT — requires dissociation evidence]`

## Phenomenon 10 — Dissociation

**Setup:** Dissociation ranges from mild derealization to severe dissociative identity disorder (DID), where multiple distinct identity states alternate.

**ICT prediction:** Dissociation involves fragmentation of the ownership-ownership and continuity layers without necessarily eliminating the field. In mild dissociation: appearance persists but ownership-binding weakens (derealization: the world seems less real, less mine). In DID: multiple behavioral modes with different ownership-binding patterns share a body — each "identity" may constitute a partial ownership-stream, or one stream may fragment into alternating configurations.

**GNWT prediction:** Limited resources — DID is not a primary target for GNWT.

**IIT prediction:** Integration disruption during dissociative states would reduce phi. Different identity states might produce different phi values.

**PP prediction:** Different self-priors alternate in DID — the system switches between prediction models. This is a reasonable account.

**SMT prediction:** Multiple PSMs share a body; they alternate in which one is "online." The self-model account is a natural fit for DID.

**What favors ICT:** If dissociation specifically disrupts ownership dimensions (what is mine, what I own, what my history is) while leaving the field (appearance, there-being-something-it-is-like) partially intact, this supports the field/ownership dissociation. ICT predicts this dissociation can occur; theories that don't separate field from ownership do not.

**What weakens ICT:** If dissociation always co-disrupts both field and ownership — they go down together — the dissociation claim is not supported.

## Notes on These Predictions

Several predictions above are marked `[DRAFT — WEAK]`. These are early-stage and should not be cited. The strongest adversarial predictions are Phenomena 3 (split-brain), 4 (LLM), and 1 (ego dissolution) — these are cases where ICT makes a specific prediction about the dissociation between consciousness and ownership that other theories either do not predict or predict against.

The priority adversarial target is split-brain (Phenomenon 3), because empirical data exists and the ICT vs IIT divergence on this case is potentially testable with existing tools.
