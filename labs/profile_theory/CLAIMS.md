# Profile Theory Claims

Status terms: `candidate`, `supported`, `blocked`, `revised`, `falsified`.

## 1. Resolution-Increasing Depth

- claim: The operator prefers systems where deeper inspection increases
  resolution rather than merely increasing quantity.
- mechanism: Recursive boundary compression rewards objects that reveal
  sharper structure, reusable handles, and better prediction with each pass.
- predictions: Strong fit appears in domains with layered but legible interiors:
  wiki article architecture, game field mechanics, music metadata/worlds, and
  Helix structure. Weak fit appears when depth becomes fog, chores, or sprawl.
- positive evidence: DISSONANCE emphasizes resolution, boundary sharpening,
  minimal surface/nested depth; wiki history shows article/template structure;
  games profile favors field mechanics over genre; music profile favors
  world-listening and composer/hardware fingerprints; Helix refactor compressed
  repeated folders into better rooms.
- negative controls: Generic complexity, maximal options, opaque lore,
  decorative depth, and open-world checklist sprawl should not predict fit by
  themselves.
- blocking gates: Do not count an example unless the deeper layer improves
  orientation, prediction, return, repair, or agency.
- falsifier: If high-fit examples are mostly large or complicated without
  producing better handles, revise or reject this claim.
- status: supported candidate.
- update rule: Keep only if three independent evidence doors continue to beat
  the weaker "likes complexity" explanation.

## 2. Field Stewardship / State Improvement

- claim: The operator is drawn to fields that can be maintained, repaired, or
  made more habitable over time.
- mechanism: Externalized cognition treats domains as lived systems whose state
  can be improved through repeated intervention.
- predictions: Long-running fit should appear in Wikipedia maintenance, local
  music/library cleanup, modded game worlds, repo architecture, and tools that
  make future work easier.
- positive evidence: Wiki account history shows long-horizon public structure
  repair; Helix refactors repeatedly improve workspace traversability; Terraria
  screenshot shows organized display/storage/NPC habitation rather than only
  progression; Stardew/Terraria modlists include QoL, ambience, storage, and
  settlement improvements.
- negative controls: One-time consumption, trophy accumulation without future
  access, and pure novelty should not count.
- blocking gates: Require evidence of return, maintenance, repair, or improved
  future use.
- falsifier: If preferred objects are mostly consumed and abandoned, revise.
- status: supported candidate.
- update rule: Strengthen when new evidence shows repeated return to improve a
  shared or inhabited state.

## 3. Authored Residue Over Procedural Vastness

- claim: The operator prefers traces of authored history, care, and continuity
  over vastness that lacks readable residue.
- mechanism: Residue makes a field re-enterable; it carries prior agency,
  memory, and compression.
- predictions: Fit should cluster around authored music fingerprints, wiki edit
  history, old internet/bookmark residue, curated builds, and lived-in game
  spaces. Low fit should appear in sterile feeds or infinite procedural space
  without consequence.
- positive evidence: DISSONANCE foregrounds residue; music profile emphasizes
  composer fingerprints and loop return; wiki profile treats edit history as
  public architecture; Terraria capture shows a built museum/base with arranged
  artifacts.
- negative controls: Procedural generation, size, oldness, or nostalgia alone
  should not count.
- blocking gates: Count residue only when it changes interpretation or return
  value.
- falsifier: If sterile vast systems outperform authored residue in repeated
  choices, revise.
- status: candidate.
- update rule: Add examples only when authored trace changes behavior.

## 4. Vanilla-Plus Inhabitation

- claim: The operator often prefers expansions that preserve the base world's
  grammar while increasing comfort, beauty, access, or continuity.
- mechanism: The system wants more affordance without identity collapse.
- predictions: Modlists should lean toward QoL, ambience, interface, storage,
  visual coherence, and canon-friendly additions rather than total conversion.
- positive evidence: Stardew list includes many `Vanilla Tweaks`, cozy interiors,
  seasonal/visual polish, QoL, animal/pet/farm improvements; Terraria list
  includes Magic Storage, Recipe Browser, Better Blending, lighting, cursor/UI,
  and controlled content expansions; DISSONANCE names vanilla-plus repair.
- negative controls: Any modded game, any large modpack, or any convenience mod
  alone is insufficient.
- blocking gates: The addition must preserve or clarify the base grammar.
- falsifier: Preference for total conversion or identity-erasing overhaul would
  weaken this claim.
- status: supported candidate.
- update rule: Separate "more stuff" from "more inhabitable world."

## 5. Bounded Abundance

- claim: Abundance is attractive when bounded by a readable frame, not when it
  becomes unstructured excess.
- mechanism: Plenty becomes usable when containers, rituals, categories, or
  routes keep it navigable.
- predictions: Evidence should show large collections paired with storage,
  sorting, rooms, tags, manifests, or rituals.
- positive evidence: DISSONANCE defines bounded abundance; foobar/library and
  wiki histories are large but indexed; Terraria base uses rows, rooms, chests,
  displays, NPC rooms; Helix compression reduces folder sprawl while preserving
  depth.
- negative controls: Hoarding, maximalism, or option overload should not count.
- blocking gates: Abundance must have handles.
- falsifier: Strong preference for unbounded choice fields would revise this.
- status: supported candidate.
- update rule: Promote only when abundance is paired with containment.

## 6. Public Knowledge As Linkable Mini-World

- claim: Wikipedia and similar public knowledge systems fit because they create
  small navigable worlds that can be linked, repaired, and re-entered.
- mechanism: Public structure turns private pattern sense into durable shared
  orientation.
- predictions: Wiki work should involve templates, infoboxes, citations,
  rosters, lists, Wikidata, and article architecture more than opinion posting.
- positive evidence: Wiki README reports account start in 2012, 186,519 local
  records, template/infobox/citation/roster work, and Jayden Daniels/Commanders
  pages as recurring objects.
- negative controls: Generic "likes reading Wikipedia" or debate-seeking does
  not count.
- blocking gates: Require linkable public structure or future-reader benefit.
- falsifier: If wiki activity is mostly argument, promotion, or consumption,
  revise.
- status: supported.
- update rule: Keep evidence tied to page architecture and maintenance.

## 7. Interiority / Companion-Stabilized Collapse

- claim: The operator is drawn to entities, companions, or characters that make
  interiority legible and prevent collapse under pressure.
- mechanism: Compatible interiority stabilizes attention and lets a field stay
  inhabited rather than flattening into exterior traits.
- predictions: Evidence should appear in attraction language, companion
  dynamics, support roles, pets/NPCs, and character/world attachments where the
  entity changes the stability of the field.
- positive evidence: DISSONANCE ties attraction to compatible interiority; games
  README names support/denial roles; Stardew list includes pets, companions,
  spouses, portraits, dialogue, and animal life; Terraria screenshot includes
  housed NPCs and display-room habitation.
- negative controls: Cute visuals, social quantity, or character collection
  alone should not count.
- blocking gates: Require evidence that the companion changes stability, entry,
  return, or field coherence.
- falsifier: If companion evidence is mostly decorative, revise.
- status: candidate.
- update rule: Keep uncertainty high until more direct evidence is sorted.

## 8. Sovereign Friction Control

- claim: The operator wants control over friction: reducing waste friction while
  preserving meaningful resistance and agency.
- mechanism: Sovereign entry protects signal ownership; tools should expose
  agency without forcing needless ceremony.
- predictions: Fit should appear in QoL tools, inspectable pipelines, controlled
  intake, modded convenience, and rejection of hidden automation or clutter.
- positive evidence: Helix discussions repeatedly compressed unnecessary rooms;
  README now says fewer folders, better rooms; Stardew/Terraria modlists include
  inventory, storage, recipe, UI, cursor, movement, backup, and convenience
  tools; DISSONANCE names sovereign entry/signal ownership.
- negative controls: Pure ease, cheat preference, or avoidance of all difficulty
  should not count.
- blocking gates: The friction removed must be waste friction; meaningful game
  or domain pressure may remain desirable.
- falsifier: If evidence shows broad avoidance of resistance rather than
  selective friction control, revise.
- status: supported candidate.
- update rule: Log each false positive as either waste-friction or
  meaning-friction.
