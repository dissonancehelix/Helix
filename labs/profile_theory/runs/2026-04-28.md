# Run 2026-04-28

## Test

Compare the claim **Resolution-Increasing Depth** against the weaker claim
**likes complexity**.

## Candidate Claim

The operator prefers systems where deeper inspection increases resolution:
better handles, better orientation, better repair, better return, or better
agency.

## Weaker Claim

The operator likes complexity.

## Evidence Doors

1. `DISSONANCE.md`: repeatedly frames value in terms of resolution, boundary
   sharpening, compression, minimal surface/nested depth, and false-positive
   control.
2. `domains/wiki/WIKI.md`: the Dissident93 account is not just high-volume;
   it shows public structure work across articles, templates, citations,
   rosters, Wikidata, Commons, and recurring pages such as Jayden Daniels and
   Washington Commanders objects.
3. `domains/games/GAMES.md`: games are predicted by field mechanics, roles,
   topology, denial, return value, and guide-layer understanding, not by genre
   or sheer difficulty.
4. `domains/music/MUSIC.md`: music fit is modeled through world-listening,
   composer fingerprints, VGM hardware grammar, loop seams, metadata, and
   return pressure, not by genre labels or volume of tracks.
5. Helix refactor discussion and current `README.md`: the operator repeatedly
   rejected extra rooms when they repeated identity or uncertainty. Depth was
   kept only where it improved orientation.
6. Inbox modlists: Stardew and Terraria mods include many QoL, ambience,
   storage, interface, pet/NPC, and vanilla-plus changes. This supports
   controlled resolution and inhabitation more than raw complexity.
7. Terraria capture:
   `C:/Users/dissonance/Documents/My Games/Terraria/Captures/Capture 2015-07-09 01_40_01.png`
   shows an organized inhabited base: NPC rooms above, tools and displays in
   horizontal bands, collections arranged by type, lighting, storage, and a
   museum-like lower room. The image supports bounded abundance and field
   stewardship more than chaotic accumulation.

## Positive Evidence

- Across wiki, games, music, repo structure, modlists, and the Terraria capture,
  preferred depth tends to be organized into rooms, roles, handles, routes,
  tags, or displays.
- The evidence repeatedly shows return and maintenance: edited pages, curated
  music metadata, modded worlds, refactored folders, and stored/displayed game
  artifacts.
- The operator explicitly corrected the repo away from abstract lifecycle rooms
  (`normalized/`, `derived/`, `staging/`) toward browseable data and output,
  which is a direct rejection of complexity that does not orient.

## Negative Controls

- Complexity alone: large files, many folders, lots of mods, or many edits are
  not enough.
- Difficulty alone: a game being hard does not predict fit.
- Open-world size: large playable spaces without readable pressure or return
  value should not count.
- Hoarding: collections count only when bounded by categories, rooms, labels,
  or future use.
- Lore volume: lore counts only when it improves the living model of the field.

## Blocking Gates

- Count an example only if deeper inspection improves at least one of:
  orientation, prediction, repair, return, agency, or inhabitation.
- Require three independent evidence doors before promoting a claim as global.
- If an example is merely large, complicated, old, or plentiful, block it.

## Falsifier

This claim weakens if future high-fit examples mostly involve:

- opaque complexity with no better handles,
- maximal option fields with no containment,
- procedural vastness with no authored residue,
- difficulty or technical intricacy for its own sake,
- folder depth that repeats names without improving navigation.

## Result

**Resolution-Increasing Depth beats "likes complexity" for this pass.**

The stronger claim explains both attraction and rejection:

- why large archives can be attractive when tagged, linked, or maintained;
- why mods fit when they improve inhabitation or control waste friction;
- why wiki work fits as public structure instead of generic information
  consumption;
- why folder depth was compressed when it repeated uncertainty;
- why the Terraria base reads as evidence: it is abundant, but roomed, lit,
  displayed, inhabited, and operational.

Status: supported candidate, not final canon.

## Update Rule

Keep the claim if new evidence continues to show depth becoming more legible.
Revise it if examples start showing unbounded complexity or opaque sprawl as
high-fit without added orientation.
