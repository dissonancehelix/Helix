# Self Domain

## Role

This domain grounds cognitive style, personality/neurotype context, attention architecture, and social/sensory constraints.

It exists because some operator patterns need an external model before they can be used responsibly: monotropism, autistic traits, schizoid-leaning reserve, sensory gating, social energy, and the difference between taste, trust, and constraint.

## Core Rule

Labels are handles for recurring constraints, not cages or generators.

Best compressions:

> Labels are handles, not cages.

> Monotropism explains the depth-channel; it does not explain the whole map.

> Sociality works best when there is a shared object to stand beside.

> Personality is the surface projection of deeper constraint architecture.

## External Model

- **Autism spectrum** is treated here as a developmental/neurotype framework involving social-communication differences, restricted/repetitive patterns, sensory differences, and variable support needs. This is an orientation frame, not a diagnosis. Anchor: NIMH Autism Spectrum Disorder overview, https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/autism-spectrum-disorder.
- **Monotropism** is an attention-allocation theory in which fewer interests may carry more attentional resource, making depth powerful and context switching costly. Anchor: Murray, Lesser & Lawson, 2005, "Attention, monotropism and the diagnostic criteria for autism", DOI 10.1177/1362361305051398, PMID 15857859, https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15857859/.
- **Big Five** is useful only as a descriptive approximation for trait tendencies. It is not an ontology of the person.
- **MBTI** is folk shorthand only. It may name a vibe or conversational handle; it is not scientific evidence.
- **Schizoid traits** are treated as a low-social-pull / emotional-reserve / privacy frame, not a diagnosis. Anchor: Merck Manual Professional, schizoid personality disorder overview, https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/psychiatric-disorders/personality-disorders/schizoid-personality-disorder-scpd.

Required caveats:

- Do not diagnose.
- Do not treat MBTI as scientific.
- Do not reduce the operator to pathology.
- Do not make monotropism explain everything.
- Do not confuse low social pull with lack of care.
- Do not confuse sensory gating with fragility.
- Do not let personality/neurotype replace the taste engine.

## Operational Model

The operator's profile is best modeled as recurring constraints and gates:

- **Monotropic depth**: high-salience systems become deep channels with strong internal continuity.
- **Attention tunneling**: music archives, Helix, Wikipedia, Trails, and game systems can become enclosed working rooms.
- **Object-mediated sociality**: social connection works best beside a shared object: article, archive, game, track, database, map, or system.
- **Internal simulation before exposure**: the system is modeled privately before public entry or commitment.
- **Context-switching friction**: switching is not impossible, but bad switches shed state and damage continuity.
- **Orientation before exposure**: entry works when the boundary, rules, and affordances are legible first.
- **Signal ownership**: the operator needs control over what signals enter, leave, and represent him.
- **Private edge requirement**: the most effective posture often preserves a backline, indirect angle, or private model.
- **Low social default / high object-depth**: default social pull is low, but object-depth and shared-interior recognition can activate real attachment.
- **Selective conscientiousness**: high care appears when structure matters; low care appears when the task is arbitrary, performative, or unrecoverable.
- **Sensory gating**: light, noise, clutter, touch, and social ambiguity are managed as signal conditions, not moral weakness.

## False Positives

Do not infer:

- every preference is autism
- every solitude preference is schizoid
- every deep interest is pathology
- every sensory preference is threat
- every social reserve means no attachment
- every repeated interest is rigidity
- personality labels explain music/games/aesthetics by themselves

## Evidence Anchors

- **Helix**: externalized cognition as a closed working body with schemas, checks, maps, tools, and rollback paths.
- **Wikipedia**: object-mediated public knowledge work; low-ego contribution through article architecture.
- **foobar/music archive**: controlled signal intake, metadata ownership, sensory gating, and world-listening.
- **Trails database**: long-horizon world-memory and hidden-structure tracking.
- **Steam/game libraries**: attention history, completion/playtime split, and state-transition mastery evidence.
- **Reddit/Twitter evidence surfaces**: object cabinets and live-state tracking, not first-class domains.
- **Old profile rooms**: inhabited social-web space where identity was built from favorites, comments, links, and object arrangement.
- **Sensory gating and signal ownership**: dark rooms, headphones, low-noise interfaces, clean prose, controllable archives.
- **Monotropic project depth**: Helix, music metadata, Wikipedia templates, and Trails ingestion as bounded chambers.
