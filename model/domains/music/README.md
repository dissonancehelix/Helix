# Domain: Music

## Domain Role

Music is a first-class externalized cognition domain because the operator has built and acted through a real archive: foobar, ListenBrainz/Last.fm, VGM metadata, DSP/audio tuning, composer tracking, and return paths into albums, artists, hardware, loops, and scenes.

This domain models music as an inhabited sound-world, not a genre preference list.

## External / Operational Model

Operational surfaces:

- foobar local archive, tags, playback history, loved tracks, radio intake, and custom VGM metadata
- ListenBrainz/Last.fm as long-range world-listening evidence
- VGM/chip/platform/composer metadata as authored sound under hardware constraint
- DSP/plugin/audio-field tuning where software and listening overlap
- future audio/DSP/metadata applications under `system/tools/music_pipeline/` and `system/tools/music_bridge/`

Key musical mechanics:

- **world-listening**: tracks are places and weather systems, not only songs
- **groove / low-end body**: bass/percussion creates a floor that lets mutation move without collapse
- **upper air**: melody, woodwind, vocal, synth, and reverb motion make vertical space
- **timbral weight**: sound texture has body, pressure, and material identity
- **coherent mutation**: change is attractive when it preserves grammar
- **return pressure**: the track or loop creates desire to re-enter
- **loop seams**: re-entry gates where time folds back without losing identity
- **composer fingerprint**: authored continuity across works
- **VGM hardware constraints**: limitation becomes grammar, not deficiency

Trust gate versus taste engine:

Trust controls entry and safety; taste predicts reward after entry. A track can be safe but boring, or strange but deeply rewarding. Do not collapse the two.

## False Positives

- genre label predicts fit by itself
- trust/safety is the same as reward
- hardware-limited sound is primitive rather than grammatical
- repetition is low-information when it creates return pressure
- surreal atmosphere belongs to aesthetics only; when sound-world is the carrier, it belongs here
