# DCP Trajectory Mode: Collapse (EIP)

**Type:** DCP Trajectory Mode
**Status:** Defined — see decision_compression.md
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-25

---

## Overview

The **collapse mode** (historically labeled EIP — Epistemic Irreversibility) describes DCP
trajectories where possibility breadth reaches near-zero and does not recover.

This is a property of DCP compression, not a separate structural law. When a possibility
space collapses, the prior state is unrecoverable from the posterior — this follows
directly from what compression means.

---

## Signature

- Final breadth < 0.01
- No direction reversals after collapse
- k typically high (>10); R² > 0.85

---

## Examples

| Case | k | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Tamam Shud (2022) | 20 | Solved — full collapse after IGG match |
| Fermat's Last Theorem | — | Proof permanently closes the space |
| Cicada 3301 rounds | 15–30 | Designed participant elimination |

---

## Notes

The "irreversibility" label reflects that once a possibility space collapses under DCP,
the information required to reconstruct the prior state is gone. This is a structural
consequence of compression, not an additional claim requiring separate validation.
