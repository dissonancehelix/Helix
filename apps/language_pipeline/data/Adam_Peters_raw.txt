'''Frederick Adam Peters''' (born May 24, 1979) is an American professional [[American football|football]] executive who is the [[General manager (American football)|general manager]] (GM) for the [[Washington Commanders]] of the [[National Football League]] (NFL). His NFL career began as a [[Scout (sport)|scout]] with the [[New England Patriots]] in 2003, spending six seasons with them before joining the [[Denver Broncos]] in 2009.

Peters served as the Broncos' college scouting director for three seasons before joining the [[San Francisco 49ers]] in 2017. He worked closely with general manager [[John Lynch (American football)|John Lynch]], initially as vice president of player personnel and later as assistant general manager being named Commanders GM in 2024. Peters has been a part of three [[Super Bowl]]-winning teams, two with the Patriots and one with the Broncos.

==Early life==
Frederick Adam Peters was born on May 24, 1979, in [[Mountain View, California]], and raised in nearby [[Cupertino]].<ref name="UCLA">{{cite web |title=Player Bio: Adam Peters |url=https://uclabruins.com/sports/2013/4/17/208195218 |website=uclabruins.com |publisher=[[UCLA Bruins]] |access-date=January 9, 2024 |archive-date=January 9, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240109104609/https://uclabruins.com/sports/2013/4/17/208195218 |url-status=live }}</ref> He attended [[Monta Vista High School]], earning all-conference honors in [[American football|football]] as a [[tight end]] and in [[baseball]] as a [[left fielder]] and [[first baseman]]. He enrolled at [[West Valley College]], a junior college in [[Saratoga, California]], in 1997.<ref name="UCLA"/> Peters was a tight end and team captain for their football team, earning two All-[[Coast Conference]] honors. He transferred to the [[University of California, Los Angeles]] (UCLA) in 1999 to play as a reserve [[defensive end]] for the [[UCLA Bruins football|Bruins]].<ref name="UCLA"/><ref name="WestValley">{{cite journal |title=Former Viking Adam Peters heads to Miami with 49ers for Super Bowl LIV |url=https://athletics.westvalley.edu/general/2019-20/releases/20200122pcu7y2 |website=athletics.westvalley.edu |publisher=[[West Valley College]] |access-date=January 8, 2024 |date=January 22, 2020 |archive-date=January 20, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240120144751/https://athletics.westvalley.edu/general/2019-20/releases/20200122pcu7y2 |url-status=live }}</ref> 
Peters decided to stop playing football midway through his time at UCLA, believing that he had no future as a player as teammates like [[DeShaun Foster]] were "bigger, stronger, faster, and better" than him.<ref name="Athletic2018">{{cite web |last1=Lombardi |first1=David |title=Fast-rising Adam Peters, a key John Lynch lieutenant, is right in his element with the 49ers |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/261077/2018/03/05/fast-rising-adam-peters-a-key-john-lynch-lieutenant-is-right-in-his-element-with-the-49ers/ |work=[[The Athletic]] |url-access=subscription |access-date=January 8, 2024 |date=March 5, 2018 |archive-date=January 8, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240108221844/https://theathletic.com/261077/2018/03/05/fast-rising-adam-peters-a-key-john-lynch-lieutenant-is-right-in-his-element-with-the-49ers/ |url-status=live }}</ref> He graduated with a [[psychology]] degree in 2002, working with the Bruins as a [[graduate assistant]] in football operations that season as a way to remain in the sport.<ref name="UCLA"/><ref name="Athletic2018"/>

==Career==
===Early scouting and executive roles===
Peters' career began following a visit by [[New England Patriots]] executives [[Scott Pioli]] and [[Jason Licht]] to UCLA in 2002. Peters, substituting for his boss who was out of town, provided film and other information on the school's [[NFL draft]] prospects.<ref name="Athletic2018"/> Pioli hired him as a [[Scout (sport)|scouting]] assistant for the 2003 [[NFL Combine]] after being impressed by his football acumen, with Peters temporarily moving to live with scout [[Nick Caserio]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Manning |first1=Bryan |title=Former NFL GM Scott Pioli excited for Commanders, Adam Peters |url=https://commanderswire.usatoday.com/2024/01/13/scott-pioli-patriots-belichick-washington-commanders-adam-peters/ |work=Commanders Wire |publisher=[[USA Today]] |access-date=January 15, 2024 |date=January 13, 2024 |archive-date=January 15, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240115065638/https://commanderswire.usatoday.com/2024/01/13/scott-pioli-patriots-belichick-washington-commanders-adam-peters/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="FieldTalk">{{cite web |last1=Lambert |first1=Ivan |title=Commanders GM Adam Peters opens up about his career and why he chose Washington |url=https://commanderswire.usatoday.com/story/sports/nfl/commanders/2025/04/12/adam-peters-washington-commanders-belichick-jayden-daniels/83054157007/ |website=Commanders Wire |publisher=[[USA Today]] |access-date=April 15, 2025 |date=April 12, 2025}}</ref> As an assistant, he put together film of prospects, drove players to doctors and hospitals, and performed [[data entry]] until becoming an area scout in 2005.<ref name="FieldTalk"/>

===Washington Commanders===
[[File:JG9 6770 (54841261163).jpg|thumb|right|Peters during a [[Washington Commanders]] game, 2025]]
Peters was named general manager of the [[Washington Commanders]] on January 15, 2024.<ref name="CommandersHire"/> He hired [[Dan Quinn (American football)|Dan Quinn]] as head coach and overhauled the team's roster, headlined by selecting quarterback [[Jayden Daniels]] second overall in the [[2024 NFL draft]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vacchiano |first1=Ralph |title=Taking Command: How Adam Peters is reshaping football in Washington |url=https://www.foxsports.com/stories/nfl/adam-peters-reshaping-football-in-washington |work=[[FOX Sports]] |access-date=July 20, 2024 |date=May 8, 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Keim |first1=John |title=Why Commanders selected Jayden Daniels No. 2 in NFL draft |url=https://www.espn.com/nfl/story/_/id/40057487/why-commanders-drafted-jayden-daniels-no-2-overall |website=ESPN |access-date=May 3, 2024 |date=May 3, 2024}}</ref> In Peters' [[2024 Washington Commanders season|first season]], the Commanders recorded their highest win total ({{Win-loss record|w=12|l=5}}) and reached their first [[NFC Championship Game]] appearance since [[1991 Washington Redskins season|1991]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Whyno |first1=Stephen |title=Washington Commanders complete a turnaround from 4-13 to making the playoffs behind Jayden Daniels |url=https://apnews.com/article/washington-commanders-20e399be74a98cf1eb204ea4c819a25d2023 |website=APNews.com |publisher=[[Associated Press]] |access-date=January 7, 2025 |date=December 31, 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Standig |first1=Ben |title=GM Adam Peters does things ‘the right way,’ and his Commanders are reaping the benefits |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6045538/2025/01/08/commanders-adam-peters-dan-quinn-playoffs/ |website=[[The Athletic]] |access-date=January 8, 2025 |date=January 8, 2025}}</ref>
