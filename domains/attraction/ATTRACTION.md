## 0. Scope and Ground Rules

This domain describes adult sexual / visual attraction as a taste surface.

It should be candid enough to preserve the real pattern. It should not sanitize attraction into vague “aesthetic appreciation,” because the sexual signal matters. It should also not collapse attraction into a porn-tag list, because the operator's taste is structured, selective, hierarchical, and strongly correction-driven.

Assume all sexual examples refer to adults. If an image or example has ambiguous age, do not use it as sexual evidence.

This domain should preserve:

- what the operator physically finds beautiful

- what makes attraction stronger or weaker
- why certain women / images / categories recur
- how face, body, palette, presentation, and detail channels interact
- how attraction relates to broader taste patterns
- false positives that should not be overpredicted

This domain should not:

- identify people in images

- treat porn categories as the whole mechanism
- flatten taste into ethnicity alone
- overstate fetish language when the operator reports weaker or more specific interest

Best rule:

> **Be explicit about the structure; do not make it crude.**
