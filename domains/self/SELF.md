## 1. What This Domain Is

The Self domain is the constraint layer underneath the taste domains.

It does not decide all preferences directly. It explains why certain kinds of entry, noise, exposure, obligation, ambiguity, attention, and social demand become costly or regulating.

The best model is not:

```text
personality label -> behavior
```

The better model is:

```text
constraint architecture
-> active mode
-> gate state
-> available energy
-> object / field / demand
-> response
```

So the Self domain asks:

- What kind of attention can stay stable?
- What destroys or fragments attention?
- What kinds of sociality are low-cost or high-cost?
- What kinds of sensory input are owned versus invading?
- What kinds of self-knowledge are reliable?
- What requires external evidence rather than introspective guesswork?
- What conditions allow attachment, effort, trust, or reply execution?

Best compression:

> **The Self domain explains the gates that other domains pass through.**

## 2. External Model Anchors

These anchors are orientation tools, not identity cages.

### 2.1 Autism spectrum / Asperger's context

The autism-spectrum frame is useful for:

- social-communication difference
- restricted / repetitive / high-depth interests
- sensory differences
- need for routine or controlled entry
- context-switching friction
- precision over social smoothing
- preference for explicit rules
- difficulty with ambiguous live social fields
- strong object-focused attention
- monotropic depth
- autistic inertia / initiation friction
- uneven skill profile

It is not sufficient to explain:

- the full music taste
- sexual attraction pattern
- specific game preferences
- political cynicism
- metaphysics
- taste in ruins, museums, Thanksgiving, basslines, Wikipedia pages, or Commanders football

Autism explains some channel constraints. It does not generate the whole map.

Best compression:

> **Autism explains signal handling and depth-channel constraints; it does not replace taste.**

### 2.2 Monotropism

Monotropism is especially useful because it describes attention allocation rather than only outward traits.

Local meaning:

```text
few active channels
+ high salience
+ strong internal continuity
+ costly switching
+ chambered depth
= monotropic depth-channel
```

This matches:

- Helix loops
- Wikipedia article maintenance
- music metadata / foobar shaping
- Trails/database/world-memory interest
- games as deep fields
- attraction-profile refinement
- recursive DISSONANCE regeneration
- going in circles because compression/refinement has become self-reinforcing

The benefit:

- intense depth
- strong reconstruction
- rich internal continuity
- ability to build detailed domain chambers
- durable long-horizon attention

The cost:

- hard context switches
- reply friction
- task initiation difficulty
- overfocus on active chamber
- losing global view if every domain is opened at once
- anxiety when too many unfinished doors remain open

Best compression:

> **Monotropism makes chambers powerful and hallway clutter expensive.**

### 2.3 Schizoid personality structure

Schizoid structure is useful as a frame for low default social pull and high internal self-sufficiency.

It maps to:

- solitude preference
- low reward from unstructured social interaction
- emotional reserve
- discomfort with forced intimacy rituals
- privacy preference
- inwardness
- reduced need for conventional social maintenance
- object-mediated bonding over broad socializing

It does **not** mean:

- no care
- no attraction
- no loyalty
- no emotion
- no attachment
- no desire for connection under the right conditions
- inability to love
- literal indifference to suffering

Best compression:

> **Schizoid structure lowers social pull; it does not erase interiority or care.**

### 2.4 Introversion

Introversion alone is too blunt.

The sharper local model:

```text
low default social pull
+ high private cognition reward
+ high object-field attachment
+ high social switching cost
+ strong threshold ownership need
= extreme introversion as field-management strategy
```

Solitude is not merely avoidance. It is where attention stays coherent.

Best compression:

> **Solitude is the controlled field where cognition can stay coherent.**

### 2.5 Big Five / MBTI

Big Five can be useful as low-resolution coordinates:

- very high openness
- low extraversion
- selective/bimodal conscientiousness
- variable agreeableness depending on structural respect
- externally low but internally variable neuroticism

MBTI can be used as folk shorthand only. It may name a vibe. It is not evidence.

Best compression:

> **Trait labels are map coordinates, not terrain.**

## 4. Attention Architecture

### 4.1 Depth-channel attention

When a field becomes active, attention tends to deepen rather than skim.

High-depth fields include:

- Helix architecture
- DISSONANCE profile refinement
- Wikipedia / Wikimedia
- music metadata / VGM / DSP
- game systems and preference analysis
- Trails / JRPG continuity
- attraction-domain modeling
- consciousness / inhabited interiority theory
- Spanish chunking and grammar routing

This can look like obsession from the outside. Internally it is more like entering a chamber where the structure becomes manipulable.

Best compression:

> **Depth is not stuckness when the field keeps increasing resolution.**

### 4.2 Context-switching friction

Switching away from an active chamber is costly because state is lost.

The cost is not only time. It is:

- losing the active model
- reopening a cold field
- rebuilding context
- reloading constraints
- translating intent back into structure
- anxiety from incomplete open loops

This explains why multi-domain self-mapping can become circular. Every domain feeds the global map, but opening all domains at once makes the map foggy.

Best compression:

> **Too many open chambers create hallway fog.**

### 4.3 Chambered architecture as accommodation

Domain files are not just repository organization. They are attention ergonomics.

They allow:

```text
one chamber active
local detail preserved
main map protected
cross-domain feedback gated
```

Best compression:

> **Chambers protect depth from becoming sprawl.**

### 4.4 Compression-Native Cognition

Compression-native cognition is not merely liking summaries or concise language.

It is the tendency to move through a field by repeatedly asking:

```text
what is the smallest structure that preserves the living operation?
```

The operator often starts with a local question, follows the pressure until it reveals a larger operating pattern, then compresses the result into a handle that can be reused elsewhere. This is why a chat can begin with a narrow historical-language question and end in ontology, Helix, evidence architecture, or self-modeling. The pathway is not random topic drift. It is compression through successive substrates.

Typical route:

```text
local anomaly
→ lineage / source history
→ substrate behavior
→ general pattern
→ domain fit
→ global law candidate
→ anti-overpromotion firewall
```

This is a strength when the compression preserves doors. It becomes risky when the compression becomes too beautiful too quickly and starts acting like proof before the evidence has caught up.

Passes:

- extracting a domain law from many examples
- turning corrections into boundary updates
- reducing a folder map without losing navigability
- compressing a chat lineage into reviewable files
- finding the same operation across music, language, software, aesthetics, and self

Fails:

- treating a beautiful mapping as causality
- overpromoting one session into universal law
- polishing a profile until the examples disappear
- mistaking metaphorical resonance for evidence
- explaining direct attachment until the attachment feels less real

Best compression:

> **Compression-native cognition finds the reusable operation inside local evidence.**

Second compression:

> **The danger is not compression; the danger is beautiful overpromotion.**

### 4.5 Fractal Self-Similarity As Recognition Pattern

A recurring recognition response appears when the same operation shows up at multiple scales.

The operator strongly responds to things that are:

- layered
- connected
- modular
- self-similar
- recursive without becoming mush
- compressed at the top and decompressible below
- able to preserve identity across scale shifts

This is not just an aesthetic preference. It is also a self-recognition pattern. A system feels beautiful when its internal organization resembles the operator's own preferred cognition:

```text
spine
→ rooms
→ doors
→ evidence
→ return paths
→ local variation under global grammar
```

This is why the chambered-domain architecture feels calming rather than merely tidy. It mirrors the attention architecture: one active room at a time, local expansion protected, global spine preserved.

Best compression:

> **The system feels beautiful when it behaves like the mind already wanted to behave.**

False positive:

> Treating fractal/self-similar beauty as mystical proof. It is a recognition signal, not causality by itself.


## 5. Social Architecture

### 5.1 Object-mediated sociality

Sociality works best when there is a shared object to stand beside.

High-pass social objects:

- game
- song
- wiki article
- theory
- artifact
- project
- archive
- map
- tool
- domain
- problem
- message with actual content
- shared media object

Low-pass social forms:

- small talk
- getting-to-know-you with no object
- live emotional performance
- phone calls
- group social ambiguity
- friendliness as obligation
- unstructured check-ins
- conversation with no route

Best compression:

> **Give the interaction an object and the social field becomes less hostile.**

### 5.2 Response debt / ghosting / reciprocal demand anxiety

This is a major explicit pattern.

The operator may leave DMs/texts unread for months. This is not best modeled as lack of care. The mechanism is more specific:

```text
message arrives
-> response requires social-state construction
-> reply opens reciprocal loop
-> other person will probably reply
-> future demand becomes anticipated
-> anxiety/effort accumulates
-> unread state freezes
```

The cost is not one message. The cost is consenting to the next loop.

This is especially likely when:

- the conversation has no shared object
- reply expectations are ambiguous
- the other person may respond quickly
- there is guilt from delay
- the field has gone cold
- the response now requires apology + explanation + content
- the operator cannot tell whether reopening the door will create more debt

Important distinction:

```text
care exists
reply execution fails
```

Best compression:

> **Unread messages become frozen doors.**

Second compression:

> **The hard part is not one reply; it is consenting to the next loop.**

### 5.3 Text vs calls

Text is preferred because it allows:

- delay
- rereading
- compression
- editing
- controlled pacing
- lower exposure
- fewer live performance demands

Calls fail because they impose:

- real-time demand
- unpredictable duration
- social performance
- immediate vocal availability
- rhythm controlled by someone else
- no clean preview of what will be required

Best compression:

> **Text gives pacing control; calls seize the clock.**

### 5.4 Low social pull is not low attachment

The operator can attach strongly when the field is real.

Attachment tends to require:

- coherent interiority
- low-performance presence
- trust through repeated consistency
- specificity
- intelligence / awareness
- shared object or shared depth
- space for silence and distance
- continuity without constant maintenance

Fails:

- social performance
- forced emotional display
- loudness
- overtalking
- generic friendliness
- attention-seeking
- charm without interior

Best compression:

> **Attachment is rare because entry is gated, not because attachment is absent.**

## 6. Sensory Architecture

### 6.1 Signal ownership

The main distinction is not low tolerance versus high tolerance.

It is:

```text
chosen signal = regulating
unchosen impact = invading
```

Passes:

- loud music when chosen
- headphones
- dark room
- controlled screen light
- cool air
- night driving
- bass pressure
- massage pressure
- DOMS localization
- controlled intensity in games

Fails:

- random banging
- fluorescent glare
- heat
- public noise
- crowded chaos
- unexpected calls
- sudden movement near body
- heights / exposed fall-space
- unowned social attention

Best compression:

> **Intensity is not the problem. Unowned intensity is.**

### 6.2 Darkness and controlled light

Darkness is not just aesthetic. It is sensory compression.

It reduces:

- visual clutter
- exposure feeling
- glare
- institutional brightness
- ambient noise translated into visual threat
- sense of being seen

Preferred:

- dark rooms
- night
- overcast/rain
- controlled monitor glow
- muted warm palettes
- enclosed spaces

Rejected:

- fluorescent lighting
- glare
- white/gray/beige sterile spaces
- bright heat exposure
- showroom death

Best compression:

> **Darkness is a boundary that makes the room inhabitable.**

### 6.3 Heat and cool air

Heat is hostile because it increases body noise:

- sweating
- itching
- irritability
- exposure feeling
- loss of control
- sensory amplification

Cool air helps because it sharpens and stabilizes the body-state.

Best compression:

> **Cool air is signal clarity; heat is body-noise.**

### 6.4 Motion and body thinking

The body is a thinking instrument.

Common regulation / thinking channels:

- pacing
- rocking
- leg bouncing
- stretching
- posture shifts
- driving
- muscle soreness localization
- massage gun pressure
- controlled loud audio

Best compression:

> **Cognition is spatial, rhythmic, sensory, and muscular before it becomes prose.**

## 7. Threat Architecture

Threat appears when a structure enters the field before it can be modeled.

General formula:

```text
unassessed structure
+ unstable transformation
+ insufficient prediction window
+ forced exposure
= threat
```

### 7.1 Random banging / acoustic invasion

Random banging is especially aversive because it is:

- abrupt
- unchosen
- physically invasive
- ambiguous in source
- impossible to ignore
- not semantically useful
- not under operator control

Controlled loud music can regulate. Random banging violates.

Compression:

> **Chosen sound is signal; random impact is invasion.**

### 7.2 Heights / spatial exposure

Heights create non-inhabitable depth:

- exposed vertical void
- catastrophic consequence from small movement
- loss of enclosure
- body placed next to irreversible transformation
- gravity becomes active threat

Compression:

> **Heights are depth without inhabitable boundary.**

### 7.3 Fast unpredictable motion

Jumping bugs, bats, erratic small moving things, and sudden near-body motion fail because:

- trajectory is unstable
- landing point unclear
- prediction window too short
- object can enter body-field before classification

Compression:

> **The object moves before the model owns the space.**

### 7.4 Social volatility

Unassessed strangers and chaotic social spaces are threatening because the operator has not yet modeled:

- impulse control
- boundary respect
- awareness level
- aggression potential
- stability
- rules of engagement

Compression:

> **Stranger threat is unclassified agency.**

## 13. Agent Use Rules

When using this domain, agents should:

- separate label from mechanism
- ask what gate is active
- preserve distinctions between trust, taste, threat, effort, and attachment
- treat ghosting as response-loop friction unless evidence says otherwise
- avoid flattery
- preserve specificity
- avoid turning neurotype into destiny
- route social suggestions through shared objects when possible
- reduce translation burden
- keep changes inspectable
- ask whether a recommendation gives a handle
- remember that low social pull does not mean no care

Agents should not:

- moralize solitude
- pathologize every preference
- prescribe journaling/tests unless requested
- assume phone calls are fine
- treat social maintenance as trivial
- frame boundaries as weakness
- call every sensitivity anxiety
- flatten the profile into “autistic introvert likes systems”

Best compression:

> **Use the labels to preserve the human, not to shrink him.**

## 17. Detailed Gate Inventory

This section exists so future agents do not collapse the Self domain into a vague “introverted autistic systems thinker” sticker. That sticker is technically related and practically useless, like putting a map legend on fire.

### 17.1 Entry Gate

Entry is easiest when:

- the field is previewable
- the rules are visible
- exits are visible
- interaction can be paced
- the operator can observe before participating
- exposure is not immediate
- the object is known before the people
- the task has a name and boundary

Entry is hardest when:

- the operator is made visible first
- the rules are hidden
- people expect live performance
- a conversation begins without an object
- sensory load is uncontrolled
- the field contains social ambiguity with consequences

Compression:

> **Orientation before exposure.**

### 17.2 Trust Gate

Trust is granted when a person/system/tool can change without betraying its thread.

Passes:

- consistency under pressure
- visible motives
- inspectable history
- ability to admit error
- rollback or repair path
- stable relation between declared surface and actual behavior

Fails:

- performative care
- hidden motive
- contradiction concealed
- emotional pressure used as control
- fake transparency
- “trust me” without structure

Compression:

> **Trust is permission for transformation.**

### 17.3 Taste Gate

Taste is not the same as trust.

Something can be safe and dead.

Taste needs:

- motion
- texture
- pressure
- return
- compatible signal
- internal specificity
- enough weirdness to remain alive
- enough lawfulness to remain inhabitable

Compression:

> **Trust says enter; taste says stay.**

### 17.4 Threat Gate

Threat appears when unmodeled transformation crosses the boundary without permission.

Threat does not require actual danger. It requires enough uncertainty, exposure, and lack of control to make the field hostile.

Compression:

> **Threat is forced exposure to untrusted structure.**

### 17.5 Effort Gate

Effort requires structural payoff.

The operator can work hard when:

- the output persists
- the boundary gets sharper
- future friction goes down
- the task enters a larger architecture
- the work is visible as improvement
- the field remembers the action

Effort collapses when:

- the task is pure obligation
- there is no re-entry value
- the surface is arbitrary
- the result disappears
- the reward is only external approval
- the labor is messy, noisy, or socially performative

Compression:

> **Work becomes available when the action changes a real field.**

### 17.6 Reply Gate

Replying requires more than words.

It requires:

- re-entering the social field
- reconstructing tone
- deciding how much to explain
- handling delay guilt
- anticipating the next response
- accepting the reopened loop

Replying is easiest when:

- the message is object-centered
- the reply can be short without seeming hostile
- the other person will not immediately demand more
- the topic has structure
- the conversation has a clean endpoint

Replying is hardest when:

- the relationship context is ambiguous
- the message is purely social maintenance
- the delay is already long
- the reply requires apology + explanation + new content
- the other person will probably answer quickly

Compression:

> **A reply is a door, not a sentence.**

## 19. Self-Modeling Strategy

The operator does not rely on pure introspection as the main evidence source.

Preferred self-understanding route:

```text
behavior / archive / dataset / artifact
-> pattern extraction
-> domain interpretation
-> global map correction
-> future prediction
```

This explains why the following are useful:

- Wikipedia contribution history
- YouTube comments/subscriptions
- Reddit/Twitter data
- music datasets
- Steam/completion lists
- modlists
- old bookmarks
- old profile versions
- chat logs
- domain files
- generated reports
- repeated corrections in conversation

The self becomes clearer when external traces can be read back.

This does not mean the operator lacks a self. It means the self is better modeled through evidence than through vibes.

Compression:

> **The archive is a mirror with timestamps.**

### 19.1 Archive advantage

Archives prevent:

- confabulation
- over-smoothing
- flattering self-story
- forgetting anomalies
- compressing away examples
- mistaking current mood for stable pattern

Archives allow:

- seeing developmental continuity
- detecting cringe as surface weather
- preserving old signal beneath old style
- comparing old and new models
- tracking what changed and what did not

Compression:

> **Old evidence keeps the current map honest.**

---

### 19.2 Session harvests as self-evidence

Chat sessions with LLMs are not only outputs. They show the operator's cognition in motion:

- what gets corrected
- what feels flattened
- what reduces anxiety
- what counts as done
- what gets rejected as too generic
- how examples move into domains
- how local evidence becomes a global candidate
- how direct attachment resists over-theory

A good session harvest should preserve what the operator said, what the agent inferred, what the agent misunderstood, what files were created, what corrections mattered, and what should be reviewed later.

Best compression:

> **The prompts show the operating system while it is running.**

### 19.3 Placeholder non-attachment vs load-bearing protection

The operator can discard placeholder structures quickly when they are not load-bearing. Temporary names, draft folder shapes, provisional labels, and working scaffolds can be replaced without much emotional resistance.

But once a structure becomes load-bearing — once it preserves a real field, reduces anxiety, enables return, or protects evidence — it becomes worth defending.

This distinction prevents a false inference:

```text
discards placeholders quickly -> does not care about structure
```

Better inference:

```text
placeholder structure is disposable
load-bearing structure is protected
```

Best compression:

> **Placeholder structures can be discarded instantly; load-bearing structures get protected.**

## 20. Relationship Submodel

This belongs in Self because relationships are not just Attraction. Attraction opens one channel; attachment requires trust, pacing, and coherent interiority.

### 20.1 Entry

Relationship entry needs:

- low-pressure communication
- visible specificity
- self-awareness
- warmth
- intelligence
- room for silence
- text-first pacing
- no forced performance
- emotional reality without melodrama

### 20.2 Maintenance

Maintenance is difficult when it becomes pure frequency obligation.

A relationship is easier to maintain when:

- connection survives gaps
- there are shared objects
- neither person treats silence as automatic betrayal
- directness is allowed
- reassurance confirms continuity rather than demanding performance
- communication does not become a treadmill

### 20.3 Reassurance

Useful reassurance says:

- the bond still exists
- the rules have not secretly changed
- distance has not erased continuity
- the other person remains internally present
- uncertainty can be discussed without punishment

Bad reassurance:

- generic affection with no behavior
- pressure for more performance
- emotional coercion
- “everything is fine” while behavior contradicts it

Compression:

> **Attachment needs continuity more than constant contact.**

## 22. Recommended Agent Defaults

When helping the operator, default to:

- object-first framing
- low-noise structure
- concrete next action
- explicit boundaries
- enough detail to preserve re-entry
- no false cheerleading
- no generic productivity advice
- no phone-call-as-default suggestions
- no “just journal every day” sludge unless asked
- preserve examples
- reduce translation burden
- show what changed
- keep raw evidence separate from active canon
- build files when useful instead of only discussing them

Bad defaults:

- vague encouragement
- over-abstract synthesis with no artifacts
- asking unnecessary clarifying questions when evidence already exists
- smoothing contradictions
- treating domains as interchangeable
- making the map pretty but less predictive

Compression:

> **Give the mind a handle, not a motivational poster.**
