```text
players
+ collision
+ ball attachment
+ tackle displacement
+ auto-possession transfer
```

## 3. Core Game Mechanics

### 3.1 Possession As Curse

Possession is not a stable reward.

In EFT, the ball attaches to a player and immediately turns them into the field's primary target.

This creates the core emotional inversion:

```text
I have the ball
→ I am powerful
→ I am slower
→ everyone is coming
→ I must pass, dodge, collide, or die
```

Best compression:

> **Possession paints a target on the body.**

### 3.2 Auto-Pickup / Forced State Change

The ball attaches instantly to anyone who touches it.

This means possession can happen by accident. New players can suddenly become the carrier because they ran through the wrong space at the wrong time.

This is not bad design. It is core to the sport.

It creates:

- sudden reversals
- involuntary responsibility
- immediate retargeting
- "oh shit I have it" moments
- compressed state transitions
- constant field reclassification

Best compression:

> **Auto-pickup makes possession a state transition, not a menu choice.**

### 3.3 Carrier Slowness / Defender Speed

Carrier slowdown creates asymmetry.

The carrier gains scoring possibility but loses mobility. Defenders gain chase power but must still time contact.

This makes possession inherently unstable.

Best compression:

> **The carrier has the future but loses the legs to keep it.**

### 3.4 Tackles As First-Class Outcomes

Tackling is not merely combat flavor.

A tackle can:

- strip the ball
- stop a goal
- remove a player from the next interaction cycle
- change upright participation count
- disqualify an opponent from immediate contest
- create space by reducing nearby agency

Best compression:

> **A tackle matters because it deletes an opponent from the next second of possibility.**

### 3.5 Passing As Exposure

Passing is high-risk because the carrier must pause/wind up and becomes exposed.

Good passing requires timing, breathing room, and field-state awareness. Bad passing is a self-imposed freeze in a collision sport.

Best compression:

> **A pass is a bet that the field will not collapse before the throw leaves.**

### 3.6 Upright Participation

The real resource is not only territory.

It is:

```text
how many upright, charging bodies can affect the next possession change?
```

Knockdowns, hazards, voids, and displacement matter because they remove players from the next micro-cycle.

Best compression:

> **The live resource is upright agency near the next state change.**

## 4. Central Skill Model

A good EFT player is not simply the one who controls the ball longest.

A good EFT player is the one who is positioned correctly at the highest number of state transitions.

This includes:

- reading where the ball will be after collision
- anticipating collapse points
- choosing when to charge carrier vs next receiver
- using hazards and voids
- denying immediate futures
- arriving where the scrum will become vulnerable
- knowing when possession is bad and interruption is better
- treating the ball as state pressure, not treasure

Best compression:

> **The best player lives at the next state change.**

Second compression:

> **New players chase the carrier; good players anticipate the collapse.**

## 5. Mapmaking / Field Design

EFT mapmaking is not decorative environment design.

A good EFT map must create:

- collision routes
- readable pressure lanes
- hazard meaning
- contested goals
- recovery paths
- vertical/edge risk
- scrum zones
- flanking routes
- enough openness for chase
- enough structure for denial
- enough danger for state transitions to matter

Bad EFT map design:

- too clean
- too wide and empty
- too narrow without alternatives
- too much safety
- too much visual noise
- hazards that are random instead of readable
- goals that reduce contest
- geometry that kills reversals
- polish that lowers friction in the wrong place

Best compression:

> **An EFT map is good when it makes the next collision legible before it becomes inevitable.**

### 5.1 Cleanliness Firewall

A change is wrong if it makes the game cleaner while reducing tension, contested interaction, or reversal density.

This is one of EFT's strongest design laws.

Best compression:

> **Clean is not good if clean kills the sport.**
