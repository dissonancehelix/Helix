# Truth Layer

The Truth Layer governs how Helix reasons about discovery claims. It defines four binding rules that prevent false promotion through self-confirmation, aesthetic inertia, or coordinate-to-ontology confusion.

---

## 1. Resolution Priority

Discovery resolution is the primary objective. Elegance, minimality, and symmetry are secondary and must never suppress measurable resolution gain.

**Resolution Gain** is defined as:
- Decrease in false twin collisions (distinct domains mapped to identical coordinates)
- Decrease in persistent isolates (isolated domains integrated into meaningful clusters)
- Increase in feasible transfer precision
- Improvement in predictive accuracy

If Resolution Gain > 0 and the candidate has survived structural hostility tests, ontology expansion is **permitted and encouraged**. Minimality claims must yield to resolution evidence.

---

## 2. Self-Confirmation Block

Agreement between models is not evidence.

If two mathematical models, projections, or algorithms agree, AND adversarial (structural hostility) tests have not been run, then:
- Confidence score is strictly **zero**
- The agreement is deemed coincidental or an encoding artifact

Only **invariance under hostility** increases the probability of structural truth. This rule applies to all automated manifold discovery and transfer feasibility calculations.

---

## 3. Structural Hostility Requirement

Every major claim (Rank, Manifold Stability, New Base, Feasible Transfer) must survive the following adversarial suite before promotion:

- **Scaling Invariance**: Survival across raw data, `StandardScaler`, `RobustScaler`, and whitening
- **Encoding Dominance Test**: Detect if categorical token frequency dominates the projection
- **Projection Family Rotation**: Consistency across SVD, PCA, Kernel PCA, and Diffusion Maps
- **Feature Permutation Null**: Claim must be statistically significant against a random feature-shuffled model
- **Domain Dropout**: Stability under random removal of up to 20% of the dataset
- **Basis Ablation**: Effect of removing the base on overall coordinate resolution and transfer precision
- **Leakage Audit**: Non-derivative status relative to Ring 0 and Ring 1 metadata (R² < 0.10)
- **Transfer Feasibility Overlay**: Manual or mechanism-aware verification of geometric neighbors

No claim can be promoted to Ring 1 or higher without a corresponding **Hostility Survival Report**.

---

## 4. Coordinate vs. Ontology Separation

Coordinate collapse is not ontological reduction.

- A low-rank manifold (e.g., k₉₀ = 1) is a coordinate convenience, not a justification for reducing ontological bases
- A domain's isolation in a manifold projection does not imply a need for new bases
- Ontology mutation requires cross-family invariance (stability under hostility) **and** transfer resolution justification

Ontology informs coordinates. Coordinate artifacts (scaling-dependent clusters, projection coincidences) never inform ontology.

---

## 5. Ontology Provisionality

No ontological component is permanent.

- No base (Ring 0 or Ring 1) is protected by historical inertia or aesthetic preference
- Any base must be split, merged, or replaced if doing so is proven to increase discovery resolution
- Temporal stability increases the probability of accuracy but never grants immunity from future falsification

Promotion of any candidate base requires: measurable resolution gain, invariance survival, leakage rejection, and ablation elasticity preservation.
