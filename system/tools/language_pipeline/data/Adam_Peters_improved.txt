'''Frederick Adam Peters''' (born May 24, 1979) is an American [[American football|football]] executive who is the [[General manager (American football)|general manager]] of the [[Washington Commanders]] of the [[National Football League]] (NFL). He began his scouting career with the [[New England Patriots]] in 2003, winning two [[Super Bowl]]s before joining the [[Denver Broncos]] in 2009. Peters managed college scouting for the Broncos for three seasons before joining the [[San Francisco 49ers]] in 2017. He held roles as the vice president of player personnel and assistant general manager for the 49ers until his appointment as Commanders general manager in 2024. Peters has won three Super Bowls as an executive.

==Early life==
Frederick Adam Peters was born on May 24, 1979, in [[Mountain View, California]], and raised in [[Cupertino]].<ref name="UCLA">{{cite web |title=Player Bio: Adam Peters |url=https://uclabruins.com/sports/2013/4/17/208195218 |website=uclabruins.com |publisher=[[UCLA Bruins]] |access-date=January 9, 2024 |archive-date=January 9, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240109104609/https://uclabruins.com/sports/2013/4/17/208195218 |url-status=live }}</ref> At [[Monta Vista High School]], he earned all-conference honors in football as a [[tight end]] and baseball as a [[left fielder]] and [[first baseman]]. Peters attended [[West Valley College]] in 1997, where he played tight end and served as team captain, earning two All-[[Coast Conference]] honors.<ref name="UCLA"/> He transferred to the [[University of California, Los Angeles]] (UCLA) in 1999, playing as a reserve [[defensive end]] for the [[UCLA Bruins football|Bruins]].<ref name="UCLA"/><ref name="WestValley">{{cite journal |title=Former Viking Adam Peters heads to Miami with 49ers for Super Bowl LIV |url=https://athletics.westvalley.edu/general/2019-20/releases/20200122pcu7y2 |website=athletics.westvalley.edu |publisher=[[West Valley College]] |access-date=January 8, 2024 |date=January 22, 2020 |archive-date=January 20, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240120144751/https://athletics.westvalley.edu/general/2019-20/releases/20200122pcu7y2 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Peters stopped playing football during his tenure at UCLA, concluding his teammates were "bigger, stronger, faster, and better" than him.<ref name="Athletic2018">{{cite web |last1=Lombardi |first1=David |title=Fast-rising Adam Peters, a key John Lynch lieutenant, is right in his element with the 49ers |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/261077/2018/03/05/fast-rising-adam-peters-a-key-john-lynch-lieutenant-is-right-in-his-element-with-the-49ers/ |work=[[The Athletic]] |url-access=subscription |access-date=January 8, 2024 |date=March 5, 2018 |archive-date=January 8, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240108221844/https://theathletic.com/261077/2018/03/05/fast-rising-adam-peters-a-key-john-lynch-lieutenant-is-right-in-his-element-with-the-49ers/ |url-status=live }}</ref> He graduated with a [[psychology]] degree in 2002 and worked with the Bruins as a [[graduate assistant]] in football operations to remain in the sport.<ref name="UCLA"/><ref name="Athletic2018"/>

==Career==
===Early scouting and executive roles===
Peters' scouting career began after New England Patriots executives [[Scott Pioli]] and [[Jason Licht]] visited UCLA in 2002. Substituting for his absent supervisor, Peters provided film and evaluations on the school's [[NFL draft]] prospects.<ref name="Athletic2018"/> Impressed by his acumen, Pioli hired him as a scouting assistant for the 2003 [[NFL Combine]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Manning |first1=Bryan |title=Former NFL GM Scott Pioli excited for Commanders, Adam Peters |url=https://commanderswire.usatoday.com/2024/01/13/scott-pioli-patriots-belichick-washington-commanders-adam-peters/ |work=Commanders Wire |publisher=[[USA Today]] |access-date=January 15, 2024 |date=January 13, 2024 |archive-date=January 15, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240115065638/https://commanderswire.usatoday.com/2024/01/13/scott-pioli-patriots-belichick-washington-commanders-adam-peters/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="FieldTalk">{{cite web |last1=Lambert |first1=Ivan |title=Commanders GM Adam Peters opens up about his career and why he chose Washington |url=https://commanderswire.usatoday.com/story/sports/nfl/commanders/2025/04/12/adam-peters-washington-commanders-belichick-jayden-daniels/83054157007/ |website=Commanders Wire |publisher=[[USA Today]] |access-date=April 15, 2025 |date=April 12, 2025}}</ref> His initial duties included compiling prospect film, escorting players to medical appointments, and performing data entry before he earned a promotion to area scout in 2005.<ref name="FieldTalk"/>

The Patriots reached four [[AFC Championship Game]]s and won Super Bowls [[Super Bowl XXXVIII|XXXVIII]] and [[Super Bowl XXXIX|XXXIX]] during his tenure. Peters joined the [[Denver Broncos]] in January 2009, seeking greater advancement opportunities than those available in New England's crowded personnel department.<ref name="FieldTalk"/><ref>{{cite web |last1=Reiss |first1=Mike |title=Eye on the process |url=https://www.boston.com/sports/extra-points/2006/04/28/eye_on_the_proc_1/ |website=[[Boston.com]] |access-date=January 14, 2024 |date=April 28, 2006 |archive-date=January 14, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240114120450/https://www.boston.com/sports/extra-points/2006/04/28/eye_on_the_proc_1/ |url-status=live }}</ref> He worked as a regional and national scout for the Broncos before his promotion to assistant director of college scouting in July 2014.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Klis |first1=Mike |title=Prominent Bronco players may start training camp on physically unable to perform list |url=https://www.denverpost.com/2014/07/20/prominent-bronco-players-may-start-training-camp-on-physically-unable-to-perform-list/ |work=[[The Denver Post]] |access-date=January 8, 2024 |date=July 20, 2014 |archive-date=January 8, 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240108215213/https://www.denverpost.com/2014/07/20/prominent-bronco-players-may-start-training-camp-on-physically-unable-to-perform-list/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Promoted to director of college scouting in 2016, Peters earned his third Super Bowl ring following Denver's victory in [[Super Bowl 50]].

===Washington Commanders===
[[File:JG9 6770 (54841261163).jpg|thumb|right|Peters during a [[Washington Commanders]] game, 2025]]
Peters was named general manager of the [[Washington Commanders]] on January 15, 2024.<ref name="CommandersHire"/> He hired [[Dan Quinn (American football)|Dan Quinn]] as head coach and overhauled the team's roster, notably selecting quarterback [[Jayden Daniels]] second overall in the [[2024 NFL draft]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vacchiano |first1=Ralph |title=Taking Command: How Adam Peters is reshaping football in Washington |url=https://www.foxsports.com/stories/nfl/adam-peters-reshaping-football-in-washington |work=[[FOX Sports]] |access-date=July 20, 2024 |date=May 8, 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Keim |first1=John |title=Why Commanders selected Jayden Daniels No. 2 in NFL draft |url=https://www.espn.com/nfl/story/_/id/40057487/why-commanders-drafted-jayden-daniels-no-2-overall |website=ESPN |access-date=May 3, 2024 |date=May 3, 2024}}</ref> In his [[2024 Washington Commanders season|first season]], the Commanders recorded a {{Win-loss record|w=12|l=5}} record and reached the [[NFC Championship Game]] for the first time since [[1991 Washington Redskins season|1991]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Whyno |first1=Stephen |title=Washington Commanders complete a turnaround from 4-13 to making the playoffs behind Jayden Daniels |url=https://apnews.com/article/washington-commanders-20e399be74a98cf1eb204ea4c819a25d2023 |website=APNews.com |publisher=[[Associated Press]] |access-date=January 7, 2025 |date=December 31, 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Standig |first1=Ben |title=GM Adam Peters does things ‘the right way,’ and his Commanders are reaping the benefits |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6045538/2025/01/08/commanders-adam-peters-dan-quinn-playoffs/ |website=[[The Athletic]] |access-date=January 8, 2025 |date=January 8, 2025}}</ref>
