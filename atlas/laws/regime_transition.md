# Law: Regime Transition

**Confidence:** Exploratory
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-15
**Probe Version:** not yet implemented

---

## Description

Complex systems do not change behavior gradually across all parameters —
they exhibit sharp transitions between qualitatively distinct behavioral
regimes. At transition boundaries, the system's response to perturbation
changes character, not just magnitude.

This pattern is a candidate universal law: the existence of regime
structure (rather than continuous behavioral gradients) may be a
fundamental property of sufficiently complex adaptive systems.

---

## Observed Conditions

- Not yet empirically measured
- Candidate substrates: dynamical systems, evolutionary systems,
  network topology transitions, language register shifts
- Theoretical basis: phase transitions in statistical physics;
  bifurcation theory in dynamical systems

---

## Failure Conditions

- Unknown — probe not yet implemented
